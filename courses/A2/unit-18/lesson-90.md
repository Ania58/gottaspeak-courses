## Idioms (6 min)
- under the weather (not well)  
- fit as a fiddle (very healthy)  
- in good shape (healthy condition)  
- out of shape (not healthy)  
- recharge your batteries (rest and get energy)  
- at death’s door (very ill)

## Skills – Reading (6 min)
### **Text: “Finding Balance for a Healthy Life”**

Many people want to live a healthier life, but it isn’t always easy. Over the past year I have tried different habits to improve my body and mind. First, I started walking every morning. At the beginning I was completely *out of shape*, but after a few weeks I felt much better and now I am finally *in good shape*. Fresh air and movement have given me more *energy*, and I don’t feel *under the weather* as often as before.

Another change is that I take short breaks during the day. When work becomes stressful, I stop for ten minutes to *recharge my batteries*. I drink water, stretch, or simply close my eyes. These small moments help my mind relax. I’ve noticed that when I am calm, I make better decisions and stay healthier.

I also try to listen to my body. If I feel tired, I rest earlier. If I feel sick, I take it seriously. A few months ago I caught a bad virus and almost felt *at death’s door*, but because I reacted quickly and rested enough, I recovered fast. Now I understand that real health is not only about the body, but also about balance and daily habits.

3. Which habits from the text could help you stay healthy?
