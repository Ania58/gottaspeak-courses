# Lesson 45 – Media Influence and Responsibility

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- evaluate the role of media in society  
- express opinions about responsibility  
- justify decisions and viewpoints  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. Do media have too much power?
2. Who is responsible for misinformation?
3. Should media be controlled?
4. Can people protect themselves from media influence?

---

## 2. Real Situation – Media Power

Read the situation:

👉 A news story spreads quickly online.  
Later, it turns out to be false, but many people still believe it.

---

### Task

Answer:

1. Who is responsible for the spread of this information?  
2. What could have been done differently?  
3. Who is affected by this situation?  

---

## 3. Decision Task – Responsibility

For each situation, decide:

👉 Who is responsible?

1. A misleading headline attracts millions of views.  
2. An influencer shares false information.  
3. A reader shares an article without checking it.  
4. A platform promotes trending content.  

---

### Follow-up

👉 Rank responsibility from:
most responsible → least responsible  

Explain your reasoning  

---

## 4. Language Focus – Responsibility

We express responsibility using:

- People should check information.  
- Media ought to be more careful.  
- There needs to be more control.  
- It is important to question sources.  

---

### Task

React to the situations using responsibility language.

1. People share information too quickly.  
2. Media use emotional headlines.  
3. Platforms promote viral content.  
4. Users don’t verify information.  

---

## 5. Ethical Thinking

Read the statement:

👉 “Media should prioritise engagement over accuracy.”

---

### Task

1. Do you agree or disagree?  
2. What are the consequences of this approach?  
3. What would be a better alternative?  

---

## 6. Real-world Scenario

Situation:

👉 You run a news platform.  
You must choose:

- publish fast but risk inaccuracy  
- publish slowly but ensure accuracy  

---

### Task

1. What do you choose?  
2. What are the consequences?  
3. How do you justify your decision?  

---

## 7. Critical Thinking – Influence

Read:

👉 “People are responsible for what they believe, not media.”

---

### Task

1. Do you agree?  
2. What arguments support this idea?  
3. What arguments go against it?  

---

## 8. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. How powerful is media influence today?
2. Should there be stricter control?
3. Who is more responsible: media or users?
4. What would improve the situation?

👉 Focus:
- justification  
- decision-making  
- real-life thinking  

---

## 9. Real Task

Write 3 short rules for:

👉 responsible media behaviour  

Example:

- Media should verify information before publishing  

---

## 10. Writing Task

Write **200–240 words**.

Topic:

**Who is responsible for misinformation: media or people?**

Include:

- both sides  
- examples  
- consequences  
- your conclusion
