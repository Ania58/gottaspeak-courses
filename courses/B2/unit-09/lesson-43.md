# Lesson 43 – Media Bias and Framing

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- recognise how media frame information  
- identify bias through language choices  
- compare different versions of the same story  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. Can the same event be described in different ways?
2. Do media always present facts objectively?
3. How can language change perception?
4. Have you ever changed your opinion after hearing another version of a story?

---

## 2. Same Event – Different Framing

Situation:

👉 A group of people gathers in a city centre to protest against rising prices.

Headlines:

1. “Citizens Demand Action in Peaceful Protest”  
2. “Crowds Disrupt City Centre During Demonstration”  
3. “Public Anger Grows as Protest Spreads”  

---

### Task

1. What image does each headline create?  
2. Which one sounds:
- positive?  
- neutral?  
- negative?  

3. What words influence your perception the most?

---

## 3. Spot the Bias

Read the statements:

1. “The government finally responded to public concerns.”  
2. “The government was forced to react under pressure.”  
3. “The government made a decision after careful consideration.”  

---

### Task

For each statement:

👉 What attitude does it suggest?  
👉 What is emphasised?  

---

## 4. Language Focus – Framing

Media often:

- focus on different details  
- emphasise certain aspects  
- omit other information  

---

### Task

Look at the situation:

👉 A company increases prices slightly.

Write:

1. a headline that presents it positively  
2. a headline that presents it negatively  
3. a neutral headline  

---

## 5. Comparing Perspectives

Read the descriptions:

A: “The company is adapting to economic challenges.”  
B: “The company is taking advantage of customers.”  

---

### Task

1. What is the difference in perspective?  
2. What assumptions does each version make?  
3. Which one sounds more objective?

---

## 6. Information Selection

Situation:

👉 A new policy reduces working hours but also lowers salaries.

---

### Task

Choose what to highlight in a report:

- reduced working hours  
- lower salaries  
- employee reactions  
- company explanation  

👉 Then explain:
- what image your version creates  
- what you chose NOT to include  

---

## 7. Critical Thinking – Hidden Influence

Read the statement:

👉 “Experts warn that this change could have serious consequences.”

---

### Task

Answer:

1. What makes this statement sound serious?  
2. What information is missing?  
3. How could it be interpreted differently?

---

## 8. Decision Task

You read two articles about the same topic:

- one uses emotional language  
- one uses neutral language  

---

### Task

Which one do you trust more?

👉 Explain:
- why  
- what signals reliability  

---

## 9. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. Is bias always intentional?
2. Should media be neutral?
3. Can we ever avoid bias completely?
4. How can people recognise framing?

👉 Focus:
- analysis  
- examples  
- reasoning  

---

## 10. Writing Task

Write **180–220 words**.

Topic:

**How media framing influences our understanding of events**

Include:

- examples  
- explanation  
- consequences  
- your opinion
