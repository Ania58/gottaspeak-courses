# Lesson 44 – Reacting to News and Information

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- react naturally to news and information  
- express doubt, surprise and concern  
- respond appropriately in conversation  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. How do you usually react to surprising news?
2. Do you believe everything you hear immediately?
3. What kind of news makes you sceptical?
4. Do people overreact to news?

---

## 2. Types of Reactions

Match the reaction with the situation.

Reactions:
1. surprise  
2. doubt  
3. concern  
4. indifference  

Situations:

a) You hear something shocking  
b) You don’t believe the information  
c) You feel worried about consequences  
d) You don’t care much  

---

## 3. Real Reactions – Language

We react in different ways:

---

### 🔹 Surprise

- That’s quite surprising.  
- I didn’t expect that.  

---

### 🔹 Doubt / scepticism

- I find that hard to believe.  
- That doesn’t sound right.  

---

### 🔹 Concern

- That’s quite worrying.  
- That could be a problem.  

---

### 🔹 Neutral reaction

- That’s interesting.  
- I see.  

---

## 4. Reaction Practice

Read the situations and respond naturally.

1. “This product guarantees success for everyone.”  
2. “Scientists say this problem is getting worse.”  
3. “A company made a huge profit in one day.”  
4. “This method works instantly.”  
5. “People are quitting their jobs to follow trends.”  
6. “This news story is everywhere online.”  

👉 Choose your reaction:
- surprise  
- doubt  
- concern  
- neutral  

---

## 5. Reaction Chains (Conversation Skill)

React to the statement and continue the conversation.

Example:

A: “This app is changing everything.”  
B: That’s interesting. What exactly does it do?

---

### Task

4. “Experts say this is a serious issue.”  

👉 Respond and ask a follow-up question
