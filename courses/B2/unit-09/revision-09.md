# Revision – Unit 9 (B2)

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- analyse news and media critically  
- recognise manipulation and bias  
- react naturally to information  
- form informed and balanced opinions  

---

## 1. Warm-up (Discussion)

Discuss:

1. How do you usually consume news?
2. Do you trust modern journalism?
3. Has technology improved or damaged media quality?
4. Do people still think critically when reading news?

👉 Try to:
- challenge one idea  
- give one balanced opinion  

---

## 2. Headlines Under Pressure

You have to choose ONE headline for an article about:

👉 journalists publishing news quickly with less verification  

Options:

1. “Media Rush Leads to Falling Standards”  
2. “Faster News Keeps People Informed”  
3. “Journalism Evolves in the Digital Age”  

---

### Task

👉 Choose one and explain:
- what perspective it represents  
- what it hides  
- how it influences the reader  

---

## 3. Information Filter

You see these 4 news sources:

- a detailed article with sources  
- a short viral post  
- a video with emotional commentary  
- a headline with no context  

---

### Task

👉 Decide:
1. which one you check first  
2. which one you ignore  
3. which one you verify  

👉 Explain your reasoning  

---

## 4. Language in Action – React Naturally

React to the news:

1. “This story was published without verification.”  
2. “This article has been shared by millions.”  
3. “Journalists are under pressure to publish faster.”  
4. “People only read headlines now.”  

👉 For each:
- show reaction (doubt / concern / surprise / neutral)  
- add one follow-up thought  

---

## 5. Bias Detector

Read the statements:

1. “Modern journalism is worse than before.”  
2. “People don’t care about truth anymore.”  
3. “Online media has destroyed quality reporting.”  

---

### Task

👉 For each:
- what assumption is being made?  
- what alternative view exists?  

---

## 6. Mini Case Study

Situation:

👉 A news outlet publishes a story quickly. Later, some details turn out to be incorrect.

---

### Task

Answer:

1. What went wrong?  
2. Who is responsible?  
3. What should happen next?  

---

### Part 1 – General Understanding

1. What types of media does the speaker use?  
2. How does the speaker choose what to read or watch?  

---

### Part 2 – Key Ideas

1. How has technology changed journalism?  
2. Why is news sometimes less reliable today?  
3. What has changed about the audience?  
4. What are the advantages of modern media?  

---

### Part 3 – Deeper Understanding

1. Why are headlines becoming more sensational?  
2. What problem do journalists face today?  
3. Is the speaker completely negative about modern media? Explain.

### Part 4 – Interpretation

Find examples of:

1. criticism of modern journalism  
2. balanced thinking  
3. personal media habits

## 8. Decision Task – Your Media Habits

You want to stay informed but avoid misinformation.

Choose your strategy:

- read fewer but high-quality sources  
- follow many sources and compare  
- rely on social media updates  

---

### Task

👉 Explain:
- your choice  
- risks  
- benefits

## 9. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. Is journalism getting worse or just different?
2. Do people prefer speed over accuracy?
3. How should we consume news responsibly?
4. What makes a source trustworthy?

👉 Focus:
- justification  
- examples  
- evaluation
