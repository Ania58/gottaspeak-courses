# Lesson 42 – Fake News and Misinformation

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- recognise how fake news works  
- explain why people believe misinformation  
- react critically to doubtful information  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. Why do people believe fake news?
2. Have you ever shared something that wasn’t true?
3. Is fake news always intentional?
4. Who is responsible for stopping misinformation?

---

## 2. What Makes Information Suspicious?

Read the examples:

1. “Scientists confirm shocking secret they don’t want you to know.”  
2. “This simple trick will make you rich in 3 days.”  
3. “Doctors hate this one method!”  
4. “Breaking: Something big just happened.”  

---

### Task

For each example:

👉 What makes it suspicious?  
👉 What is the intention behind it?  

---

## 3. Why People Believe It

Match the reason with explanation.

1. emotional reaction  
2. confirmation bias  
3. lack of time  
4. authority effect  

a) people trust something because an “expert” is mentioned  
b) people react without thinking  
c) people accept information that supports their beliefs  
d) people don’t check details  

---

## 4. Case Analysis

Read the situation:

👉 A post claims that a certain food cures serious illnesses. It includes personal stories but no scientific evidence.

---

### Task

Answer:

1. Why might people believe this?  
2. What is missing from the information?  
3. What questions should you ask before trusting it?  

---

## 5. Language Focus – Reacting to Doubtful Information

Instead of saying:
❌ This is fake  

We say:

- This doesn’t seem entirely reliable.  
- It raises some questions.  
- It’s difficult to verify.  
- I’d be cautious about trusting this.  

---

### Task

React to the statements:

1. “This product works instantly for everyone.”  
2. “Everyone agrees this is true.”  
3. “This was shared by thousands of people.”  
4. “Experts say this is the best solution.”  

👉 Express doubt or caution  

---

## 6. Information Flow – How Fake News Spreads

Put the stages in logical order:

- People share it without checking  
- A message is created  
- It reaches a large audience  
- It appears convincing  
- It spreads quickly online  

---

### Follow-up

👉 Where in this process can it be stopped?

---

## 7. Critical Thinking – Real vs Suspicious

You see these posts:

1. A detailed article with sources and data  
2. A short emotional post with no source  
3. A post shared by a well-known influencer  
4. A news article from an unknown website  

---

### Task

Rank them from:
👉 most trustworthy → least trustworthy  

👉 Explain your reasoning  

---

## 8. Decision Task

You see a shocking post online.

Choose your reaction and explain:

- share immediately  
- ignore it  
- check it first  

👉 What factors influence your decision?

---

## 9. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. Why is fake news so effective?
2. Are people responsible for what they share?
3. Can social media control misinformation?
4. How can people think more critically?

👉 Focus:
- reasoning  
- evaluation  
- real-life examples  

---

## 10. Writing Task

Write **180–220 words**.

Topic:

**Why people believe fake news**

Include:

- psychological reasons  
- examples  
- consequences  
- your opinion
