# Lesson 71 – The Entertainment Industry

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- talk about the entertainment industry using natural B2 language  
- describe roles and responsibilities  
- explain how productions are created  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. What types of films or shows do you watch most often?
2. Do you ever think about how films are made?
3. What makes a production successful?
4. Is acting more talent or hard work?

---

1. actor  
2. director  
3. producer  
4. screenwriter  
5. editor

## 3. Useful Collocations

Match:

1. work on  
2. be involved in  
3. take part in  
4. be responsible for  
5. play  

---

a) a project  
b) a role  
c) a production  
d) something  
e) a film  

---

### Task

👉 Complete:

1. She is currently __________ a new series.  
2. He was __________ the production from start to finish.  
3. They __________ a major film project.  
4. He __________ the main role in the film.  
5. She __________ several productions last year.  

---

## 4. Language in Action

We describe work in the industry like this:

- I’m currently working on a project.  
- I was involved in a production last year.  
- I’m responsible for managing the team.  
- He played a key role in the film.  

---

### Task

👉 Describe:

1. a film or series you like  
2. who was involved  
3. what made it successful

## 5. Phrasal Verbs – Industry Context

Match:

1. come out  
2. take on  
3. carry out  
4. bring out  

Meanings:

b) release (a film/product)  
c) accept a role or responsibility
