# Lesson 75 – Opinions and Reviews

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- express opinions in a detailed and natural way  
- describe strengths and weaknesses  
- give balanced reviews  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. Do you usually read reviews before watching something?
2. What makes a review useful?
3. Do you trust other people’s opinions?
4. Have you ever disagreed with a review?

---

## 2. Moving Beyond “Good” and “Bad”

Replace:

1. It was good  
2. It was bad  
3. It was interesting  
4. It was boring  

---

👉 Use:

- engaging  
- disappointing  
- predictable  
- well-executed  
- overrated  

---

### Task

👉 Rewrite:

1. The film was good.  
2. The acting was bad.  
3. The story was interesting.  
4. The ending was boring.  

---

## 3. Structuring an Opinion

We often say:

- What stood out was…  
- I wouldn’t say it was…, but…  
- It was rather…  
- Overall, I’d say…  

---

### Task

👉 Complete:

1. What stood out was __________.  
2. I wouldn’t say it was __________, but __________.  
3. It was rather __________.  
4. Overall, I’d say __________.  

---

## 4. Describing Performances

Match:

1. convincing  
2. flat  
3. powerful  
4. natural  
5. exaggerated  

---

a) realistic  
b) too strong/artificial  
c) strong emotional impact  
d) believable  
e) lacking emotion  

---

### Task

👉 Complete:

1. The performance was very __________.  
2. It felt a bit __________ at times.  
3. The actor gave a __________ performance.  
4. Some scenes were too __________.  

---

## 5. Phrasal Verbs – Reviews

Match:

1. stand out  
2. fall short  
3. live up to  
4. come across  

Meanings:

a) seem to be  
b) be noticeable  
c) not reach expectations  
d) meet expectations  

---

### Task

👉 Create sentences about:

- something impressive  
- something disappointing  
- expectations  
- how something feels  

---

## 6. Idioms – Opinions

Match:

1. hit or miss  
2. worth watching  
3. not live up to the hype  
4. steal the show  

Meanings:

a) inconsistent  
b) very good  
c) disappoint expectations  
d) be the best part  

---

### Task

👉 Use TWO idioms in short review-style sentences  

---

## 7. Real-life Scenario

Situation:

👉 Someone asks you if a film is worth watching.

---

### Task

👉 Give a balanced answer:

- positives  
- negatives  
- final recommendation  

---

## 8. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. What makes a film successful?
2. Is acting or story more important?
3. Do people judge films too quickly?
4. What makes a performance memorable?

👉 Try to use:
- 2 phrasal verbs  
- 1 idiom  

---

## 9. Writing Task

Write **180–220 words**.

Topic:

**Write a review of a film or series**

Include:

- description  
- strengths and weaknesses  
- your opinion  
- recommendation
