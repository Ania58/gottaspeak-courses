# Lesson 55 – Moving On and Emotional Growth

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- describe emotional growth  
- reflect on past experiences  
- explain how people move on and improve  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. Do people change after relationships?
2. What can we learn from difficult experiences?
3. Is emotional growth automatic?
4. How long does it take to “move on”?

---

## 2. Change Over Time

Read the situations:

1. Someone becomes more independent after a breakup.  
2. A person understands their mistakes and changes behaviour.  
3. Someone repeats the same pattern in relationships.  
4. A person becomes more careful and emotionally aware.  

---

### Task

👉 For each:

- what changed?  
- was it positive or negative?  

---

## 3. Learning from Experience

Look at the ideas:

- understanding your behaviour  
- recognising mistakes  
- improving communication  
- setting boundaries  
- avoiding the same problems  

---

### Task

👉 Choose TWO that are most important  

Explain:
- why  
- what happens without them  

---

## 4. Language in Action

We describe growth like this:

- I learned to understand myself better.  
- I realised what I really need.  
- I started to make better decisions.  
- I became more aware of my behaviour.  
- It helped me grow as a person.  

---

### Task

👉 Describe:

1. something you learned from a difficult situation  
2. how it changed your behaviour  
3. what is different now  

---

## 5. Phrasal Verbs – Moving Forward

Match:

1. move on  
2. get back into  
3. work through  
4. open up  

Meanings:

a) return to something  
b) deal with a problem  
c) continue with life  
d) share emotions  

---

### Task

👉 Describe situations where people:

- continue after a difficult experience  
- deal with emotional problems  
- start something again  
- express feelings  

---

## 6. Idioms – Growth & Change

Match:

1. learn from your mistakes  
2. grow as a person  
3. turn a negative into a positive  
4. gain perspective  
5. start fresh  

Meanings:

a) understand things more clearly  
b) improve emotionally  
c) begin again  
d) improve from experience  
e) find something good in a bad situation  

---

### Task

👉 Choose TWO idioms and describe:

- one situation of growth  
- one situation where someone didn’t learn  

---

## 7. Scenario – Moving On

Situation:

👉 Someone feels ready to move forward after a difficult relationship.

---

### Task

👉 What should they focus on?

Choose and explain:

- new experiences  
- self-reflection  
- avoiding relationships  
- building confidence  

---

## 8. Critical Thinking

Read:

👉 “People only grow when something goes wrong.”

---

### Task

1. Do you agree?  
2. Give an example  
3. What is missing from this idea?  

---

## 9. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. What helps people move on?
2. Do people always learn from their mistakes?
3. Can difficult experiences be positive?
4. What does emotional growth look like?

👉 Try to use:
- 2 phrasal verbs  
- 1 idiom  

---

## 10. Writing Task

Write **180–220 words**.

Topic:

**How difficult experiences help people grow**

Include:

- examples  
- changes  
- benefits  
- your opinion
