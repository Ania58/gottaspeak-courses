# Lesson 52 – Communication in Relationships

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- recognise communication styles  
- explain misunderstandings  
- react appropriately in conversations  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. Is it easy to communicate clearly?
2. Why do people misunderstand each other?
3. Is it better to be direct or indirect?
4. Do people always say what they really mean?

---

## 2. When Communication Fails

Read the situations:

1. Someone says “It’s fine” but is clearly upset.  
2. A person avoids talking about a problem.  
3. Someone reacts strongly to a small comment.  
4. A person misunderstands a message completely.  

---

### Task

👉 For each:

- what is the real problem?  
- what caused the misunderstanding?  

---

## 3. Direct vs Indirect Communication

Look at the examples:

- “This doesn’t work.”  
- “Maybe we could try something else…”  

---

### Task

👉 For each style:

- what are the advantages?  
- what are the risks?  

👉 When is each better?

---

## 4. Language in Action

We describe communication like this:

- That didn’t come across the way I intended.  
- It sounded more serious than I meant.  
- I didn’t mean it like that.  
- I tend to avoid difficult conversations.  

---

### Task

👉 Describe situations where:

1. you misunderstood someone  
2. someone misunderstood you  
3. communication caused a problem  

---

## 5. Phrasal Verbs – Communication

Match:

1. bring up  
2. sort out  
3. talk over  
4. open up  

Meanings:

a) discuss carefully  
b) mention a topic  
c) solve a problem  
d) share feelings  

---

### Task

👉 Describe situations where people:

- start a difficult conversation  
- solve a misunderstanding  
- discuss a problem  
- express emotions  

---

## 6. Idioms – Communication

Match:

1. get the wrong idea  
2. clear the air  
3. read between the lines  
4. say what’s on your mind  
5. keep something to yourself  

Meanings:

a) not express thoughts  
b) misunderstand  
c) express feelings honestly  
d) understand hidden meaning  
e) resolve tension  

---

### Task

👉 Choose TWO idioms and describe real-life situations  

---

## 7. Mini Dialogues

Read:

A: “You never listen to me.”  
B: “That’s not true!”  

---

### Task

👉 Improve the response:

- make it less aggressive  
- keep the meaning  

---

Next:

A: “I’m fine.”  
B: __________________________  

👉 Show:
- awareness  
- sensitivity  

---

## 8. Real-life Scenario

Situation:

👉 You feel misunderstood in a conversation.

---

### Task

Write what you would say to:

- explain your intention  
- avoid conflict  
- clarify the message  

---

## 9. Decision Task

Situation:

👉 You disagree with someone close to you.

Options:

- say exactly what you think  
- stay quiet  
- explain your opinion carefully  

---

### Task

👉 Choose and explain:
- why  
- risks  
- what usually works best  

---

## 10. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. Why do misunderstandings happen?
2. Is honesty always the best approach?
3. How can people communicate better?
4. What makes communication difficult?

👉 Try to use:
- 2 phrasal verbs  
- 1 idiom  

---

## 11. Writing Task

Write **180–220 words**.

Topic:

**Why communication problems happen in relationships**

Include:

- causes  
- examples  
- consequences  
- solutions
