# Revision – Unit 11 (B2)

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- analyse relationship dynamics  
- explain emotional reactions  
- evaluate behaviour in relationships  
- reflect on personal experiences  

---

## 1. Warm-up (Discussion)

Discuss:

1. Do people keep memories from past relationships?
2. Is it better to forget or remember?
3. Why do small things become emotionally important?
4. Can relationships end without conflict?

---

## 2. Emotional Value of Objects

Read the examples:

- an old message  
- a gift  
- a photo  
- a small personal item  

---

### Task

👉 Choose ONE and explain:

- why it might be emotionally important  
- why it might be difficult to let go  

---

## 3. Reaction to Breakups

People react differently:

- keep memories  
- throw everything away  
- talk about it  
- avoid thinking about it  

---

### Task

👉 Rank these from:
most helpful → least helpful  

👉 Justify your ranking  

---

## 4. Phrasal Verbs – Relationships

Use:

- break up  
- move on  
- get over  
- hold on to  

---

### Task

👉 Describe:

1. someone who can’t move on  
2. someone who recovers quickly  
3. someone who keeps emotional attachment  

---

## 5. Idioms – Emotional Situations

Use:

- be stuck in the past  
- let go  
- go through a tough time  
- start fresh  

---

### Task

👉 Create short scenarios using TWO idioms  

---

## 6. Mini Case

Situation:

👉 Two people break up but continue working together.

---

### Task

👉 What challenges might they face?  
👉 What makes it possible (or not)?  

---

## 7. Listening – Museum of Broken Relationships

🔗 **Listening:**  
[Museum of Broken Relationships](https://youtu.be/R6Q731asMtg?si=ZbNu_k6tdDfoG06o)

---

## 8. Listening Tasks

### Part 1 – General Understanding

1. What is the main idea of the museum?  
2. Why was it created?  

---

### Part 2 – Key Details

1. What object inspired the idea of the museum?  
2. What makes the museum unique?  
3. Who writes the stories?  
4. What is the purpose of donating objects?  

---

### Part 3 – Deeper Understanding

1. Why do small objects become emotionally powerful?  
2. How does the museum help people emotionally?  
3. What does this show about human relationships?  

---

### Part 4 – Interpretation

1. Why do people connect with strangers’ stories?  
2. What role does storytelling play in emotional healing?  
3. Is this idea more symbolic or practical?  

---

## 9. Decision Task

Situation:

👉 You have an object that reminds you of a past relationship.

Options:

- keep it  
- throw it away  
- give it meaning (like in the museum)  

---

### Task

👉 Choose and explain:
- why  
- emotional impact  
- long-term effect  

---

## 10. Critical Thinking

Read:

👉 “Letting go is more important than remembering.”

---

### Task

1. Do you agree?  
2. What is the opposite view?  
3. When is remembering useful?  

---

## 11. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. Why do people hold on to the past?
2. What helps people move on?
3. Can emotional experiences be shared?
4. Do people learn from relationships?

👉 Try to use:
- 2 phrasal verbs  
- 1 idiom  

---

## 12. Writing Task

Write **200–240 words**.

Topic:

**How people deal with the end of relationships**

Include:

- emotions  
- behaviours  
- strategies  
- your opinion
