# Lesson 53 – Conflict and Arguments

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- explain why conflicts happen  
- recognise different reactions in arguments  
- choose more effective ways to deal with conflict  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. Why do people argue?
2. Are arguments always negative?
3. How do you usually react in conflict?
4. Is it better to avoid or face conflict?

---

## 2. Conflict in Action

Read the situations:

1. A small disagreement turns into a serious argument.  
2. Someone reacts emotionally without listening.  
3. A person avoids conflict completely.  
4. Two people stop talking after an argument.  

---

### Task

👉 For each:

- what caused the conflict?  
- what made it worse?  

---

## 3. Escalation vs Resolution

Look at the reactions:

- raising your voice  
- interrupting  
- listening carefully  
- staying calm  
- blaming the other person  

---

### Task

👉 Group them into:

- escalating conflict  
- reducing conflict  

👉 Then:
Which one is the most damaging? Why?

---

## 4. Language in Action

We describe conflict like this:

- I lost my temper.  
- Things got out of control.  
- I tried to stay calm.  
- It turned into a bigger problem.  
- I avoided the conversation.  

---

### Task

👉 Describe a situation where:

1. an argument escalated  
2. someone stayed calm  
3. a conflict was avoided  

---

## 5. Phrasal Verbs – Conflict

Match:

1. fall out  
2. make up  
3. calm down  
4. bring up  

Meanings:

a) become friends again  
b) argue and stop being friendly  
c) become less emotional  
d) mention a problem  

---

### Task

👉 Describe situations where people:

- argue seriously  
- fix a conflict  
- become calmer  
- start a difficult conversation  

---

## 6. Idioms – Arguments

Match:

1. lose your temper  
2. blow up  
3. keep your cool  
4. clear the air  
5. get into an argument  

Meanings:

a) stay calm  
b) become very angry  
c) start arguing  
d) remove tension  
e) react suddenly with anger  

---

### Task

👉 Choose TWO idioms and describe real-life situations  

---

## 7. Mini Dialogues – Real Reactions

Read:

A: “You never listen to me.”  
B: “That’s not true!”  

---

### Task

👉 Suggest a better response that:
- reduces conflict  
- keeps the conversation going  

---

Next:

A: “Why didn’t you tell me earlier?”  
B: __________________________  

👉 Show:
- calm reaction  
- explanation  

---

## 8. Decision Task

Situation:

👉 You feel angry during a conversation.

Options:

- react immediately  
- stay quiet  
- pause and respond later  

---

### Task

👉 Choose and explain:
- why  
- risks  
- best long-term result  

---

## 9. Critical Thinking

Read:

👉 “Most arguments are not about the real problem.”

---

### Task

1. Do you agree?  
2. Give an example  
3. What is usually the real issue?  

---

## 10. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. Why do arguments escalate?
2. How can people stay calm?
3. Is avoiding conflict a good idea?
4. What makes communication worse in arguments?

👉 Try to use:
- 2 phrasal verbs  
- 1 idiom  

---

## 11. Writing Task

Write **180–220 words**.

Topic:

**Why people argue and how conflicts can be resolved**

Include:

- causes  
- examples  
- consequences  
- solutions
