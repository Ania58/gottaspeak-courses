# Lesson 54 – Breakups and Emotional Reactions

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- describe emotional reactions after a breakup  
- explain how people deal with endings  
- analyse different ways of coping  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. Why are breakups so difficult?
2. Do people react differently to the same situation?
3. Is it better to move on quickly or take time?
4. What helps people deal with emotional situations?

---

## 2. Emotional Reactions

Read the situations:

1. Someone keeps thinking about the past.  
2. A person tries to distract themselves all the time.  
3. Someone removes everything that reminds them of the relationship.  
4. A person talks about it openly with others.  

---

### Task

👉 For each:

- what emotion is behind it?  
- is it a healthy reaction?  

---

## 3. Ways of Coping

Look at the behaviours:

- avoiding reminders  
- keeping memories  
- talking to others  
- focusing on new goals  
- ignoring emotions  

---

### Task

👉 Group them into:

- helpful  
- unhelpful  
- depends on the situation  

👉 Explain your choices  

---

## 4. Language in Action

We describe emotional reactions like this:

- I found it hard to move on.  
- I struggled to stop thinking about it.  
- I tried to focus on something else.  
- I started to feel better over time.  
- It took me a while to accept it.  

---

### Task

👉 Describe:

1. a difficult emotional situation  
2. how someone reacted  
3. what helped them  

---

## 5. Phrasal Verbs – Relationships Ending

Match:

1. break up  
2. get over  
3. move on  
4. hold on to  

Meanings:

a) continue thinking about something  
b) end a relationship  
c) recover emotionally  
d) continue with life  

---

### Task

👉 Describe situations where people:

- end a relationship  
- recover  
- cannot let go  
- start again  

---

## 6. Idioms – Emotional Reactions

Match:

1. get over someone  
2. go through a tough time  
3. let go  
4. be stuck in the past  
5. start fresh  

Meanings:

a) move on  
b) have difficulty emotionally  
c) release something emotionally  
d) experience difficulty  
e) begin again  

---

### Task

👉 Choose TWO idioms and describe:

- one difficult situation  
- one positive outcome  

---

## 7. Scenario – Emotional Choice

Situation:

👉 You still think about a past relationship.

Options:

- keep memories  
- remove everything  
- focus on new experiences  

---

### Task

👉 Choose and explain:
- why  
- risks  
- what helps long-term  

---

## 8. Perspective Task

Read:

👉 “Holding on to memories helps people heal.”

---

### Task

Discuss:

1. Why do people find it hard to move on?
2. What helps people recover emotionally?
3. Is it good to stay in contact with an ex?
4. Do people learn from relationships?

👉 Try to use:
- 2 phrasal verbs
