# Lesson 51 – Types of Relationships

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- describe different types of relationships  
- talk about relationship dynamics  
- analyse what makes relationships strong or difficult  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. What makes a relationship strong?
2. Are friendships different from romantic relationships?
3. Do relationships change over time?
4. Is it easy to maintain long-term relationships?

---

## 2. Types of Relationships

Look at the situations:

1. Two people talk every day and share everything.  
2. Friends who used to be close but rarely talk now.  
3. A couple who often argue but stay together.  
4. People who get along well but aren’t very close.  

---

### Task

👉 Describe each relationship:

- close / distant  
- stable / difficult  
- improving / getting worse  

👉 Then:
Which one seems healthiest? Why?

---

## 3. Relationship Dynamics

Look at the behaviours:

- supporting each other  
- avoiding conflict  
- arguing frequently  
- losing contact  
- trusting each other  

---

### Task

👉 Group them into:

- positive dynamics  
- negative dynamics  

👉 Then:
Which ones are most damaging long-term?

---

## 4. Language in Action

We describe relationships like this:

- We get on really well.  
- We’ve drifted apart over time.  
- We used to be very close.  
- We don’t see eye to eye.  

---

### Task

👉 Describe:

1. a strong relationship  
2. a relationship that changed  
3. a difficult relationship  

---

## 5. Phrasal Verbs – Relationships

Match:

1. get on with  
2. fall out with  
3. drift apart  
4. make up  

Meanings:

a) become less close  
b) have a good relationship  
c) argue and stop being friendly  
d) become friends again  

---

### Task

👉 Describe real situations where people:

- stop talking  
- become close  
- argue  
- repair a relationship  

---

## 6. Idioms – Relationships

Match:

1. see eye to eye  
2. be on good terms  
3. hit it off  
4. keep in touch  
5. go your separate ways  

Meanings:

a) stay in contact  
b) agree  
c) separate  
d) have a good relationship  
e) like each other immediately  

---

### Task

👉 Choose TWO idioms and describe:

- one positive relationship  
- one relationship that ended  

---

## 7. Real-life Scenario

Situation:

👉 You have a friend you were very close to, but now you rarely talk.

---

### Task

Explain:

- what might have happened  
- how the relationship changed  
- what could improve it  

---

## 8. Decision Task

Situation:

👉 You feel a relationship is becoming negative.

Options:

- try to fix it  
- ignore the problem  
- distance yourself  

---

### Task

👉 Choose and explain:
- why  
- risks  
- possible outcomes  

---

## 9. Critical Thinking

Read:

👉 “Some relationships naturally fade, and that’s okay.”

---

### Task

1. Do you agree?  
2. Give one example  
3. What is the limitation of this idea?  

---

## 10. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. Why do relationships change over time?
2. Is it important to maintain all relationships?
3. What causes people to drift apart?
4. Can broken relationships always be fixed?

👉 Try to use:
- 2 phrasal verbs  
- 1 idiom  

---

## 11. Writing Task

Write **180–220 words**.

Topic:

**What makes a relationship strong or weak?**

Include:

- examples  
- reasons  
- changes over time  
- your opinion
