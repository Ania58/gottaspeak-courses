## 4. Grammar – Expressing Preference & Justification

We use these expressions to sound natural when making decisions.

---

### 🔹 Common structures

- I’d rather buy something cheaper.  
- I tend to choose quality over price.  
- I usually go for well-known brands.  
- I prefer shopping online because it’s convenient.  
- Since it’s cheaper, I decided to buy it.

## 7. Reading – Consumer Decisions

Read the text.

Making decisions about what to buy is not always easy. People are influenced by many factors, including price, quality and personal preferences.

Some consumers prefer to buy cheaper products, while others focus on long-term value. Although expensive items may seem costly at first, they can last longer and be more reliable.

Advertising also plays a major role. Many people are attracted to well-known brands, even if cheaper alternatives are available.

At the same time, more people are becoming aware of their spending habits. They try to make better decisions by comparing products and thinking carefully before buying.

In the end, the best choice depends on individual priorities and needs.

---
