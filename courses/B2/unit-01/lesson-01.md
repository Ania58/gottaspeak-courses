## 4. Advanced Language – Modifiers (B2)

We often use modifiers to show intensity or degree.

### Examples:

- **very** shy → basic  
- **fairly** shy → moderate  
- **quite** shy → stronger  
- **extremely** shy → very strong  

More advanced modifiers:

- highly motivated  
- deeply concerned  
- slightly awkward  
- rather confident  
- fairly independent

## 7. Reading – Understanding Yourself

Read the text.

Understanding your own personality is not always easy. Many people believe they know themselves well, but their behaviour often changes depending on the situation. For example, someone may appear confident at work but feel insecure in social situations.

One important aspect of self-awareness is recognising your strengths and weaknesses. People who are highly self-aware tend to make better decisions because they understand how their emotions influence their actions. On the other hand, those who lack self-awareness may repeat the same mistakes without realising it.

Another challenge is accepting uncomfortable truths about yourself. It can be difficult to admit that you are impatient, overly sensitive, or afraid of criticism. However, this honesty is essential for personal growth.

Personality is not completely fixed. While some traits remain stable, people can develop new habits and ways of thinking. Over time, individuals may become more confident, more open-minded, or better at managing their emotions.

In the end, self-awareness is a continuous process. The more you reflect on your thoughts and behaviour, the better you understand who you are and how you relate to others.
