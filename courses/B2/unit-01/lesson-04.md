## 4. Grammar – Gerunds vs Infinitives (Meaning Change)

Some verbs change meaning depending on the form.

---

### 🔹 stop

- stop **doing** → finish an activity  
👉 I stopped smoking.  

- stop **to do** → pause one action to do another  
👉 I stopped to smoke.  

---

### 🔹 remember

- remember **doing** → recall a past action  
👉 I remember meeting her.  

- remember **to do** → not forget to do something  
👉 Remember to call her.  

---

### 🔹 try

- try **doing** → experiment  
👉 Try restarting the system.  

- try **to do** → make an effort  
👉 I tried to open the door.  

---

### 🔹 regret

- regret **doing** → feel sorry about the past  
👉 I regret saying that.  

- regret **to do** → formal, used for bad news  
👉 We regret to inform you…

## 7. Reading – Growth Through Difficulty

Read the text.

Personal growth rarely happens without challenges. In fact, many people only begin to develop new skills or perspectives when they are forced out of their comfort zone. Difficult situations often require individuals to adapt, learn and rethink their approach to problems.

One common mistake is avoiding challenges altogether. While this may feel safer in the short term, it limits personal development. People who refuse to take risks may never discover their full potential.

Another important factor is how people respond to failure. Some individuals give up after experiencing a setback, while others use it as motivation to improve. The difference often lies in mindset. Those with a growth mindset see failure as an opportunity to learn rather than as a sign of weakness.

It is also important to recognise that progress is not always immediate. People may feel like they have “hit a wall” when they stop seeing improvement. However, this is often a natural part of the learning process.

In the end, growth requires patience, effort and a willingness to face difficulties. Those who persist are more likely to achieve long-term success.
