## 4. Grammar – Discourse Markers (B2)

We use discourse markers to organise ideas and sound more natural and advanced.

---

### 🔹 Giving opinions

- In my opinion,  
- From my point of view,  
- Personally, I believe that…  

---

### 🔹 Adding ideas

- Furthermore,  
- In addition,  
- What’s more,  

---

### 🔹 Contrasting ideas

- However,  
- On the other hand,  
- Having said that,  

---

### 🔹 Giving examples

- For instance,  
- For example,  

---

### 🔹 Concluding

- In conclusion,  
- To sum up,  
- All things considered,  

---

### Practice

Choose the best option.

1. I understand your point. __________, I still disagree.  
(a) Furthermore (b) However (c) For example  

2. __________, people should think more carefully before acting.  
(a) In conclusion (b) For instance (c) However  

3. He didn’t study. __________, he failed the exam.  
(a) On the other hand (b) As a result (c) Furthermore  

4. __________, this solution might not work for everyone.  
(a) Having said that (b) In addition (c) For example

## 7. Reading – The Art of Argument

Read the text.

Being able to express your opinion clearly is an important skill in both personal and professional life. However, many people confuse having an opinion with being able to argue effectively.

A strong argument is not just about stating what you think. It requires structure, logic and evidence. Simply repeating your opinion without supporting it is unlikely to convince others.

Another important aspect is listening. Good communicators do not just speak; they also consider other perspectives. By understanding opposing views, it becomes easier to respond in a thoughtful and respectful way.

Emotions can also influence discussions. While passion can make an argument more engaging, it can also lead to conflict if not controlled. Staying calm and focused helps maintain a productive conversation.

Finally, it is important to accept that not all discussions end in agreement. In some cases, the best outcome is to respect different viewpoints and move on.
