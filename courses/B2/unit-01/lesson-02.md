## 4. Grammar – Mixed Conditionals (B2)

We use mixed conditionals to connect past and present.

### 🔹 Past → Present

If + past perfect → would + base verb

👉 Used to describe how a past situation affects the present.

Example:
- If I **had studied harder**, I **would have a better job now**.  

---

### 🔹 Present → Past

If + past simple → would have + past participle

👉 Used to describe how a present situation affected the past.

Example:
- If I **were more confident**, I **would have taken that opportunity**.

## 7. Reading – The Cost of a Decision

Read the text.

Every decision we make has consequences, some of which we only understand much later. While small daily choices may seem insignificant, major life decisions can shape our future in unexpected ways.

Many people struggle with decision-making because they fear making the wrong choice. This fear can lead to hesitation or even inaction. However, not making a decision is also a decision in itself, often with its own consequences.

Regret is a common feeling when reflecting on past decisions. People often think about what might have happened if they had chosen differently. For example, someone might say, “If I had taken that opportunity, my life would be completely different now.”

Interestingly, not all negative outcomes are truly negative. Some situations that initially seem like failures later turn out to be valuable experiences. These moments can teach important lessons and lead to personal growth.

Ultimately, decision-making is about balancing risk and reward. While it is impossible to predict every outcome, learning to accept uncertainty is an important part of personal development.
