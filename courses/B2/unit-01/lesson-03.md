## 4. Grammar – Used to / Would / Be used to / Get used to

### 🔹 Used to + base verb  
👉 past habits or states (no longer true)

- I **used to wake up** late.  

---

### 🔹 Would + base verb  
👉 repeated past actions (NOT states)

- When I was a child, I **would play** outside every day.  

❌ NOT: *I would be shy*  

---

### 🔹 Be used to + noun / -ing  
👉 being comfortable with something

- I am **used to working** under pressure.  

---

### 🔹 Get used to + noun / -ing  
👉 process of adapting

- I am **getting used to waking up early**.

## 7. Reading – Why Habits Are Hard to Change

Read the text.

Habits play a significant role in shaping our daily lives. From the moment we wake up to the way we spend our free time, much of our behaviour is automatic. This is why changing habits can be so challenging.

One reason habits are difficult to change is that they are deeply rooted in our routines. Over time, the brain creates patterns that make certain actions feel natural and effortless. As a result, even when people want to change, they often fall back into old behaviours.

Another factor is emotional attachment. Many habits are connected to feelings such as comfort or stress relief. For example, someone might eat unhealthy food not because they are hungry, but because it helps them cope with stress.

However, change is possible with consistency and awareness. People who successfully change their habits usually replace old behaviours with new ones rather than simply trying to stop. For instance, instead of giving up a bad habit completely, they introduce a healthier alternative.

In the long run, developing better habits can significantly improve quality of life. Although the process may be slow, small changes can lead to meaningful results over time.
