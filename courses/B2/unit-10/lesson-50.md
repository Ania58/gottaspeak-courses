# Lesson 50 – Changing Behaviour

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- explain how habits can change  
- evaluate different strategies for improvement  
- describe progress and setbacks  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. Is it easy to change habits?
2. What usually stops people from changing?
3. Do people need motivation or structure?
4. What is the hardest habit to break?

---

## 2. Change in Real Life

Read the situations:

1. Someone tries to stop using their phone at night but fails.  
2. A person starts exercising but quits after two weeks.  
3. Someone successfully builds a daily routine.  
4. A person improves slowly over time.  

---

### Task

👉 For each situation:

- what is happening?  
- why is it successful / unsuccessful?  

---

## 3. What Actually Works?

Look at the strategies:

- making small changes  
- setting clear goals  
- removing distractions  
- relying on motivation  
- building routines  

---

### Task

👉 Choose TWO strategies that work best  

Explain:
- why  
- when they fail  

---

## 4. Language in Action

We describe change like this:

- I managed to change my routine.  
- I ended up going back to old habits.  
- I tried to break the habit.  
- I kept doing it consistently.  
- I developed a better routine.  

---

## 5. Phrasal Verbs – Change

Match:

1. give up  
2. stick with  
3. cut down on  
4. fall back into  

Meanings:

a) return to old behaviour  
b) reduce  
c) continue  
d) stop  

---

### Task

👉 Describe situations where people:

- stop doing something  
- continue despite difficulty  
- reduce a habit  
- return to old habits  

---

## 6. Idioms – Change & Progress

Match:

1. turn over a new leaf  
2. back to square one  
3. take small steps  
4. make progress  
5. get back on track  

Meanings:

a) improve gradually  
b) return to the beginning  
c) start behaving better  
d) continue improving  
e) return to a good routine  

---

### Task

👉 Choose TWO idioms and describe real-life examples  

---

## 7. Case Study

Situation:

👉 Someone wants to stop procrastinating but keeps failing.

---

### Task

1. What is the real problem?  
2. What strategy would help most?  
3. What mistake are they making?

## 8. Decision Task

Situation:

👉 You want to build a new habit.

Choose your approach:

- start big and push yourself  
- start small and build slowly  
- wait until you feel ready  

---

### Task

👉 Choose and explain:
- why  
- risks  
- what usually works long-term

## 9. Critical Thinking

Read:

👉 “People fail because they expect fast results.”

---

### Task

## 10. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. Why is change so difficult?
2. What helps people stay consistent?
3. Can people completely change their behaviour?
4. What is more important: discipline or environment?

👉 Try to use:
- 2 phrasal verbs  
- 1 idiom
