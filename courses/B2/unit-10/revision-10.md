# Revision – Unit 10 (B2)

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- analyse behaviour and habits  
- explain decision-making processes  
- recognise thinking errors  
- evaluate strategies for change  

---

## 1. Warm-up (Discussion)

Discuss:

1. Are people in control of their habits?
2. What is harder to change: behaviour or mindset?
3. Why do people continue harmful habits?
4. Can external help really change behaviour?

---

## 2. Behaviour Patterns

Read the situations:

1. Someone spends hours online every day  
2. A person knows something is harmful but continues  
3. Someone tries to change but returns to old habits  
4. A person improves after changing their environment  

---

### Task

👉 Match each situation with:

- lack of control  
- awareness but no action  
- relapse  
- successful change  

👉 Then explain:
What makes the difference between success and failure?

---

## 3. Habit Breakdown

Situation:

👉 A person spends too much time on their phone.

---

### Task

Break it down:

- trigger  
- behaviour  
- consequence  

👉 Then:
Which part should change first?

---

## 4. Decision Under Influence

Situation:

👉 A teenager chooses to spend all their time online instead of studying or socialising.

---

### Task

👉 What influences this decision?

Choose and explain:

- habit  
- comfort  
- avoidance  
- lack of control  
- environment  

---

## 5. Phrasal Verbs – Behaviour

Use the phrasal verbs in context:

- give up  
- fall back into  
- cut down on  
- stick to  

---

### Task

👉 Describe:

1. someone trying to stop a bad habit  
2. someone returning to old behaviour  
3. someone reducing a harmful activity  
4. someone successfully continuing a routine  

---

## 6. Idioms – Behaviour & Change

Use the idioms:

- get back on track  
- turn over a new leaf  
- back to square one  
- take small steps  

---

### Task

👉 Choose TWO and describe:

- one success  
- one failure

## 7. Listening – Behaviour and Addiction

🔗 **Listening:**  
[Internet Addiction and Behaviour Change](https://youtu.be/YuT_RAugJu0?si=LAGRI7Q3b5dKQNFS)

---

### Part 1 – General Understanding

1. What problem is described?  
2. What solution is presented?  

---

### Part 2 – Key Details

1. How many hours does Kyle spend online?  
2. What changes happened in his life?  
3. What is the purpose of the camp?  
4. How does the camp try to help participants?  

---

### Part 3 – Deeper Understanding

1. Why is internet addiction a serious issue?  
2. What role does human interaction play?  
3. Is the camp a complete solution? Explain.  

---

### Part 4 – Interpretation

1. What does this situation show about habits?  
2. What makes change difficult in this case?  
3. What helps people change behaviour?

## 8. Strategy Evaluation

You want to reduce screen time.

Choose ONE strategy:

- remove access completely  
- limit usage gradually  
- replace it with another activity  

---

### Task

👉 Evaluate:
- effectiveness  
- difficulty  
- long-term impact  

---

## 9. Critical Thinking

Read:

👉 “People don’t change unless they are forced to.”

---

### Task

1. Do you agree?  
2. Give one example  
3. When is this true / not true?

Discuss:

1. Why are habits so powerful?
2. Can people change without external help?
3. What is the biggest obstacle to change?

- 2 phrasal verbs  
- 1 idiom
