# Lesson 48 – Cognitive Biases

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- recognise common thinking errors  
- explain why people make incorrect assumptions  
- analyse how bias affects decisions  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. Do people think logically most of the time?
2. Have you ever made a wrong assumption?
3. Why do people trust their first impression?
4. Is it easy to change your mind?

---

## 2. Bias in Action

Read the situations:

1. Someone believes something because it confirms their opinion.  
2. A person trusts their first impression of someone.  
3. Someone assumes something without enough information.  
4. A person follows what most people think.  

---

### Task

👉 Identify the bias (in your own words)  
👉 Explain why it happens  

---

## 3. Thinking Patterns

Look at the behaviours:

- trusting first impressions  
- following the majority  
- ignoring information that doesn’t fit your belief  
- making quick assumptions  

---

### Task

👉 Match each behaviour with a consequence  

Example:
→ trusting first impressions → misunderstanding people  

---

## 4. Language in Action

We describe thinking errors like this:

- People tend to jump to conclusions.  
- We are likely to trust familiar ideas.  
- People often assume things too quickly.  
- We tend to ignore information that doesn’t fit our beliefs.  

---

## 5. Phrasal Verbs – Thinking

👉 used naturally in context

Match meaning:

1. jump to → ______  
2. stick to → ______  
3. go along with → ______  
4. rule out → ______  

Meanings:

a) reject an idea  
b) accept something without questioning  
c) make a quick judgement  
d) continue believing something  

---

### Task

Use them in context:

👉 Describe situations where people:

- accept something too easily  
- reject an idea  
- follow others  
- keep the same belief  

---

## 6. Idioms – Thinking Errors

Match:

1. jump to conclusions  
2. take something at face value  
3. follow the crowd  
4. have second thoughts  
5. see things differently  

Meanings:

a) change your perspective  
b) doubt a decision  
c) accept something without questioning  
d) copy others  
e) decide too quickly  

---

### Task

👉 Choose TWO idioms and describe real situations where they apply  

---

## 7. Case Study

Situation:

👉 A person reads one article and immediately forms a strong opinion.

---

### Task

1. What bias is involved?  
2. What is missing in their thinking?  
3. What should they do differently?  

---

## 8. Decision Task

Situation:

👉 You hear an opinion you strongly disagree with.

Options:

- reject it immediately  
- consider it carefully  
- ignore it  

---

### Task

👉 Choose and explain:
- why  
- what bias could affect your choice  

---

## 9. Critical Thinking

Read:

👉 “People trust information that feels familiar.”

---

### Task

1. Why is this true?  
2. What problem does it create?  
3. How can people avoid this?  

---

## 10. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. Why do people make wrong assumptions?
2. Is first impression reliable?
3. How can people avoid bias?
4. Why do people follow others?

👉 Try to use:
- 2 phrasal verbs  
- 1 idiom  

---

## 11. Writing Task

Write **180–220 words**.

Topic:

**Why people don’t always think logically**

Include:

- examples  
- reasons  
- consequences  
- your opinion
