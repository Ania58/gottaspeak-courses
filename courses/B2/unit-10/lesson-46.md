# Lesson 46 – Habits and Behaviour

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- recognise patterns in behaviour  
- describe habits and routines naturally  
- analyse why people act automatically  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. Do you think most of your actions are conscious or automatic?
2. What is one habit you would like to change?
3. Why are habits difficult to break?
4. Are habits always negative?

---

## 2. Behaviour in Action

Read the situations:

1. Someone checks their phone every few minutes without thinking.  
2. A person always buys coffee from the same place every day.  
3. Someone delays important tasks until the last moment.  
4. A person watches videos even when they don’t really enjoy them.  

---

### Task

For each situation:

👉 What kind of habit is it?  
👉 Why might this behaviour happen?  

---

## 3. Pattern Recognition

Look at the behaviours:

- scrolling social media late at night  
- snacking while watching something  
- checking notifications constantly  
- avoiding difficult tasks  

---

### Task

Group them into:

👉 helpful habits  
👉 neutral habits  
👉 harmful habits  

Explain your choices  

---

## 4. Language in Context

We describe habits naturally:

- I tend to check my phone without realising.  
- I often end up wasting time online.  
- I’m used to doing this every day.  
- I have a habit of overthinking things.  

---

### Task

Describe your own behaviour:

👉 Choose 3:

- something you do automatically  
- something you do too often  
- something you would like to change  

---

## 5. Trigger → Behaviour → Result

Read the example:

👉 Trigger: feeling bored  
👉 Behaviour: checking phone  
👉 Result: wasting time  

---

### Task

Analyse the patterns:

1. Trigger: stress → Behaviour: __________ → Result: __________  
2. Trigger: free time → Behaviour: __________ → Result: __________  
3. Trigger: difficulty → Behaviour: __________ → Result: __________  

👉 Then answer:
What part is easiest to change?

---

## 6. Decision Task

Situation:

👉 You realise you spend too much time online.

Choose ONE strategy:

- limit screen time  
- remove certain apps  
- change your routine  

---

### Task

👉 Explain:
- why you chose it  
- what problem it solves  
- what could go wrong  

---

## 7. Real-life Scenario

Situation:

👉 You want to build a better habit (e.g. exercise, studying, reading).

---

### Task

Create a simple plan:

- when  
- how often  
- what might stop you  
- how you will deal with it  

---

## 8. Critical Thinking

Read the statement:

👉 “People don’t lack motivation, they lack good habits.”

---

### Task

1. Do you agree or disagree?  
2. Give one example  
3. What is the limitation of this idea?  

---

## 9. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. Why do people repeat bad habits?
2. Is it possible to change behaviour easily?
3. What makes habits powerful?
4. What is more important: motivation or routine?

👉 Focus:
- examples  
- reasoning  
- real-life experience  

---

## 10. Writing Task

Write **180–220 words**.

Topic:

**Why habits are difficult to change**

Include:

- reasons  
- examples  
- consequences  
- your opinion
