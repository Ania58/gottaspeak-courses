# Lesson 49 – Motivation and Self-Control

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- explain why motivation changes  
- describe lack of self-control  
- analyse behaviour related to procrastination  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. What motivates people to take action?
2. Why do people lose motivation?
3. Is discipline more important than motivation?
4. Do you find it easy to stay focused?

---

## 2. Real Situations

Read the situations:

1. Someone plans to study but ends up watching videos.  
2. A person starts a project but doesn’t finish it.  
3. Someone delays an important task again and again.  
4. A person wants to change habits but gives up quickly.  

---

### Task

👉 For each situation:

- what is the problem?  
- what causes it?  

---

## 3. Motivation vs Reality

Look at the statements:

- “I’ll do it later.”  
- “I don’t feel like it today.”  
- “I’ll start tomorrow.”  
- “Just one more video.”  

---

### Task

👉 What do these say about:
- motivation  
- self-control  

👉 Which one is the most dangerous? Why?

---

## 4. Language in Action

We describe behaviour like this:

- I struggle to stay focused.  
- I fail to finish what I start.  
- I manage to complete tasks when I plan properly.  
- I try to avoid distractions.  

---

## 5. Phrasal Verbs – Effort & Focus

Match:

1. put off  
2. carry on  
3. give up  
4. stick to  

Meanings:

a) stop trying  
b) continue  
c) delay  
d) follow a plan  

---

### Task

👉 Describe situations where people:

- delay tasks  
- stop trying  
- continue despite difficulty  
- follow a routine  

---

## 6. Idioms – Motivation

Match:

1. get things done  
2. lose track of time  
3. push yourself  
4. lack motivation  
5. stay on track  

Meanings:

a) remain focused  
b) have no energy to act  
c) complete tasks  
d) not notice time passing  
e) force yourself to act  

---

### Task

👉 Choose TWO idioms and describe real-life situations  

---

## 7. Case Study

Situation:

👉 Someone wants to improve their life but keeps repeating the same habits.

---

### Task

1. What is the main problem?  
2. What is stopping them?  
3. What would help them change?  

---

## 8. Decision Task

Situation:

👉 You need to finish an important task but feel unmotivated.

Options:

- wait until you feel motivated  
- force yourself to start  
- do something easier first  

---

### Task

👉 Choose and explain:
- why  
- risks  
- what usually happens  

---

## 9. Critical Thinking

Read:

👉 “Motivation is unreliable. Discipline is more important.”

---

### Task

1. Do you agree?  
2. Give one example  
3. What is the limitation of this idea?  

---

## 10. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. Why do people procrastinate?
2. How can people stay motivated?
3. Is discipline something you can learn?
4. What works better: pressure or routine?

👉 Try to use:
- 2 phrasal verbs  
- 1 idiom  

---

## 11. Writing Task

Write **180–220 words**.

Topic:

**Why people fail to stay motivated**

Include:

- reasons  
- examples  
- consequences  
- possible solutions
