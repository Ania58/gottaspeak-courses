# Lesson 47 – Decision-Making

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- analyse how decisions are made  
- explain irrational or impulsive choices  
- justify decisions in different situations  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. Do you usually think carefully before making decisions?
2. What kind of decisions do people regret most?
3. Is it better to decide quickly or slowly?
4. Do emotions help or harm decision-making?

---

## 2. Real Decisions

Read the situations:

1. Someone buys something expensive without thinking.  
2. A person avoids making a decision for a long time.  
3. Someone follows a friend’s advice instead of their own opinion.  
4. A person changes their mind at the last moment.  

---

### Task

For each situation:

👉 What type of decision is it?
- impulsive  
- influenced  
- delayed  
- uncertain  

👉 What might be the reason?

---

## 3. What Influences Decisions?

Look at the factors:

- emotions  
- pressure from others  
- lack of information  
- habits  
- fear of failure  

---

### Task

For each factor:

👉 Give one real example  
👉 Explain how it affects decisions  

---

## 4. Decision Paths

Read the scenario:

👉 You are about to accept a job offer.

---

### Task

Describe two different outcomes:

1. You decide quickly without thinking  
2. You take time to analyse everything  

👉 What happens in each case?

---

## 5. Language in Action

We describe decisions like this:

- I ended up choosing the easier option.  
- I hesitated to make a decision.  
- I went with my first instinct.  
- I was about to change my mind.  
- I finally made up my mind.  

---

### Task

Describe a real situation when:

👉 you made a quick decision  
👉 you delayed a decision  
👉 you changed your mind  

---

## 6. Decision Under Pressure

Situation:

👉 You must decide quickly with limited information.

Options:

- act immediately  
- wait for more information  
- ask someone else  

---

### Task

👉 Choose one and explain:
- why  
- risks  
- possible consequences  

---

## 7. Critical Thinking

Read the statement:

👉 “People don’t make bad decisions, they just don’t have enough information.”

---

### Task

1. Do you agree?  
2. Give a counterargument  
3. When is this true / not true?  

---

## 8. Real-life Scenario

Situation:

👉 You regret a decision you made in the past.

---

### Task

Explain:

- what happened  
- what influenced your decision  
- what you would do differently now  

---

## 9. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. Why do people make impulsive decisions?
2. Is overthinking a problem?
3. Should we trust our instincts?
4. What helps people make better decisions?

👉 Focus:
- examples  
- reasoning  
- reflection  

---

## 10. Writing Task

Write **180–220 words**.

Topic:

**Why people make bad decisions**

Include:

- reasons  
- examples  
- consequences  
- how to improve decision-making
