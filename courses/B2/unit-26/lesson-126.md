## 3. Grammar – Participle Clauses

Look at the difference:

- Animals that are taken from the wild often do not survive.
- Animals taken from the wild often do not survive.

- Products that are sold illegally are difficult to trace.
- Products sold illegally are difficult to trace.

- People who buy exotic pets may not realise the consequences.
- People buying exotic pets may not realise the consequences.

We use participle clauses to make sentences more compact and natural.

### Common patterns

- animals taken from the wild
- products sold on the black market
- people buying illegal goods
- species facing extinction
- authorities trying to stop the trade

## 9. Reading

Read the text.

Illegal wildlife trade has become one of the most profitable forms of environmental crime in the world. Animals taken from the wild are sold for food, medicine, decoration or as exotic pets. In many cases, buyers are not fully aware of the damage caused by the trade they support.

Species facing extinction are often the most valuable on the black market. Products made from protected animals can be sold at extremely high prices, especially when they are associated with status, tradition or supposed health benefits. This creates strong demand, encouraging traffickers to expand their operations.

Animals transported across borders are frequently kept in terrible conditions. Many do not survive the journey. Those reaching markets alive may carry disease, suffer severe stress or die soon after being sold. What appears to be a luxury product is often the final stage of a brutal process.

Authorities trying to stop the trade face major challenges. Networks operating across several countries are hard to trace, and enforcement is often inconsistent. In some areas, corrupt officials or weak legal systems allow illegal goods to slip through the cracks.

Reducing demand remains one of the most important goals. As long as people continue buying rare animals and wildlife products, suppliers will continue finding ways to profit from them.
