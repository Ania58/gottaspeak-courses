# Revision – Unit 26

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- analyse how illegal trade operates  
- understand enforcement challenges  
- describe systems and consequences precisely  
- evaluate real-world problems  

---

## 1. Vocabulary in Context

Complete naturally:

1. Illegal wildlife trade is driven by strong __________  
2. Weak __________ allows traffickers to continue operating  
3. Animals are transported through hidden __________  
4. Many species are taken from the __________  
5. The entire system operates through complex __________  

---

## 2. Structural Precision

Rewrite using more advanced structures:

1. Animals are taken from the wild. They are sold illegally.  
2. Networks operate across borders. They are difficult to control.  
3. Products are sold on the market. They are hard to trace.  
4. People buy endangered animals. They support illegal trade.  
5. Authorities try to stop the trade. They face many challenges.  

---

## 3. Emphasis

Rewrite using inversion:

1. Authorities rarely catch the people behind the trade.  
2. This problem is not only illegal, it is dangerous.  
3. People should never ignore this issue.  
4. We have never seen such a large trade before.  
5. Governments react only when the situation becomes serious.  

---

## 4. Noun Phrase Development

Expand:

1. illegal trade  
2. global network  
3. weak law  
4. high demand  
5. environmental damage  

Make each more detailed and precise.
