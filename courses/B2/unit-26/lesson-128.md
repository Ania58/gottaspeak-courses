## 3. Grammar – Noun Phrases

Compare:

- The trade is growing quickly.  
→ the rapid growth of the trade  

- People demand exotic animals.  
→ growing demand for exotic animals  

- Authorities control the system poorly.  
→ weak control of the system  

---

### Patterns:

✔ adjective + noun  
- illegal trade  
- global demand  

✔ noun + noun  
- wildlife trade  
- market system  

✔ noun + of  
- growth of demand  
- impact of trade  

✔ noun + for  
- demand for products  
- need for regulation

## 9. Reading

Read the text.

The illegal wildlife trade is driven by a complex system of global demand and weak enforcement. The rapid growth of international markets has made it easier for traffickers to reach buyers across borders.

One of the main factors behind this trade is the increasing demand for rare and exotic products. The desire for status, tradition and profit has created a powerful market for items that are often sourced illegally.

The lack of effective regulation allows these systems to continue operating. In many cases, the failure of local enforcement and the involvement of organised networks make the situation even more difficult to control.

The environmental impact of this trade is significant. The loss of endangered species and the destruction of ecosystems are long-term consequences that are difficult to reverse.
