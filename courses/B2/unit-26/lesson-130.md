## 3. Grammar – Relative Clauses

### Defining

- The networks that operate globally are hard to stop  
- The people who buy these products support the trade  

---

### Non-defining

- These networks, which operate globally, are hard to stop  
- The products, which are sold illegally, are difficult to trace  

---

### Reduction

- Networks operating globally are hard to stop  
- Products sold illegally are difficult to trace  
- People involved in the trade often avoid detection

## 9. Reading

Read the text.

Illegal trade networks are complex systems that operate across multiple regions. The people involved in these operations often include suppliers, distributors and buyers, each playing a specific role.

Products sold on the black market move through hidden routes connecting different countries. These routes, which are constantly changing, make tracking the trade extremely difficult.

Animals taken from the wild are often transported under poor conditions. Many do not survive the journey, while those that do may suffer long-term health problems.

Authorities trying to control these networks face significant challenges. Systems designed to monitor trade are often limited, allowing illegal goods to pass through unnoticed.

Understanding how these networks function is essential for developing effective solutions.
