# Lesson 111 – Social Events and Expectations

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- describe what is expected in social situations  
- contrast expectations with reality  
- express experiences in a natural way

❌ People talk

✔ The idea is that everyone enjoys themselves  
✔ In reality, people often feel uncomfortable  
✔ What actually happens is…  

---

## 4. Precision Task 

Choose the BEST option:

1. At most social events, you are __________ to interact with others.  
   (expected / supposed / required)

2. The __________ is that everyone has a good time.  
   (idea / expectation / assumption)

3. In __________, many people feel uncomfortable.  
   (reality / fact / truth)

4. Conversations can feel __________ rather than natural.  
   (forced / artificial / unnatural)

5. People often __________ along with the situation.  
   (go / follow / move)

## 9. Reading

Read the text:

👉 *Social events are often seen as enjoyable occasions where people relax and connect with others. However, there is usually a difference between expectation and reality.*

*People are expected to engage in conversation, show interest and behave politely. In reality, many individuals feel uncomfortable, especially when they do not know others well.*

*Conversations may feel forced, and the atmosphere can become awkward. Despite this, most people go along with the situation to avoid appearing rude.*

*Understanding this difference can help people feel less pressure and manage social situations more naturally.*
