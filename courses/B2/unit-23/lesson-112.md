# Lesson 112 – Awkward Situations

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- describe awkward situations  
- react naturally in conversation  
- express uncertainty and discomfort

## 3. Language Focus – Natural Reactions

👉 instead of:
❌ I don’t know what to say  

👉 use:

✔ That was a bit awkward  
✔ I didn’t know how to respond  
✔ I just smiled and said nothing  
✔ I wasn’t sure what to do  
✔ I tried to change the subject

## 4. Precision Task 

Choose the BEST option:

1. I didn’t know how to __________ to what he said.  
   (react / respond / reply)

2. There was a moment of complete __________  
   (silence / pause / stillness)

3. I tried to __________ the situation by making a joke.  
   (handle / manage / ease)

4. It became clear that something was __________  
   (wrong / off / strange)

5. I felt completely __________ in that situation.  
   (uncomfortable / uneasy / awkward)

## 9. Reading

Read the text:

👉 *Awkward situations are a natural part of social interaction. They often occur when expectations are unclear or when people do not know how to react.*

*One common example is awkward silence, where no one knows what to say. In these moments, people may try to ease the situation by making a joke or changing the subject.*

*Another cause of discomfort is misunderstanding. A comment that is intended as harmless may be interpreted differently, creating tension.*

*Learning how to react naturally in these situations can make social interactions less stressful and more manageable.*
