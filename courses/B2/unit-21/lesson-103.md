# Lesson 103 – Privacy vs Safety

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- express balanced arguments  
- compare opposing viewpoints  
- structure opinions clearly and logically  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. Is privacy more important than safety?
2. Should governments monitor citizens?
3. Where should limits be set?
4. Would you accept less privacy for more security?

## 3. Language Focus – Structuring Arguments

👉 instead of random opinions:

✔ One of the main arguments in favour of…  
✔ A common concern is that…  
✔ This raises the question of whether…  
✔ Another point to consider is…  
✔ From a different perspective…  

---

## 4. Precision Task 

Choose the BEST option:

1. One of the main __________ is the protection of personal data.  
   (issues / concerns / problems)

2. This __________ the question of how much control is acceptable.  
   (raises / lifts / brings)

3. A common __________ is that surveillance may go too far.  
   (view / concern / thought)

4. From a different __________, security measures are necessary.  
   (side / perspective / direction)

5. Another point to __________ is the role of technology.  
   (consider / think / reflect)

## 6. Sentence Transformation (Stronger → More Balanced)

1. Surveillance is bad.  
2. Governments should control everything.  
3. Privacy is more important than safety.  
4. People don’t like being monitored.  
5. Technology creates problems.

## 9. Reading

Read the text:

👉 *The debate between privacy and security has become increasingly important in modern society. Governments argue that surveillance measures are necessary to protect public safety.*

*However, many people express concerns about how personal data is collected and used. The possibility of misuse raises serious questions about civil rights and individual freedom.*

*From one perspective, monitoring systems help prevent crime and improve safety. From another, they may lead to excessive control and a loss of privacy.*

*Finding a balance between these two priorities remains a major challenge for policymakers around the world.*

---

## 10. Reading Comprehension

Answer:

1. Why do governments support surveillance?  
2. What concerns do people have?  
3. What are the two perspectives?  
4. What is the main challenge?
