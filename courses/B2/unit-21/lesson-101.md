## 3. Language Focus – Relative Clauses (Real Use)

👉 Not theory — clarity:

✔ Passengers who fail to follow the rules may be delayed  
✔ Items that are considered dangerous are removed  
✔ The scanner, which uses advanced technology, detects threats  

---

👉 difference:

- defining → essential info  
- non-defining → extra info  

---

## 4. Precision Task 

Choose the BEST option:

1. Passengers __________ do not follow instructions may face delays.  
   (who / which / whose)

2. Items __________ are considered dangerous will be removed.  
   (who / that / where)

3. The scanner, __________ detects hidden objects, is highly effective.  
   (that / which / where)

4. Staff __________ responsibility is to ensure safety must follow strict rules.  
   (who / whose / which)

5. The area __________ passengers are checked can become crowded.  
   (which / where / that)

---

## 5. Sentence Building (Clarity First)

Combine the sentences using relative clauses:

1. Passengers are delayed. They do not follow instructions.  
2. Some items are removed. They are considered dangerous.  
3. The scanner is new. It improves detection.  
4. Staff work under pressure. They check passengers.  
5. This is the area. Security checks take place here.  

---

## 6. Rewrite (Basic → Precise)

Rewrite to sound more natural and precise:

1. People who don’t follow rules have problems.  
2. They check bags that have dangerous things.  
3. The machine is very advanced and detects objects.  
4. Workers have an important job and must be careful.  
5. This is the place where they check passengers.

## 9. Reading

Read the text:

👉 *Airport security procedures are designed to ensure passenger safety while maintaining efficiency. The screening process, which involves multiple stages, aims to detect any potential threats.*

*Passengers who do not comply with regulations may experience delays. Items that are considered dangerous are removed during inspection, and additional checks may be carried out if necessary.*

*Modern technology plays a key role in this process. Devices such as body scanners, which use advanced imaging systems, can identify objects that would otherwise be difficult to detect.*

*Despite these measures, challenges remain. High passenger numbers and time pressure can affect the overall efficiency of the system.*
