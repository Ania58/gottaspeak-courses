# Lesson 104 – The Future of Security

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- talk about future developments without basic future tenses  
- describe trends and expectations  
- analyse how technology may change security

## 3. Language Focus – Prediction

❌ Security will change  

👉 use:

✔ may lead to new risks  
✔ is unlikely to replace humans completely

## 4. Precision Task 

Choose the BEST option:

1. Security systems are __________ to become more automated.  
   (likely / possible / expected)

2. AI systems __________ reduce human error in many cases.  
   (tend to / aim to / try to)

3. New technologies may __________ privacy concerns.  
   (raise / increase / create)

4. Automation is __________ to replace all human roles.  
   (unlikely / unable / impossible)

5. These systems are __________ to improve efficiency.  
   (designed / intended / planned)

## 9. Reading

Read the text:

👉 *Security systems are becoming increasingly sophisticated, driven by rapid technological development. Advanced screening methods are expected to improve both speed and accuracy.*

*Artificial intelligence plays a central role in this transformation. Systems designed to analyse large amounts of data can identify patterns that may not be visible to human operators.*

*However, these developments also raise important concerns. The use of biometric data and digital identity systems may lead to privacy issues if not properly regulated.*

*While automation tends to reduce human error, it is unlikely to eliminate the need for human oversight completely. A balanced approach remains essential.*
