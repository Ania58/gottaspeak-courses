## 3. Language Focus – Reduced Clauses 

👉 instead of:

❌ People who travel through airports are monitored  
✔ People travelling through airports are monitored  

---

❌ Data that is collected from passengers is analysed  
✔ Data collected from passengers is analysed  

---

👉 forms:

- who are → -ing  
- that is → past participle  

---

## 4. Precision Task

Choose the BEST option:

1. Passengers __________ through security are subject to additional checks.  
   (passing / passed / having passed)

2. Data __________ from surveillance systems is analysed automatically.  
   (collecting / collected / having collected)

3. People __________ suspicious behaviour may be stopped.  
   (showing / shown / being shown)

4. Devices __________ to detect threats are constantly updated.  
   (used / using / having used)

5. Footage __________ by cameras can be reviewed later.  
   (recording / recorded / having recorded)

---

## 5. Sentence Transformation (Full → Reduced)

Rewrite using reduced clauses:

1. People who are travelling through airports are monitored.  
2. Data that is collected from passengers is stored.  
3. Cameras that are installed in public areas record activity.  
4. Systems that are used for detection are advanced.  
5. Passengers who are carrying restricted items are stopped.  

---

## 6. Sentence Building (Precision)

Combine naturally:

1. Surveillance systems monitor people. They move through the airport.  
2. Cameras record footage. It is used later for analysis.  
3. Authorities track passengers. They show unusual behaviour.  
4. Data is collected. It helps improve security.  
5. Systems detect threats. They operate automatically.  

👉 Use reduced clauses

## 9. Reading

Read the text:

👉 *Modern surveillance systems play a crucial role in maintaining security. Data collected from various sources is analysed to detect potential threats.*

*Passengers moving through controlled areas are often monitored using advanced technologies such as facial recognition. These systems, designed to operate continuously, can identify unusual behaviour patterns.*

*However, the widespread use of surveillance raises important questions about privacy. Information stored by authorities may be used for security purposes, but it can also lead to concerns about data misuse.*

*Balancing safety and privacy remains one of the key challenges in modern security systems.*
