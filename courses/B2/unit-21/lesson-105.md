# Lesson 105 – Human vs Technology

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- evaluate systems and decisions  
- compare human judgement and technology  
- express nuanced opinions

## 3. Language Focus – Evaluation (Real Use)

👉 instead of:
❌ Technology is better  

👉 use:

✔ tends to be more reliable in routine situations  
✔ is not always effective in complex cases  
✔ can perform tasks more efficiently  
✔ may lack human judgement  
✔ is often more consistent but less flexible  

---

## 4. Precision Task 

Choose the BEST option:

1. Technology tends to be more __________ in repetitive tasks.  
   (reliable / stable / predictable)

2. Human judgement is __________ in uncertain situations.  
   (essential / important / necessary)

3. Automated systems may __________ context or nuance.  
   (miss / lose / ignore)

4. Technology is often more __________ than humans.  
   (consistent / stable / controlled)

5. Human decision-making can be __________ by emotion.  
   (affected / influenced / changed)

## 9. Reading

Read the text:

👉 *The use of technology in security systems has increased significantly in recent years. Automated systems are capable of processing large amounts of data quickly and consistently.*

*However, technology is not always effective in situations that require judgement or interpretation. Human operators are better equipped to handle unexpected scenarios and make decisions based on context.*

*One of the main advantages of automation is efficiency. Systems can perform routine checks faster and more accurately than humans. On the other hand, they may lack flexibility and fail to adapt to unusual situations.*

*As a result, many experts argue that the most effective approach combines both human and technological elements.*
