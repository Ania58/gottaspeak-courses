# Lesson 109 – Costs and Decisions

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- evaluate different housing options  
- express trade-offs clearly  
- justify decisions using precise language  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. What matters more: price or comfort?
2. Would you pay more for a better location?
3. Is cheap accommodation always a bad idea?
4. What would you sacrifice to save money?

## 3. Language Focus – Decision Language

👉 instead of:
❌ It’s good / bad  

👉 use:

✔ The main advantage is…  
✔ The downside is…  
✔ One thing to consider is…  
✔ It depends on priorities such as…  
✔ This option may be better if…  

---

## 4. Precision Task 

Choose the BEST option:

1. The rent is relatively __________ considering the location.  
   (reasonable / affordable / acceptable)

2. One major __________ is the lack of space.  
   (issue / disadvantage / problem)

3. This option offers better __________ for money.  
   (value / benefit / quality)

4. The main __________ is that it is far from campus.  
   (drawback / weakness / limitation)

5. Students often need to __________ between cost and comfort.  
   (decide / choose / balance)

## 9. Reading

Read the text:

👉 *Choosing accommodation often involves balancing several factors, including cost, location and comfort. While some students prioritise affordability, others are willing to pay more for convenience or better living conditions.*

*Lower-cost options may seem attractive at first, but they can involve hidden costs such as long commuting times or poor facilities. On the other hand, more expensive accommodation often offers greater comfort and reliability.*

*Ultimately, the best decision depends on individual priorities. Students must weigh the advantages and disadvantages of each option before making a choice.*
