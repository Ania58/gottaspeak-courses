# Lesson 108 – Problems with Accommodation

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- describe problems precisely  
- explain situations clearly  
- express complaints in a natural, realistic way  

---

Discuss:

2. What is worse: noisy neighbours or messy flatmates?

## 3. Language Focus – Describing Problems

👉 instead of:
❌ It is bad  

👉 use:

✔ One of the main issues is…  
✔ The problem seems to be…  
✔ What makes it worse is…  
✔ The situation becomes difficult when…  

---

## 4. Precision Task 

Choose the BEST option:

1. One of the main issues is the __________ of privacy.  
   (absence / lack / loss)

2. The situation becomes __________ when flatmates are unreliable.  
   (worse / more difficult / challenging)

3. The problem seems to __________ poor communication.  
   (come from / result from / be caused by)

4. What makes it worse is the __________ noise at night.  
   (constant / continuous / regular)

5. The flat is in __________ condition and needs repairs.  
   (poor / bad / weak)

## 9. Reading

Read the text:

👉 *Living in shared accommodation can be both convenient and challenging. One of the most common issues is the lack of privacy, especially when several people share a limited space.*

*Another frequent problem is noise. Flatmates who have different schedules may unintentionally disturb others, making it difficult to rest or study.*

*Maintenance issues can also cause frustration. Delays in repairs or poor communication with landlords often make the situation worse.*

*As a result, many students have to learn how to manage conflict, communicate effectively and adapt to different living conditions.*

---
