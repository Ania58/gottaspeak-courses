# Lesson 107 – Renting and Contracts

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- understand key elements of rental agreements  
- describe obligations and conditions  
- use semi-formal language for real-life situations

## 3. Language Focus – Obligation (Real English)

👉 instead of:
❌ You must pay rent  

👉 use:

✔ Tenants are required to pay rent on time  
✔ The contract states that…  
✔ It is the tenant’s responsibility to…  
✔ The landlord is expected to…  

---

## 4. Precision Task 

Choose the BEST option:

1. Tenants are __________ to pay rent on a monthly basis.  
   (expected / required / supposed)

2. The contract __________ that utilities are not included.  
   (says / states / explains)

3. It is the tenant’s __________ to maintain the property.  
   (duty / responsibility / role)

4. The landlord is __________ to carry out necessary repairs.  
   (required / responsible / expected)

5. A deposit is usually __________ before moving in.  
   (paid / given / provided)

## 9. Reading

Read the text:

👉 *Renting accommodation involves a number of legal and practical considerations. A tenancy agreement typically outlines the responsibilities of both the tenant and the landlord.*

*Tenants are required to pay rent regularly and maintain the property in good condition. At the same time, landlords are responsible for carrying out repairs and ensuring that the property meets basic living standards.*

*One of the most important aspects of renting is understanding the contract. Failure to follow its terms may result in financial or legal consequences.*

*For this reason, it is essential for tenants to review all conditions carefully before signing an agreement.*
