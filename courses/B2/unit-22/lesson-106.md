# Lesson 106 – Types of Accommodation

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- describe different types of accommodation precisely  
- distinguish between similar options  
- define living situations clearly

## 3. Language Focus – Defining Precisely

👉 instead of:
❌ It’s a flat  

👉 use:

✔ It’s a type of accommodation where…  
✔ It’s a place where students…  
✔ It refers to a situation in which…  

---

## 4. Precision Task 

Choose the BEST option:

1. A studio apartment is a space __________ everything is in one room.  
   (where / which / that)

2. A shared flat is a place __________ people live together.  
   (that / where / which)

3. University housing refers to accommodation __________ provided by the institution.  
   (which is / that / where)

4. A rented room is a situation __________ you live in someone else’s home.  
   (where / that / which)

5. A short-term rental is an option __________ does not require a long contract.  
   (where / what / which)

## 9. Reading

Read the text:

👉 *Students today have a wide range of accommodation options available to them. Some choose to live in university housing, which is often located on campus and offers a structured environment.*

*Others prefer shared flats, where they can split costs and live more independently. This option allows for greater flexibility but may also involve challenges related to shared responsibilities.*

*Studio apartments provide complete privacy, as they are designed for one person. However, they are typically more expensive and require full financial responsibility.*

*Each type of accommodation offers different advantages depending on the student’s priorities, lifestyle and budget.*
