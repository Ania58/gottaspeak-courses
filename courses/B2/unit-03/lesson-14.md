## 4. Grammar – Hedging Language

We use hedging to sound more careful and less absolute.

---

### 🔹 Common structures

- People **tend to** believe what they see online.  
- It **seems that** the information is incorrect.  
- This **appears to be** a reliable source.  
- It is **likely that** people will trust this.  
- It **could be argued that** social media is harmful.  

---

### 🔹 Why use hedging?

👉 to sound more natural  
👉 to avoid overgeneralising  
👉 to express opinions professionally

## 7. Reading – Truth in the Digital Age

Read the text.

In the digital age, information is more accessible than ever before. However, this does not mean that all information is reliable. It appears that many people accept what they read without questioning it.

Social media platforms allow information to spread quickly, but this also increases the risk of misinformation. People tend to trust content that confirms their beliefs, even if it is not supported by evidence.

It could be argued that the problem is not only the availability of false information, but also the way people consume it. Many users do not take the time to verify sources or consider alternative perspectives.

At the same time, there are tools available to help people identify reliable information. With greater awareness, individuals can become more critical and responsible consumers of media.

In the end, understanding how information works is essential in today’s world.
