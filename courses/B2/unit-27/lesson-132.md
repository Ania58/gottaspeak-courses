## 3. Grammar – Contrast Structures

We use these to compare choices:

✔ while  
✔ whereas  
✔ rather than  
✔ instead of  

Examples:

- This version is accurate, while the other sounds more natural  
- One translation focuses on meaning, whereas the other follows the structure  
- The translator chose clarity rather than literal accuracy  
- They adapted the phrase instead of translating it directly

## 9. Reading

Read the text.

Translation is rarely a simple choice between right and wrong. Instead, it often involves deciding what to prioritise: meaning, tone or structure. A sentence may be grammatically correct, while still sounding unnatural or confusing.

Literal translations can preserve structure, whereas adapted versions focus on clarity and natural expression. This creates a constant tension between accuracy and readability.

In many cases, meaning does not transfer directly. Cultural references, humour and emotional tone are especially difficult to reproduce. A phrase that works perfectly in one language may lose its effect completely in another.

Rather than translating word for word, experienced translators focus on how the message will be understood. Instead of copying the original structure, they aim to recreate the intended effect.
