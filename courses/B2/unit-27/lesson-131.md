## 3. Grammar – Cleft Sentences (Advanced Use)

We use cleft sentences to highlight what is important.

- What matters is the meaning, not the words  
- What makes translation difficult is the context  
- It’s the tone that changes everything  
- It’s not the words that are the problem, but the intention

## 9. Reading

Read the text.

Translation is often misunderstood as a simple process of replacing words from one language with words from another. In reality, it is far more complex. What matters is not the individual words, but the meaning they carry within a specific context.

A translator must decide how to convey tone, intention and nuance. These elements are often deeply connected to culture, making them difficult to transfer directly. A sentence that sounds natural in one language may sound strange or even misleading in another.

It is the context that shapes meaning. Without understanding the situation in which something is said, it is easy to misinterpret the message. This is why literal translations often fail to communicate what the speaker actually intended.

In many cases, the goal is not to translate the words themselves, but to recreate the effect they have on the reader. What makes a good translation is the ability to balance accuracy with natural expression.
