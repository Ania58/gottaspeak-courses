## 9. Reading

Read the text.

Evaluating language is not always straightforward. A sentence may be grammatically correct but still sound unnatural or unclear. This is especially true in translation, where multiple versions can be technically accurate.

What matters is how effectively the meaning is communicated. A slightly different word choice can make a sentence far more natural, while a literal translation may fall short in terms of clarity.

In many cases, the best version is not the most accurate in terms of structure, but the one that sounds most natural and appropriate in context. This requires careful judgement and experience.

Understanding these differences allows speakers to make better decisions and produce higher-quality language.
