## 9. Reading

Read the text.

Style plays a crucial role in how language is understood. Two sentences may carry the same basic meaning, but differ significantly in tone and effect. What sounds natural in one context may sound inappropriate in another.

In translation, this becomes even more important. A translator must decide not only what the original text means, but how it should sound. A direct translation may be accurate, but fail to capture the intended tone.

From a stylistic point of view, small changes can have a large impact. A slightly more formal expression may sound more professional, while a more direct one may appear too aggressive.

Understanding how style shapes meaning allows for more effective communication and better translation.
