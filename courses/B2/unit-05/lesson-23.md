## 4. Grammar – Comparing & Contrasting

---

### 🔹 Common structures

- While some people prefer cities, others choose the countryside.  
- Whereas one culture values independence, another focuses on community.  
- In contrast, some countries have stricter rules.  
- Compared to my country, life here is more relaxed.  

---

### Practice

Choose the more appropriate option.

1. __________ some cultures value punctuality, others are more flexible.  
(a) While (b) Because  

2. He prefers quiet places, __________ she enjoys busy cities.  
(a) so (b) whereas  

3. This country is more formal. __________, people here are more relaxed.  
(a) For example (b) In contrast  

4. __________ my hometown, this city is much faster-paced.  
(a) Because (b) Compared to  

5. __________ some people like traditions, others prefer change.  
(a) So (b) While  

6. She values independence, __________ her family focuses on community.  
(a) because (b) whereas

## 7. Reading – Cultural Differences

Read the text.

Travelling allows people to experience different cultures and ways of life. While some differences are easy to notice, others take time to understand.

For example, in some countries people are very direct in communication, whereas in others indirect communication is preferred. This can sometimes lead to misunderstandings.

Another difference is attitudes towards time. In certain cultures, punctuality is extremely important, while in others flexibility is more common.

Compared to smaller towns, big cities often have a faster pace of life. People may seem less personal, but this is often due to the environment.

In the end, understanding cultural differences helps people communicate better and avoid conflict.
