# Lesson 70 – Handling Difficult Situations

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- react to difficult situations clearly and calmly  
- choose appropriate strategies  
- explain decisions under pressure  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. How do you usually react under pressure?
2. What makes a situation difficult?
3. Is it better to react quickly or take time?
4. Do people make worse decisions under stress?

---

## 2. Types of Difficult Situations

Read:

1. Something goes wrong unexpectedly  
2. Someone reacts emotionally  
3. A plan fails at the last moment  
4. There is a misunderstanding  

---

### Task

👉 For each:

- what is the main challenge?  
- what could make it worse?  

---

## 3. Useful Strategies

We often respond like this:

- The best way to deal with this would be…  
- I’d rather avoid making it worse.  
- We need to find a way to fix this.  
- That might cause more problems.  

---

### Task

👉 Complete:

1. The best way to deal with this would be __________.  
2. I’d rather avoid __________.  
3. We need to find a way to __________.  
4. That might make things __________.  

---

## 4. Reacting in Real Time

Compare:

❌ This is bad.  
✔ This isn’t ideal, but we can still work around it.  

❌ This is a disaster.  
✔ It’s not great, but it’s manageable.  

---

### Task

👉 Improve:

1. This is terrible.  
2. This won’t work.  
3. This is a big problem.  
4. Everything is wrong.  

(make them sound controlled and realistic)

---

## 5. Phrasal Verbs – Difficult Situations

Match:

1. deal with  
2. sort out  
3. work around  
4. calm down  

Meanings:

a) fix a situation  
b) handle  
c) find an alternative solution  
d) become less emotional  

---

### Task

👉 Create sentences about:

- handling a problem  
- fixing something  
- finding another solution  
- staying calm  

---

## 6. Idioms – Difficult Situations

Match:

1. under pressure  
2. keep your cool  
3. not the end of the world  
4. think on your feet  

Meanings:

a) react quickly  
b) stay calm  
c) not a serious problem  
d) in a stressful situation  

---

### Task

👉 Use TWO idioms in short situations  

---

## 7. Real-life Scenario

Situation:

👉 Something important goes wrong at the last moment.

---

### Task

👉 Explain:

- your immediate reaction  
- what you say  
- what you do next  

(use B2 language)

---

## 8. Decision Under Pressure

Situation:

👉 You have very little time to decide what to do.

---

### Task

👉 Explain:

- what you consider first  
- what you avoid  
- your final decision  

---

## 9. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. Why do people panic in difficult situations?
2. What helps people stay calm?
3. Is quick thinking better than careful thinking?
4. Can people learn to handle pressure?

👉 Try to use:
- 2 phrasal verbs  
- 1 idiom  

---

## 10. Writing Task

Write **180–220 words**.

Topic:

**How to deal with difficult situations effectively**

Include:

- types of situations  
- strategies  
- challenges  
- your opinion
