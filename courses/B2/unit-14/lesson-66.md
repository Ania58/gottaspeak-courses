# Lesson 66 – Framing Problems Clearly

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- describe problems in a more natural, detailed way  
- explain ongoing issues  
- avoid overly simple language  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. What kind of problems do people deal with every day?
2. Is it better to describe a problem briefly or in detail?
3. Why do some people struggle to explain problems clearly?
4. Do misunderstandings often come from poor explanations?

---

## 2. Basic vs Natural Language

Compare:

❌ I have a problem with my computer.  
✔ I’ve been having issues with my computer lately.  

❌ It doesn’t work.  
✔ It keeps freezing and slowing everything down.  

---

### Task

👉 Improve the sentences:

1. I have a problem with my phone.  
2. My internet is bad.  
3. I can’t find my keys.  
4. My work is stressful.  

---

## 3. Describing Ongoing Problems

Look at the patterns:

- I’ve been dealing with…  
- I keep running into…  
- Things haven’t been going as planned…  
- It seems to be getting worse…  

---

### Task

👉 Complete:

1. I’ve been dealing with __________ for a while now.  
2. I keep running into __________ when I try to __________.  
3. Things haven’t been going as planned because __________.  
4. It seems to be getting worse since __________.  

---

## 4. Adding Detail

We often make problems clearer by adding:

- cause  
- effect  
- frequency  

---

Example:
👉 My laptop keeps crashing, especially when I try to open multiple tabs.

---

### Task

👉 Expand:

1. My phone isn’t working.  
2. I feel tired.  
3. My schedule is busy.  

(add: when / why / how often)

---

## 5. Phrasal Verbs – Problems

Match:

1. run into  
2. deal with  
3. sort out  
4. come up with  

Meanings:

a) handle a problem  
b) find a solution  
c) encounter unexpectedly  
d) resolve something  

---

### Task

👉 Create sentences about:

1. encountering a problem  
2. solving a situation  
3. handling something difficult  
4. finding a solution  

---

## 6. Idioms – Problems

Match:

1. a bit of a mess  
2. not the end of the world  
3. easier said than done  
4. a tricky situation  

Meanings:

a) difficult situation  
b) not as serious as it seems  
c) difficult to do  
d) disorganised/problematic  

---

### Task

👉 Use TWO idioms in short situations  

---

## 7. Real-life Scenario

Situation:

👉 Something isn’t working properly (e.g. device, plan, schedule).

---

### Task

👉 Explain the problem clearly:

- what’s happening  
- how often  
- what the impact is  

---

## 8. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. Why do people sometimes explain problems poorly?
2. What makes an explanation clear?
3. Is it better to simplify or explain in detail?
4. How do people react to unclear problems?

👉 Try to use:
- 2 phrasal verbs  
- 1 idiom  

---

## 9. Writing Task

Write **180–220 words**.

Topic:

**Describe a problem you had and how you explained it**

Include:

- situation  
- details  
- impact  
- result
