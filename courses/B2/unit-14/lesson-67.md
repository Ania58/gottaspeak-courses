# Lesson 67 – Giving Nuanced Advice

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- give advice in a natural, less direct way  
- suggest options instead of giving orders  
- adjust tone depending on the situation  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. Do people like receiving advice?
2. When does advice sound too direct?
3. Is it better to be honest or polite when giving advice?
4. Do you usually give or ask for advice?

---

## 2. Direct vs Natural Advice

Compare:

❌ You should talk to him.  
✔ You might want to talk to him directly.  

❌ Don’t do that.  
✔ I wouldn’t do that if I were you.  

---

### Task

👉 Rewrite:

1. You should stop eating junk food.  
2. You should talk to your boss.  
3. You shouldn’t ignore the problem.  
4. You should try something different.  

(use softer, natural B2 language)

---

## 3. Key Structures

We often use:

- You might want to consider…  
- It might be worth…  
- One option would be…  
- I wouldn’t recommend…  
- If I were you, I’d…  

---

### Task

👉 Complete:

1. It might be worth __________.  
2. You might want to consider __________.  
3. One option would be __________.  
4. I wouldn’t recommend __________.  

---

## 4. Giving Options

Instead of one solution, we give choices:

Example:
👉 You could try talking to him, or you might want to give it some time.

---

### Task

👉 Give TWO options for:

1. a stressful situation  
2. a technical problem  
3. a misunderstanding  

---

## 5. Phrasal Verbs – Advice & Solutions

Match:

1. figure out  
2. deal with  
3. sort out  
4. work through  

Meanings:

a) solve step by step  
b) handle something  
c) understand a solution  
d) resolve a situation  

---

### Task

👉 Create sentences about:

- solving a problem  
- understanding a situation  
- handling difficulty  
- fixing something  

---

## 6. Idioms – Advice

Match:

1. think twice  
2. go for it  
3. weigh your options  
4. play it safe  

Meanings:

a) be careful  
b) consider choices  
c) take a risk  
d) reconsider  

---

### Task

👉 Use TWO idioms in short advice situations  

---

## 7. Real-life Scenario

Situation:

👉 A friend is dealing with a difficult situation (work, study, personal).

---

### Task

👉 Give advice:

- suggest at least 2 options  
- avoid direct commands  
- explain your reasoning  

---

## 8. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. Why do people give bad advice?
2. Is it better to give clear or flexible advice?
3. When should you not give advice?
4. Do people follow advice?

👉 Try to use:
- 2 phrasal verbs  
- 1 idiom  

---

## 9. Writing Task

Write **180–220 words**.

Topic:

**Give advice for a difficult situation**

Include:

- description of the problem  
- possible solutions  
- advantages and risks  
- your recommendation
