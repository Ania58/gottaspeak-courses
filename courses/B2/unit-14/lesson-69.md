# Lesson 69 – Evaluating Options and Making Decisions

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- compare different options clearly  
- explain advantages and disadvantages  
- justify decisions  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. Do you usually make decisions quickly or slowly?
2. What makes a decision difficult?
3. Do people always think about consequences?
4. Is it better to choose the safest option?

---

## 2. Comparing Options

Look at the structure:

- On the one hand…, on the other hand…  
- The main advantage is…  
- The main drawback is…  

---

### Task

👉 Complete:

1. On the one hand __________, on the other __________.  
2. The main advantage of this option is __________.  
3. The main drawback is __________.  

---

## 3. Thinking About Consequences

We often explain results like this:

- This would mean…  
- That could lead to…  
- In the long run…  
- The risk is that…  

---

### Task

👉 Complete:

1. If I choose this, it would mean __________.  
2. That could lead to __________.  
3. In the long run, __________.  
4. The risk is that __________.  

---

## 4. Choosing the Best Option

We often say:

- The best option would be…  
- It makes more sense to…  
- I’d rather…  
- I’m leaning towards…  

---

### Task

👉 Complete:

1. I’m leaning towards __________ because __________.  
2. It makes more sense to __________.  
3. The best option would be __________.  

---

## 5. Phrasal Verbs – Decisions

Match:

1. go for  
2. rule out  
3. think over  
4. end up  

Meanings:

a) consider carefully  
b) choose  
c) eliminate  
d) finally result in  

---

### Task

👉 Create sentences about:

- choosing something  
- rejecting an option  
- thinking carefully  
- unexpected results  

---

## 6. Idioms – Decisions

Match:

1. make up your mind  
2. sit on the fence  
3. take a chance  
4. play it safe  

Meanings:

a) choose  
b) not decide  
c) take a risk  
d) choose the safe option  

---

### Task

👉 Use TWO idioms in short situations  

---

## 7. Real-life Scenario

Situation:

👉 You have two options:

- stay in a stable situation  
- try something new but uncertain  

---

### Task

👉 Explain:

- advantages of both  
- risks  
- your final decision  

(use full B2 language)

---

## 8. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. Why do people struggle to make decisions?
2. Is it better to take risks or stay safe?
3. Do people regret their decisions?
4. How important is long-term thinking?

👉 Try to use:
- 2 phrasal verbs  
- 1 idiom  

---

## 9. Writing Task

Write **180–220 words**.

Topic:

**Describe a difficult decision and how you made it**

Include:

- options  
- advantages and disadvantages  
- consequences  
- final choice
