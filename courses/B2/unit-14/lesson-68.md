# Lesson 68 – Complaints and Explaining Issues

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- explain problems clearly and logically  
- structure complaints effectively  
- adjust tone depending on the situation  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. Do you usually complain when something goes wrong?
2. What makes a complaint effective?
3. Is it better to be direct or polite?
4. When do people overreact to problems?

---

## 2. Structure of a Complaint

A clear complaint usually includes:

1. expectation  
2. what actually happened  
3. the problem  

---

Example:
👉 I was expecting the service to be faster, but it took over an hour and no one updated us.

---

### Task

👉 Complete:

1. I was expecting __________, but instead __________.  
2. What happened was __________, which caused __________.  
3. The issue seems to be __________.  

---

## 3. Making It Sound Natural

Compare:

❌ This is bad.  
✔ What’s concerning is that the product stopped working after just one day.  

❌ I don’t like it.  
✔ I’m not entirely satisfied with the result.  

---

### Task

👉 Improve:

1. This is a problem.  
2. I don’t like this service.  
3. It’s not good.  
4. This doesn’t work properly.  

---

## 4. Adding Detail

We often explain:

- cause  
- impact  
- result  

---

Example:
👉 The delivery was late, which meant I missed the event.

---

### Task

👉 Expand:

1. The service was slow.  
2. The product was damaged.  
3. The information was unclear.  

(add: consequence)

---

## 5. Phrasal Verbs – Complaints

Match:

1. point out  
2. bring up  
3. deal with  
4. look into  

Meanings:

a) investigate  
b) mention  
c) handle  
d) highlight  

---

### Task

👉 Create sentences about:

- mentioning a problem  
- investigating something  
- handling a complaint

---

## 6. Idioms – Complaints

Match:

1. make a big deal out of something  
2. not worth the trouble  
3. get something off your chest  
4. a minor issue  

Meanings:

a) express frustration  
b) not serious  
c) overreact  
d) not worth effort  

---

### Task

👉 Use TWO idioms in short complaint situations
