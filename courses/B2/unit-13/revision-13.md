# Revision – Unit 13 (B2)

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- use food-related vocabulary naturally  
- describe food and eating habits  
- make decisions about food safety  
- understand key ideas from a listening  

---

## 1. Warm-up (Discussion)

Discuss:

1. Do you usually check expiration dates?
2. What food do you often throw away?
3. Do you trust your senses (smell, taste)?
4. What is a common mistake people make with food?

---

## 2. Food in Everyday Use

Complete the sentences:

1. I usually eat __________ during the week.  
2. I try to cut down on __________.  
3. I don’t like food that is too __________.  
4. When I cook, I usually __________ the ingredients first.  
5. I avoid eating __________ because __________.

## 9. Listening Tasks

### Part 1 – General Understanding

1. What is the main problem discussed in the video?  
2. Why do people throw away food?  

---

### Part 2 – Key Details

1. What do most expiration dates actually show?  
2. Why are these dates often misleading?  
3. Which foods can last longer than expected?  
4. When is food actually unsafe?  

---

### Part 3 – Practical Understanding

1. What should you use to check if food is still good?  
2. Which foods are more risky?  
3. What do experts suggest to reduce waste?  

---

### Part 4 – Language Focus

Complete:

1. Food can go __________ if it is not stored properly.  
2. Many products are still __________ to eat after the date.  
3. People often __________ food because of confusion.  
4. It’s better to __________ your senses than rely only on labels.
