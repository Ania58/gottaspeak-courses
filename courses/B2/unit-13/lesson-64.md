# Lesson 64 – Eating Out and Ordering Food

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- order food naturally  
- make polite requests  
- deal with problems in a restaurant  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. How often do you eat out?
2. Do you prefer restaurants or takeaways?
3. What do you usually order?
4. Have you ever had a bad experience in a restaurant?

---

## 2. Ordering Food

Match:

1. I’d like  
2. Could I have  
3. I’ll take  
4. Can I get  

---

a) the same  
b) a coffee, please  
c) a table for two  
d) the chicken  

---

### Task

👉 Complete:

1. __________ a glass of water, please.  
2. __________ the pasta.  
3. __________ a table for two.  
4. __________ the same as you.  

---

## 3. Making Requests

Match:

1. Could you  
2. Would you mind  
3. Can we  
4. Is it possible to  

---

a) sit outside?  
b) bring the bill?  
c) split the bill?  
d) changing this?  

---

### Task

👉 Complete:

1. __________ bring us the menu?  
2. __________ change this dish?  
3. __________ sit by the window?  
4. __________ have the bill, please?  

---

## 4. Language in Action

We speak like this in restaurants:

- I’d like to order…  
- Could we have the menu?  
- Can we get the bill?  
- Is everything OK with the food?  

---

### Task

👉 Create short exchanges:

1. ordering food  
2. asking for the bill  
3. asking for something extra  

---

## 5. Phrasal Verbs – Eating Out

Match:

1. eat out  
2. send back  
3. take away  
4. try out  

Meanings:

a) return food  
b) eat at a restaurant  
c) test something new  
d) order food to go  

---

### Task

👉 Describe situations where you:

- go to a restaurant  
- return food  
- order takeaway  
- try a new place  

---

## 6. Idioms – Food & Restaurants

Match:

1. the bill is on me  
2. go Dutch  
3. cost an arm and a leg  
4. worth every penny  

Meanings:

a) share the cost  
b) very expensive  
c) very good value  
d) I will pay  

---

### Task

👉 Choose TWO idioms and create short examples  

---

## 7. Real-life Scenario

Situation:

👉 Your food is cold and not what you ordered.

---

### Task

👉 Say what you would say to the waiter:

- describe the problem  
- ask for a solution  
- stay polite  

---

## 8. Problem Solving

Read:

👉 The service is slow and your order is wrong.

---

### Task

👉 What do you do?

- wait  
- complain  
- leave  

👉 Explain:
- why  
- what is the best approach  

---

## 9. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. What makes a good restaurant experience?
2. Is service or food more important?
3. Do people complain too much in restaurants?
4. Is eating out worth the cost?

👉 Try to use:
- 2 phrasal verbs  
- 1 idiom  

---

## 10. Writing Task

Write **160–200 words**.

Topic:

**A restaurant experience (good or bad)**

Include:

- what happened  
- what you ordered  
- the problem or highlight  
- your opinion
