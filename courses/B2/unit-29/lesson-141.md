## 9. Reading

Read the text.

Identity is not something fixed. It develops over time, shaped by experience, environment and personal choices. People often present different versions of themselves depending on the situation.

In some cases, the way a person expresses themselves can reveal more than their words. Art, for example, can reflect emotions and experiences that are difficult to explain directly.

At the same time, there is often a difference between how people see themselves and how others perceive them. This gap can influence behaviour and self-expression.

What defines identity is not a single factor, but a combination of influences that evolve over time.
