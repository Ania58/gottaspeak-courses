# Revision – Unit 24

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- analyse complex financial situations  
- understand real-world economic issues  
- express causes, consequences and risks  
- evaluate long-term decisions  

---

## 1. Vocabulary Precision 

Choose the BEST option:

1. Many workers are __________ of having insufficient savings.  
   (in danger / at risk / threatened)

2. Wages have __________ over the past decades.  
   (remained / stagnated / slowed)

3. Rising costs have reduced __________ income.  
   (spare / disposable / available)

4. Some people lack the __________ to manage their finances.  
   (awareness / knowledge / ability)

5. Unexpected events can __________ long-term plans.  
   (damage / disrupt / affect)

## 3. Precision in Context (Balanced Options)

Choose the BEST option:

1. Many people struggle __________ increasing living costs.  
   (against / with / under)

2. This often results __________ long-term financial instability.  
   (from / into / in)

3. People are responsible __________ their own savings.  
   (to / for / of)

4. This can lead __________ serious financial problems.  
   (for / to / into)

5. Some individuals depend __________ external support.  
   (of / on / from)


---

## 4. Cause & Effect 

Choose the BEST option:

1. Wages have not increased; __________, saving has become harder.  
   (however / therefore / consequently)

2. Living costs are rising; __________ many people cannot save.  
   (while / so / because)

3. People lack financial knowledge; __________ they often make poor decisions.  
   (therefore / although / despite)

4. Some workers have unstable jobs; __________, they find it difficult to plan long-term.  
   (meanwhile / as a result / however)

5. Medical costs are high; __________ this can quickly reduce savings.  
   (as a result / even though / whereas)
