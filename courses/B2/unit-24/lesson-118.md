# Lesson 118 – Life Choices and Priorities

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- compare different life choices  
- express contrasting opinions  
- explain priorities and trade-offs  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. What is more important: money or happiness?
2. Do people make rational or emotional decisions?
3. What influences life choices the most?
4. Is it better to focus on the present or the future?

---

## 4. Precision Task

Choose the BEST option:

1. __________ some people focus on saving, others prefer to enjoy life now.  
   (Although / Despite / While)

2. The main __________ is between comfort and independence.  
   (contrast / conflict / difference)

3. The challenge __________ balancing present and future needs.  
   (involves / lies in / is)

4. Some decisions require people to __________ short-term benefits.  
   (lose / sacrifice / give up)

5. People often choose __________ their priorities.  
   (depending on / according to / based on)

## 6. Transformation (Basic → Contrast)

Rewrite:

1. Some people save money. Others spend it.  
2. Some jobs are stable. Others are flexible.  
3. Some people plan. Others don’t.  
4. Some choices are easy. Others are difficult.  
5. Some people think about the future. Others don’t.  

👉 combine using contrast

## 9. Reading

Read the text:

👉 *Life decisions often involve balancing different priorities. While some people focus on financial stability, others prioritise flexibility and personal satisfaction.*

*One of the main challenges is deciding what matters most. Choosing a stable career may offer security, but it can limit freedom. On the other hand, a flexible lifestyle may provide more independence, but less financial stability.*

*Ultimately, there is no single correct choice. Each decision depends on individual goals, values and circumstances.*

---

## 10. Reading Comprehension

Answer:

1. What is the main issue in life decisions?  
2. What are the two main options described?  
3. What is the challenge?  
4. Why is there no single answer?
