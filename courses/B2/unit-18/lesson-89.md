## 3. Grammar – Modal Verbs (Real Use)

### Obligation / Expectation

- You’re expected to stay late.  
- You have to meet deadlines.  

---

### Advice

- You should take a break.  
- You might want to set boundaries.  

---

### Lack of obligation

- You don’t have to respond immediately.  
- You’re not expected to work overtime.  

---

### Possibility

- This can lead to burnout.  
- It might affect your health.  

---

## 4. Choose the Correct Form

Choose the correct modal verb. In each sentence, BOTH options are grammatically possible — focus on meaning.

1. You (should / might want to) take a break if you’re feeling exhausted.  
2. Employees (have to / are expected to) attend weekly meetings.  
3. You (don’t have to / shouldn’t) check emails after work hours.  
4. This situation (can / might) lead to burnout if it continues.  
5. Managers (must / are supposed to) support their teams.

## 8. Reading

Read the text:

👉 *Maintaining a healthy work-life balance has become increasingly difficult in modern workplaces. Many employees are expected to be available outside working hours, which can lead to stress and burnout.*

*Although flexible working arrangements are becoming more common, they do not always reduce pressure. In some cases, employees feel that they have to respond to messages at any time, even when they are not officially working.*

*Experts suggest that setting clear boundaries is essential. Employees should not feel obligated to be constantly available, and employers should respect personal time.*

*Ultimately, achieving balance requires both individual effort and organisational support. Without this, long-term productivity and well-being may be seriously affected.*
