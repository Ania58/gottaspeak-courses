# Revision – Unit 18 (B2)

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- describe modern workplaces precisely  
- compare different work environments  
- explain workplace trends and expectations  
- understand and analyse authentic listening  

---

## 1. Advanced Vocabulary (Precision Task)

Choose the BEST option (not just correct, but most natural):

1. A company with a strong __________ tends to retain employees.  
   (environment / culture / structure)

2. Employees who can __________ their workload perform better.  
   (handle / control / survive)

3. A __________ workplace often leads to burnout.  
   (competitive / high-pressure / ambitious)

4. Career growth depends on __________ opportunities.  
   (learning / development / training)

---

## 2. Comparisons (Meaning-Based)

Choose the option that BEST completes the meaning:

1. Modern offices are __________ traditional ones in terms of flexibility.  
   (far more flexible than / more flexible / slightly flexible than)

2. Open-plan offices are __________ private offices.  
   (not nearly as quiet as / less quieter than / more less quiet than)

3. This company is __________ innovative workplaces in the industry.  
   (one of the most / one of the more / most of the)

## 4. Linking (Logic)

Choose the BEST connector:

1. The company offers benefits; __________, employees are still stressed.  
   (however / moreover)

2. __________ the workload is high, productivity remains strong.  
   (Although / Therefore)

3. Employees feel supported; __________, they perform better.  
   (as a result / whereas)

4. The salary is competitive; __________, the working hours are demanding.  
   (however / in addition)

5. The company invested in training; __________, staff performance improved.  
   (as a result / although)

---

## 5. Modal Verbs (Meaning)

Choose the BEST option based on meaning (both are possible — think about nuance):

1. You (might want to / should) reconsider taking on another project right now.  
2. New employees (have to / are expected to) complete onboarding within the first week.  
3. You (don’t have to / shouldn’t) attend the meeting if it’s not relevant to your role.  
4. This decision (can / must) have serious consequences if not handled properly.  
5. Managers (are supposed to / have to) provide regular feedback, but in reality it doesn’t always happen.  

---

## 6. Future Forms (Context)

Choose the BEST option based on meaning (focus on intention vs prediction vs arrangement):

1. I (will probably switch / am switching) to a remote role next year — I’m still considering it.  
2. The company (will expand / is expanding) into new markets — the process has already started.  
3. We (are going to launch / will launch) the new platform in June — it’s already scheduled.  
4. I think automation (will replace / is replacing) some roles, but not all of them.  
5. She (is meeting / will meet) the client tomorrow — it’s already arranged in her calendar.

## 10. Listening Tasks

### Part 1 – General Understanding

1. What is unusual about Google’s office?  
2. Is it presented as positive, negative or both?  

---

### Part 2 – Workplace Design

1. What facilities are mentioned?  
2. How is this office different from traditional ones?  

---

### Part 3 – Company Strategy

1. Why does Google invest in these environments?  
2. What is their long-term goal?  

---

### Part 4 – Productivity

1. How can these environments affect productivity?  
2. What do experts warn about?  

---

### Part 5 – Reality Check

1. Why can’t most companies copy Google?  
2. What really matters more than perks?
