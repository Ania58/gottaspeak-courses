## 4. Functional Language – Agreeing & Disagreeing

At B2 level, we avoid:
❌ *You’re wrong.*  
❌ *I don’t agree.*

---

### 🔹 Agreeing naturally

- I completely agree with that.  
- That’s a fair point.  
- I see what you mean.  

---

### 🔹 Partial agreement

- I see your point, but…  
- That’s true to some extent, however…  

---

### 🔹 Disagreeing diplomatically

- I’m not entirely convinced that…  
- I’m not sure I agree with that.  
- I can see why you’d say that, but…

## 7. Reading – Disagreement in Communication

Read the text.

Disagreement is a natural part of communication, but the way it is expressed can affect relationships.

Some people prefer to express their opinions directly, while others choose a more diplomatic approach. Direct communication can be efficient, but it may also lead to conflict.

On the other hand, being too indirect can make communication unclear. People may not fully understand each other’s position.

Effective communication involves balancing honesty with respect. A well-expressed disagreement can lead to better understanding and stronger relationships.

In the end, it is not disagreement itself that causes problems, but how it is handled.
