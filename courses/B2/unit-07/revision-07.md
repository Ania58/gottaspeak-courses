# Revision – Unit 7 (B2)

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- express and defend opinions with nuance  
- react to ideas diplomatically  
- build structured arguments  
- analyse consequences and reflect on viewpoints  

---

## 1. Warm-up (Discussion Starter)

Discuss:

1. Do people think critically enough today?
2. Why do people follow trends instead of questioning them?
3. Is buying second-hand a good idea or not?
4. What influences people’s opinions the most?

👉 Try to:
- soften at least one opinion  
- challenge one idea

- This is completely ineffective.  
- It seems to me that this might not work well.  
- I would argue that this approach is ineffective.  
- It could be said that this is not ideal.

## 5. Argumentation – Building Ideas

Expand the ideas into short arguments (2–3 sentences).

1. Buying second-hand products is beneficial.  
2. People are influenced by trends.  
3. Advertising affects decision-making.  
4. Online platforms change consumer behaviour.  

👉 Include:
- a clear argument  
- a reason  
- an example  

---

## 6. Hypothetical Reasoning

Develop each idea logically.

1. If more people bought second-hand items…  
2. If fast fashion continues to grow…  
3. This could lead to…  
4. This might result in…  

---

Now extend one idea into a full mini-argument (2–3 sentences).

## 7. Reflection – Changing Perspectives

Complete the reflection.

1. I used to think that buying second-hand __________, but now I realise __________.  
2. My perspective has changed because __________.  
3. Looking at it now, I would say __________.  

---

Rewrite one answer to sound more formal and precise.

## 8. Listening – Second-hand Culture

🔗 **Listening:**  
[Buying Second-hand Items](https://youtu.be/EEvfYC94iKI?si=lBbssF8B8gCj_rLn)

---

### Part 1 – General Understanding

1. What is the main topic of the podcast?  
2. Why is second-hand shopping becoming more popular?  

---

### Part 2 – Key Information

1. What are charity shops and what is their purpose?  
2. Why are second-hand items more affordable?  
3. How does second-hand shopping help the environment?  
4. What is the difference between charity shops and online platforms?  
5. What advantages do online second-hand platforms offer?  

---

### Part 3 – Critical Thinking

1. Why do some people have a negative opinion about second-hand shopping?  
2. Do you think these opinions are justified? Why / why not?  
3. What argument from the speaker do you find most convincing?

## 9. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. Is second-hand shopping a good idea?
2. Should people be encouraged to change their habits?
3. What are the strongest arguments for and against it?
4. Has your opinion changed during this lesson?

👉 Try to use:
- nuanced opinion phrases  
- diplomatic disagreement  
- hypothetical reasoning  
- reflection language
