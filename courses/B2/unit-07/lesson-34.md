## 4. Grammar / Function – Hypothetical Reasoning

We use conditional thinking to explore ideas.

---

### 🔹 Talking about possible outcomes

- If this continues, it could lead to problems.  
- If people change their habits, things might improve.  

---

### 🔹 Speculating

- This might result in higher costs.  
- That could have a negative impact.  

---

### 🔹 Evaluating ideas

- If we look at it this way, it could mean that…  
- This would probably cause…

## 7. Reading – Thinking About Consequences

Read the text.

In many situations, people focus on immediate results rather than long-term consequences. This can lead to decisions that seem beneficial at first but create problems later.

For example, spending money without planning may feel satisfying in the moment. However, if this behaviour continues, it can result in financial difficulties.

On the other hand, thinking ahead allows people to make better decisions. Considering possible outcomes helps avoid negative consequences.

At the same time, not all outcomes can be predicted. Even well-thought-out decisions may lead to unexpected results.

In the end, the ability to think about consequences is essential for making informed choices.

---
