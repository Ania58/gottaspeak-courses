## 4. Functional Language – Building Arguments

At B2 level, we don’t just say:
❌ *I think this is good because…*

We structure ideas:

---

### 🔹 Introducing an argument

- The main argument is that…  
- One key issue is that…  

---

### 🔹 Explaining

- This can be explained by…  
- The reason for this is…  

---

### 🔹 Giving examples

- A clear example of this is…  
- For instance…

## 7. Reading – What Makes an Argument Strong?

Read the text.

A strong argument is not just about having an opinion. It requires clear reasoning, supporting evidence and logical structure.

Many people express opinions without explaining them. However, without reasons or examples, arguments are less convincing.

A well-structured argument usually includes a main idea, an explanation and at least one example. This helps the listener understand and evaluate the point being made.

Clarity is also important. Arguments should be easy to follow and avoid unnecessary complexity.

In the end, the strongest arguments are those that combine clear thinking with effective communication.
