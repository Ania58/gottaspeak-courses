## 4. Grammar / Function – Changing Perspectives

At B2 level, we describe **how thinking evolves**, not just opinions.

---

### 🔹 Describing past beliefs

- I used to think that…  
- I used to assume that…  

---

### 🔹 Showing change

- However, I’ve come to realise that…  
- Over time, my perspective has changed.  
- I now see things differently.  

---

### 🔹 Explaining why

- This is mainly because…  
- This shift happened when…

## 7. Reading – Changing Perspectives

Read the text.

People often believe that their opinions are fixed, but in reality, they change over time. Experience, new information and different perspectives can all influence how we think.

For example, someone may initially believe that success depends only on hard work. However, after gaining more experience, they may realise that other factors such as opportunity and support also play an important role.

Changing opinions does not mean being inconsistent. On the contrary, it often shows growth and deeper understanding.

At the same time, some people resist changing their views. This can limit their ability to learn and adapt.

In the end, being open to new ideas allows people to develop and improve their thinking.

---

### Reading Questions
