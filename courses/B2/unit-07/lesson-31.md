## 4. Grammar – Nuanced Opinions

At B2 level, we avoid simple statements like:
❌ *I think this is good.*

We use more controlled language:

---

### 🔹 Expressing softer opinions

- I tend to think that…  
- It seems to me that…  
- I’m inclined to believe that…  

---

### 🔹 Expressing stronger opinions

- I would argue that…  
- There’s a strong case for…  

---

### 🔹 Adding distance

- It could be said that…  
- Some people would argue that…

## 7. Reading – Different Perspectives

Read the text.

In modern society, people are constantly exposed to different opinions. While this can encourage open-mindedness, it can also lead to confusion and disagreement.

Some individuals prefer to express their opinions clearly and directly. Others believe that it is better to be more careful and avoid strong statements.

In many situations, the way an opinion is expressed is just as important as the opinion itself. Using more nuanced language can help avoid conflict and make discussions more productive.

At the same time, being too indirect can make communication unclear. It is important to find a balance between clarity and diplomacy.

In the end, effective communication depends on understanding both your own perspective and the perspectives of others.

---

### Reading Questions
