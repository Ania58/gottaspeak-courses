## 4. Grammar – Modals + have + past participle (B2)

We use these forms to talk about the past.

---

### 🔹 must have + past participle  
👉 strong certainty (logical conclusion)

- He must have forgotten the meeting.  

---

### 🔹 might / may / could have + past participle  
👉 possibility

- She might have misunderstood the instructions.  

---

### 🔹 can’t have + past participle  
👉 strong negative deduction

- He can’t have finished already.  

---

### 🔹 should have + past participle  
👉 criticism / regret

- You should have checked the details.  

---

### 🔹 could have + past participle  
👉 missed opportunity

- I could have done it better.

## 7. Reading – A Costly Mistake

Read the text.

James had been working on an important project for weeks. The deadline was approaching, and the pressure was increasing. One day, he sent the final report to the client without double-checking it.

A few hours later, his manager called him. There was a major error in the data. The client noticed it immediately, and the company lost an important opportunity.

James felt terrible. He knew he should have reviewed the report more carefully. His manager was disappointed but supportive. Instead of blaming him, she encouraged him to learn from the mistake.

Looking back, James realised that he had been rushing because of stress. He also understood that asking for help might have prevented the problem.

Although the situation was difficult, it became an important lesson. James became more careful in his work and more willing to ask for feedback.
