## 4. Grammar – Indirect Questions (B2)

We use indirect questions to sound more polite.

---

### 🔹 Direct vs Indirect

Direct:
- Where is the office?  

Indirect:
- Could you tell me **where the office is**?  

---

### 🔹 Structure

👉 no inversion (normal sentence order)

❌ Where is he working?  
✅ Do you know **where he is working**?  

---

### 🔹 Useful phrases

- Could you tell me…  
- Do you know…  
- I was wondering if…  
- Would you mind telling me…  

---

## 5. Functional Language – Softening

We soften language to sound polite.

---

### Examples:

Direct:
- Send me the report.  

Softened:
- Could you send me the report?  
- I was wondering if you could send me the report.  

---

### Practice

Rewrite the sentences to sound more polite.

## 8. Reading – A Job Interview

Read the text.

Emma had been preparing for her job interview for several days. She researched the company, reviewed common interview questions, and practised her answers. However, she knew that communication was not only about answering questions but also about asking them.

During the interview, Emma tried to sound professional and polite. Instead of asking direct questions, she used indirect forms such as “Could you tell me more about the team?” and “I was wondering how success is measured in this role.”

The interviewer appreciated her approach. It showed that she was both confident and respectful. Emma also made sure to listen carefully and respond thoughtfully.

At the end of the interview, she asked a few final questions and thanked the interviewer for their time. A few days later, she followed up with a polite email.

Emma didn’t know if she would get the job, but she felt confident that she had communicated effectively.

---

### Reading Questions
