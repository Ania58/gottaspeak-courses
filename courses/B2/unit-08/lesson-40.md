At B2 level, we don’t just say:
❌ *I think this is good.*

We build conclusions:

---

### 🔹 Weighing ideas

- On the one hand…, on the other hand…  

---

### 🔹 Evaluating

- Taking everything into account…  
- Based on this…  

---

### 🔹 Concluding

- A balanced view would be…  
- Overall, it seems that…

## 7. Reading – Finding a Balanced View

Read the text.

In many discussions, people focus on one side of an argument and ignore alternative perspectives. This can lead to extreme opinions and poor decision-making.

A balanced approach involves considering multiple viewpoints and evaluating the evidence. This does not mean that all opinions are equally valid, but it requires careful analysis.

For example, while technology has many advantages, it also creates challenges such as misinformation and overuse. Ignoring either side leads to an incomplete understanding.

Developing the ability to form balanced conclusions is essential in a world full of information and opinions.

In the end, good judgement depends on the ability to think critically and consider different perspectives.
