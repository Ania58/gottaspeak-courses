## 4. Grammar / Function – Expressing Uncertainty

At B2 level, we avoid:
❌ *This is definitely true.*  
❌ *This is wrong.*

---

### 🔹 Expressing uncertainty

- It might be true that…  
- This could suggest that…  
- It seems possible that…  

---

### 🔹 Evaluating information

- It’s difficult to say whether…  
- This may be biased because…  
- It’s not entirely clear if…  

---

### 🔹 Adding distance

- Some people might argue that…  
- It could be interpreted as…

## 7. Reading – Different Perspectives

Read the text.

In today’s world, information is often presented in a way that reflects a particular perspective. This can make it difficult to identify what is objective and what is influenced by bias.

Different people may interpret the same information in different ways. This is because everyone brings their own experiences and assumptions.

As a result, it is important to question information and consider multiple viewpoints. Accepting information without analysis can lead to misunderstanding.

However, being too sceptical can also be problematic. Not all information is biased, and some sources are more reliable than others.

In the end, critical thinking involves balancing trust and doubt.
