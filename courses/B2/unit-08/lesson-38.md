## 4. Grammar / Function – Wish & If Only

We use **wish / if only** to talk about:
👉 regret  
👉 unreal situations  
👉 frustration  

---

### 🔹 Talking about the past (regret)

- I wish I had saved more money.  
- If only I had known the truth.  

---

### 🔹 Talking about the present (unreal)

- I wish I had more time.  
- If only things were different.  

---

### 🔹 Talking about situations that annoy us

- I wish people would think more carefully.

## 7. Reading – Looking Back at Decisions

Read the text.

People often reflect on past decisions and think about what they could have done differently. This is a natural part of learning and personal development.

For example, someone might regret not taking an opportunity or making a risky choice. If they had acted differently, their situation might be better now.

However, constantly focusing on regret can be harmful. It is important to learn from past experiences without becoming stuck in them.

At the same time, reflecting on decisions can lead to growth. It helps people understand themselves better and make better choices in the future.

In the end, regret is not only about the past, but also about how we move forward.

---

### Reading Questions

1. Why do people reflect on the past?
2. What example is given?
3. Why can regret be harmful?
4. What is the benefit of reflection?
5. What is the main message?
