# Revision – Unit 8 (B2)

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- analyse influence and media messages  
- express regret and unreal situations naturally  
- recognise bias and uncertainty  
- form balanced and reflective conclusions  

---

## 1. Warm-up (Discussion)

Discuss:

1. Do people make decisions based on logic or emotion?
2. How much are our choices influenced by others?
3. Is it easy to recognise manipulation?
4. Do people regret their decisions too often?

👉 Try to:
- question one idea  
- give one balanced opinion  

---

## 2. Vocabulary Revision – Influence & Thinking

Rewrite the sentences using a more precise expression.

1. This message tries to influence people.  
→ This message __________________________________________  

2. People believe things without checking.  
→ People tend to _________________________________________  

3. This information is not completely reliable.  
→ This information _______________________________________  

4. This idea affects how people think.  
→ This idea ______________________________________________  

5. This could be a biased opinion.  
→ It might ______________________________________________  

---

## 3. Analysing Language – Persuasion

Explain what each statement is designed to do.

1. “Only a few places left – act now.”  
2. “Everyone is choosing this option.”  
3. “Experts recommend this.”  
4. “This will change your life.”  

👉 For each:
- intention  
- emotional effect  

---

## 4. Functional Language – Wish / If Only

Rephrase the ideas using **wish / if only**.

1. I didn’t think carefully before making the decision.  
2. I don’t have enough confidence now.  
3. I followed other people’s expectations.  
4. I made the wrong choice in the past.  
5. I didn’t take a risk.  

---

Now extend ONE sentence into a short reflection (2–3 sentences).

---

## 5. Critical Thinking – Bias & Perspective

Read the statements and respond critically.

1. “University is the only path to success.”  
2. “Money is the most important factor in life.”  
3. “People who take risks are irresponsible.”  
4. “Following your passion always leads to success.”  

👉 Respond:
- question the idea  
- introduce an alternative perspective  

---

## 6. Argumentation – Developing Ideas

Turn the ideas into short structured arguments.

1. People are influenced by expectations.  
2. Financial decisions are often emotional.  
3. Regret can help people grow.  

👉 Include:
- a clear argument  
- a reason  
- an example
