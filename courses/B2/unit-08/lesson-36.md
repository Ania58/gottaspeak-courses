## 4. Grammar / Function – Evaluating Information

At B2 level, we don’t say:
❌ *This is true.*  
❌ *This is false.*

We analyse:

---

### 🔹 Expressing uncertainty

- It appears that…  
- It seems that…  
- It could be argued that…  

---

### 🔹 Referring to evidence

- There is evidence that…  
- This suggests that…  

---

### 🔹 Reporting claims

- It is claimed that…  
- Some sources suggest that…

## 7. Reading – Fact or Opinion?

Read the text.

In today’s digital world, people are constantly exposed to large amounts of information. However, not all of it is reliable.

Many articles present opinions as facts. This can make it difficult for readers to distinguish between objective information and personal viewpoints.

One common problem is bias. Some sources may present information in a way that supports a particular perspective, while ignoring other viewpoints.

To evaluate information effectively, it is important to consider the source, check for evidence and compare different opinions.

In the end, critical thinking is essential for understanding the information we receive.
