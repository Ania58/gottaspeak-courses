## 4. Grammar – Modals for Advice & Obligation

---

### 🔹 should  
👉 general advice

- You should get more sleep.  

---

### 🔹 ought to  
👉 similar to *should*, slightly more formal

- You ought to see a doctor.  

---

### 🔹 had better  
👉 strong advice (possible negative consequence)

- You had better rest, or you’ll get worse.  

---

### 🔹 need to  
👉 necessity / obligation

- You need to take this seriously.  

---

### 🔹 Key difference

- should → suggestion  
- had better → warning  
- need to → necessity

## 7. Reading – Taking Care of Your Health

Read the text.

Taking care of your health is essential, but many people ignore early signs of problems. Instead of taking action, they continue their usual routines until the situation becomes serious.

One of the main issues is a lack of awareness. People often underestimate symptoms and delay seeking help. As a result, minor problems can develop into more serious conditions.

Another factor is lifestyle. Poor diet, lack of exercise and stress can all affect physical and mental health. Experts often advise making small but consistent changes.

It is also important to listen to your body. When you feel tired or unwell, it may be a sign that you need to slow down.

In the end, prevention is more effective than treatment. Taking care of your health early can prevent bigger problems later.
