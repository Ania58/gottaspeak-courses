## 3. Grammar – Cause & Effect

### Cause:

- because  
- due to  
- as a result of  

---

### Effect:

- as a result  
- this led to  
- therefore  

---

### Examples:

- English spread because of British expansion.  
- Due to global trade, English became more important.  
- This led to its worldwide use.  

---

## 4. Choose the Correct Form

1. English spread (because / as a result) of historical events.  
2. Due (of / to) globalisation, English is widely used.  
3. This (lead / led) to the dominance of English.  
4. As a result, people (learn / learned) English worldwide.

## 8. Reading

Read the text:

👉 *English has become a global language due to a combination of historical and modern factors. One major reason is the expansion of the British Empire, which spread the language across many parts of the world. Later, the economic and cultural influence of the United States strengthened its position even further.*

*As a result of globalisation, English is now widely used in business, science and technology. It has become the main language of international communication, allowing people from different countries to connect and exchange ideas.*

*This widespread use has led to a situation where English is often seen as essential for career development. Therefore, more and more people are choosing to learn it, which continues to reinforce its global dominance.*
