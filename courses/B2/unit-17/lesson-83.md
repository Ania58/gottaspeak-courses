## 3. Grammar – Reported Speech

### Direct → Reported

- “English is difficult.”  
👉 He said that English was difficult.  

---

- “Spelling doesn’t match pronunciation.”  
👉 Experts say that spelling doesn’t match pronunciation.  

---

### Key Points:

✔ backshift (past reporting)  
✔ no change (general truths)  

---

## 4. Choose the Correct Form

1. He said that English (is / was) difficult.  
2. Experts say that spelling (doesn’t / didn’t) match pronunciation.  
3. They said that many words (have / had) changed over time.  
4. She explained that English (has / had) many influences.

## 8. Reading

Read the text:

👉 *English spelling is often considered one of the most confusing aspects of the language. Experts explain that this is largely due to historical changes. Over time, pronunciation has evolved, but spelling has not always been updated to reflect these changes.*

*For example, during the Great Vowel Shift, the way many words were pronounced changed significantly. However, the written forms remained the same. As a result, many modern English words no longer match their original pronunciation.*

*Some linguists say that attempts to standardise spelling have been made, but these efforts have not fully succeeded. This is why English spelling still contains many irregular forms today.*
