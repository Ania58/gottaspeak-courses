# Lesson 82 – Where English Words Come From

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- talk about origins of English vocabulary  
- use passive voice naturally  
- describe how words entered the language

## 3. Grammar – Passive Voice

### Present Passive

- English is influenced by many languages.  
- Words are borrowed from different sources.  

---

### Past Passive

- Many words were introduced after 1066.  
- French vocabulary was added to English.  

---

## 4. Choose the Correct Form

1. English (is influenced / influences) by many languages.  
2. Many words (were introduced / introduced) by the Normans.  
3. Latin words (are used / use) in scientific language.  
4. New vocabulary (is added / adds) every year.

## 8. Reading

Read the text:

👉 *English vocabulary has been shaped by many historical events. One of the most important influences came from the Norman Conquest in 1066, when French was introduced into England. As a result, many French words were adopted, especially in areas such as law, government and cuisine.*

*Earlier, the language had already been influenced by Latin and Norse. Latin was brought by the Romans, while Norse words were added by Viking settlers. These words were gradually integrated into everyday use.*

*Today, English is considered a global language partly because of its ability to absorb new vocabulary. Words from different languages are still being added, which makes English a constantly evolving system.*
