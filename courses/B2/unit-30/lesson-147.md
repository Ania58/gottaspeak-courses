## 3. Grammar – Discourse Markers (Advanced Use)

We use these to sound natural:

✔ to be honest  
✔ the thing is  
✔ what I mean is  
✔ you know  
✔ actually  
✔ basically  

---

### Example:

To be honest, I find it difficult to speak sometimes.  
The thing is, I don’t always feel confident.  
What I mean is, I know the words, but I can’t always use them quickly.

## 9. Reading

Read the text.

Fluency is not about speaking quickly, but about connecting ideas smoothly. Many learners pause too often because they focus on correctness instead of communication.

Discourse markers help speakers organise their thoughts and guide the listener. They make speech sound more natural and easier to follow.

Hesitation is normal, but too much of it can interrupt the flow. With practice, speakers learn to manage pauses and express themselves more confidently.

What matters most is not perfect grammar, but the ability to communicate ideas clearly and naturally.
