# Lesson 124 – Decisions and Consequences

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- analyse decisions across time  
- connect past actions with present results  
- describe alternative outcomes

## 5. Transformation (Time Shift Thinking)

Rewrite:

1. People didn’t save money. Now they have problems.  
👉 (mixed conditional)

2. The treatment failed. The patient is still ill.  
👉 (mixed conditional)

3. They didn’t do enough research. Now they don’t trust the results.  
👉 (mixed conditional)

4. People don’t understand the risks. They made bad decisions.  
👉 (mixed conditional)

5. The system is weak. People suffered in the past.  
👉 (mixed conditional)

## 9. Reading

Read the text:

*In many real-life situations, decisions are not isolated events but part of a longer chain of consequences. A single choice can affect both immediate outcomes and long-term results.*

*For example, failing to address a health issue early can lead to more serious complications later. If a condition is ignored for too long, treatment becomes more difficult and recovery takes longer.*

*Similarly, decisions made without proper research can result in ineffective or even harmful outcomes. People often rely on incomplete information, which increases the risk of making poor choices.*

*Understanding how decisions connect across time can help individuals make better choices and avoid unnecessary risks.*
