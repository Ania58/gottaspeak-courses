## 9. Reading 

Read the text:

👉 *In many parts of the world, traditional medicine continues to play an important role in everyday healthcare. These remedies are often based on natural ingredients such as plants, roots and herbs, and are passed down through generations.*

*In Australia, for example, researchers have started to investigate bush medicine used by Indigenous communities. Some of these treatments have been used for hundreds of years to deal with infections, pain and skin conditions.*

*What makes bush medicine interesting is that it is sometimes so effective that scientists want to understand why it works. However, the challenge is that many of these remedies have not been tested in controlled scientific environments.*

*As a result, even if a treatment appears to work, it may not be reliable enough to use on a larger scale. There is also the risk that certain remedies could cause side effects that are not immediately obvious.*

*At the same time, modern medicine is not perfect. Some treatments are too expensive or not accessible enough for everyone. This creates a situation where people turn to alternative solutions, even if the evidence is limited.*

*The key question is not whether traditional medicine is good or bad, but whether it can be studied and used safely alongside modern medical practices.*
