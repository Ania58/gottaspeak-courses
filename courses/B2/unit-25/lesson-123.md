# Lesson 123 – Future Developments in Medicine

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- describe future processes  
- talk about completed actions in the future  
- express duration leading up to a future point

## 3. Grammar – Future Forms 

### Future Continuous
✔ This time next year, researchers will be studying new treatments  
✔ Doctors will be working on new solutions  

---

### Future Perfect
✔ By 2030, scientists will have developed new methods  
✔ Many diseases will have been reduced  

---

### Future Perfect Continuous
✔ By next year, they will have been testing this treatment for 5 years  
✔ Researchers will have been working on this problem for decades

## 5. Transformation (Time Perspective)

Rewrite:

1. Researchers are studying this now.  
👉 (future continuous)

2. Scientists will finish the research by 2030.  
👉 (future perfect)

3. They are testing the treatment for several years.  
👉 (future perfect continuous)

4. Doctors are working on new methods.  
👉 (future continuous – future context)

5. Experts will analyse the data before publishing it.  
👉 (future perfect)

## 9. Reading

Read the text:

*The future of medicine is likely to change significantly over the next few decades. Scientists are already developing new treatments that could transform how diseases are managed.*

*In the coming years, researchers will be focusing more on personalised medicine, where treatments are adapted to individual patients. At the same time, technology will be playing a larger role in diagnosis and prevention.*

*By 2035, many traditional treatments will have been replaced by more advanced solutions. However, this does not mean that traditional medicine will disappear completely.*

*In fact, researchers will have been studying natural remedies more closely, trying to understand their effects and integrate them into modern healthcare systems.*
