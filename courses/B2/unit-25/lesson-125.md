# Lesson 125 – Evaluating Information

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- emphasise important ideas  
- evaluate information more precisely  
- express judgement clearly

## 3. Grammar – Cleft Sentences (Emphasis)

Examples:

✔ It is the lack of evidence that makes this claim weak  
✔ It was the results that caused concern  
✔ What matters is the reliability of the data  
✔ What we need is more research

## 9. Reading

Read the text:

*In today’s world, people are constantly exposed to new information about health and treatments. However, not all of this information is reliable.*

*What often influences people is not the quality of evidence, but how convincing the message appears. This can lead to situations where weak or misleading claims are accepted as facts.*

*It is the lack of critical thinking that allows such ideas to spread. Without careful evaluation, people may rely on assumptions rather than evidence.*

*Understanding how to assess information is essential, especially when it comes to health. What matters most is not how popular a treatment is, but whether it has been proven to be effective.*
