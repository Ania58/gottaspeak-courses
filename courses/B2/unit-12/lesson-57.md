# Lesson 57 – Self-Image

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- describe self-image and perception  
- compare self-perception with others’ opinions  
- analyse how self-image affects behaviour  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. Do people see themselves realistically?
2. Is self-confidence based on reality or perception?
3. Do others see us differently than we see ourselves?
4. What influences self-image the most?

---

## 2. Self vs Others

Read the situations:

1. Someone thinks they are shy, but others see them as calm.  
2. A person believes they are confident, but others see them as arrogant.  
3. Someone feels invisible, but others notice them a lot.  
4. A person thinks they are not good enough, but others admire them.  

---

### Task

👉 For each:

- what is the self-image?  
- what is the external perception?  

👉 Then:
Why is there a difference?

---

## 3. How Self-Image Affects Behaviour

Look at the behaviours:

- avoiding opportunities  
- overcompensating  
- staying quiet  
- trying too hard  
- not taking risks  

---

### Task

👉 Match each behaviour with a possible belief  

Example:
→ avoiding opportunities → “I’m not good enough”

---

## 4. Language in Action

We describe perception like this:

- I see myself as quite independent.  
- I come across as more confident than I feel.  
- I’m often seen as quiet.  
- I tend to underestimate myself.  

---

### Task

👉 Describe:

1. how you see yourself  
2. how others might see you differently  
3. one misunderstanding about you  

---

## 5. Phrasal Verbs – Self-Perception

Match:

1. come across as  
2. open up  
3. hold back  
4. build up  

Meanings:

a) express yourself more  
b) appear in a certain way  
c) gain confidence  
d) avoid expressing yourself  

---

### Task

👉 Describe situations where people:

- appear differently than they feel  
- become more open  
- hide their personality  
- develop confidence  

---

## 6. Idioms – Self-Image

Match:

1. believe in yourself  
2. be too hard on yourself  
3. put yourself down  
4. fake it till you make it  
5. have a low opinion of yourself  

Meanings:

a) lack confidence  
b) criticise yourself  
c) act confident until you become it  
d) trust your abilities  
e) think negatively about yourself  

---

### Task

👉 Choose TWO idioms and describe real-life situations  

---

## 7. Scenario – Misunderstood Identity

Situation:

👉 People often misunderstand your personality.

---

### Task

👉 What do you do?

- explain yourself  
- ignore it  
- change your behaviour  

👉 Explain:
- why  
- risks  
- long-term effect  

---

## 8. Perspective Task

Read:

👉 “Confidence is more important than ability.”

---

### Task

1. Do you agree?  
2. Give an example  
3. What is the limitation of this idea?  

---

## 9. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. Why do people underestimate themselves?
2. How can self-image affect success?
3. Is it possible to change how you see yourself?
4. Should people care about others’ opinions?

👉 Try to use:
- 2 phrasal verbs  
- 1 idiom  

---

## 10. Writing Task

Write **180–220 words**.

Topic:

**How self-image influences behaviour and decisions**

Include:

- examples  
- differences between perception and reality  
- consequences  
- your opinion
