# Lesson 59 – Confidence and Insecurity

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- explain what influences confidence  
- describe insecure behaviour  
- analyse how confidence affects decisions  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. What makes someone confident?
2. Is confidence natural or learned?
3. Why do people feel insecure?
4. Can confidence be faked?

---

## 2. Confidence in Action

Read the situations:

1. Someone avoids speaking in public.  
2. A person takes risks without hesitation.  
3. Someone doubts their abilities even when they are good.  
4. A person acts confident but feels insecure inside.  

---

### Task

👉 For each:

- what level of confidence is shown?  
- what might be the reason?  

---

## 3. Behaviour Patterns

Look at the behaviours:

- avoiding challenges  
- overthinking everything  
- comparing yourself to others  
- taking initiative  
- staying silent  

---

### Task

👉 Match each behaviour with:

- confidence  
- insecurity  

👉 Then:
Which behaviours limit people the most?

---

## 4. Language in Action

We describe confidence like this:

- I struggle to speak in front of people.  
- I tend to doubt myself.  
- I managed to overcome my fear.  
- I’m likely to avoid risky situations.  
- I feel more confident over time.  

---

### Task

👉 Describe:

1. a situation where someone lacked confidence  
2. a situation where someone showed confidence  
3. how confidence changed over time  

---

## 5. Phrasal Verbs – Confidence

Match:

1. build up  
2. hold back  
3. speak up  
4. push yourself  

Meanings:

a) force yourself to act  
b) gain confidence  
c) express your opinion  
d) stop yourself  

---

### Task

👉 Describe situations where people:

- gain confidence  
- stop themselves from acting  
- express their opinion  
- try harder  

---

## 6. Idioms – Confidence

Match:

1. believe in yourself  
2. fake it till you make it  
3. step out of your comfort zone  
4. lack confidence  
5. doubt yourself  

Meanings:

a) not trust your abilities  
b) act confident to become confident  
c) not feel confident  
d) trust yourself  
e) try something difficult  

---

### Task

👉 Choose TWO idioms and describe real-life situations  

---

## 7. Scenario – Insecurity

Situation:

👉 You feel insecure in an important situation (e.g. interview, presentation).

---

### Task

👉 What do you do?

- avoid the situation  
- prepare and face it  
- pretend to be confident  

👉 Explain:
- why  
- risks  
- best long-term result  

---

## 8. Perspective Task

Read:

👉 “Confidence matters more than ability.”

---

### Task

1. Do you agree?  
2. Give an example  
3. What is the limitation of this idea?  

---

## 9. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. Why do people lack confidence?
2. How can people build confidence?
3. Is insecurity always negative?
4. What helps people overcome fear?

👉 Try to use:
- 2 phrasal verbs  
- 1 idiom  

---

## 10. Writing Task

Write **180–220 words**.

Topic:

**Why confidence is important in life**

Include:

- examples  
- consequences  
- challenges  
- your opinion
