# Lesson 58 – Social Identity

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- explain how identity is shaped by society  
- analyse the influence of environment and expectations  
- describe social roles and pressures  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. How much of your personality is influenced by your environment?
2. Do people behave differently in different social groups?
3. Is it easy to be yourself in every situation?
4. What influences identity the most: family, culture, or society?

---

## 2. Identity in Context

Read the situations:

1. Someone behaves differently at work and with friends.  
2. A person feels pressure to follow social expectations.  
3. Someone changes their behaviour to fit into a group.  
4. A person rejects social norms and behaves differently.  

---

### Task

👉 For each:

- what is influencing their behaviour?  
- is it positive or negative?  

---

## 3. Social Pressure

Look at the situations:

- choosing a career based on expectations  
- following trends  
- adapting behaviour to a group  
- hiding true opinions  
- expressing individuality  

---

### Task

👉 Group them into:

- influenced by others  
- independent behaviour  

👉 Then:
Which one is harder to maintain? Why?

---

## 4. Language in Action

We describe social influence like this:

- I was influenced by my environment.  
- I grew up in a very different culture.  
- I felt expected to behave a certain way.  
- I try to fit into different situations.  

---

### Task

👉 Describe:

1. how your environment influenced you  
2. a situation where you adapted your behaviour  
3. a situation where you stayed true to yourself  

---

## 5. Phrasal Verbs – Social Behaviour

Match:

1. fit in  
2. stand up for  
3. go along with  
4. break away from  

Meanings:

a) follow others  
b) defend something  
c) be accepted in a group  
d) become independent  

---

### Task

👉 Describe situations where people:

- follow a group  
- defend their beliefs  
- try to belong  
- become independent  

---

## 6. Idioms – Social Identity

Match:

1. follow the crowd  
2. stand your ground  
3. be true to yourself  
4. go against the norm  
5. feel out of place  

Meanings:

a) be different from expectations  
b) not feel comfortable  
c) follow others  
d) stay firm in your beliefs  
e) be authentic  

---

### Task

👉 Choose TWO idioms and describe real-life situations  

---

## 7. Scenario – Social Pressure

Situation:

👉 Everyone around you expects you to make a certain decision (e.g. career, lifestyle).

---

### Task

👉 What do you do?

- follow expectations  
- make your own choice  
- try to balance both  

👉 Explain:
- why  
- risks  
- long-term effect  

---

## 8. Perspective Task

Read:

👉 “People are shaped more by society than by themselves.”

---

### Task

1. Do you agree?  
2. Give an example  
3. What is the opposite view?  

---

## 9. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. How does society influence behaviour?
2. Is it important to fit in?
3. When should people resist social pressure?
4. Can people be completely independent?

👉 Try to use:
- 2 phrasal verbs  
- 1 idiom  

---

## 10. Writing Task

Write **180–220 words**.

Topic:

**How society influences identity and behaviour**

Include:

- examples  
- advantages and disadvantages  
- challenges  
- your opinion
