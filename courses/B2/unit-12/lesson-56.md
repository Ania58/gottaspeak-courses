# Lesson 56 – Personality Traits

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- describe personality traits naturally  
- compare self-image with reality  
- analyse how personality affects behaviour  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. Is personality something you are born with or something you develop?
2. Do people see themselves accurately?
3. Can personality change over time?
4. What personality traits do you value most?

---

## 2. First Impressions

Read the situations:

1. Someone seems confident but feels insecure inside.  
2. A person is quiet but very observant.  
3. Someone appears friendly but avoids deeper conversations.  
4. A person seems serious but has a strong sense of humour.  

---

### Task

👉 For each:

- what is the visible personality?  
- what might be hidden?  

👉 Then:
Why do people create different impressions?

---

## 3. Personality in Action

Look at the behaviours:

- speaking a lot in conversations  
- avoiding risks  
- overthinking decisions  
- helping others  
- staying calm under pressure  

---

### Task

👉 Match each behaviour with a trait  

Then answer:
👉 Which traits are:
- useful  
- limiting  

---

## 4. Language in Action

We describe personality like this:

- I tend to overthink things.  
- I’m likely to avoid conflict.  
- I come across as confident.  
- I see myself as quite independent.  

---

### Task

👉 Describe yourself:

1. how you see yourself  
2. how others might see you differently  
3. one trait you would like to change  

---

## 5. Phrasal Verbs – Personality & Behaviour

Match:

1. open up  
2. hold back  
3. show off  
4. fit in  

Meanings:

a) try to impress others  
b) become more expressive  
c) feel comfortable in a group  
d) avoid expressing yourself  

---

### Task

👉 Describe situations where people:

- hide their personality  
- express themselves  
- try too hard to impress  
- adapt to others  

---

## 6. Idioms – Personality

Match:

1. be yourself  
2. put on a front  
3. stand out  
4. blend in  
5. be hard on yourself  

Meanings:

a) not show your real personality  
b) accept yourself  
c) be different from others  
d) be too critical of yourself  
e) be similar to others  

---

### Task

👉 Choose TWO idioms and describe real-life situations  

---

## 7. Real-life Scenario

Situation:

👉 You meet someone for the first time and want to make a good impression.

---

### Task

👉 What do you do?

Choose and explain:

- be completely natural  
- adapt your behaviour  
- try to impress  

👉 What are the risks of each?

---

## 8. Perspective Task

Read:

👉 “People don’t change their personality, they just learn to control it.”

---

### Task

1. Do you agree?  
2. Give an example  
3. What is the opposite view?  

---

## 9. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. Do people know themselves well?
2. Why do people behave differently in different situations?
3. Can personality be improved?
4. Is it important to be authentic?

👉 Try to use:
- 2 phrasal verbs  
- 1 idiom  

---

## 10. Writing Task

Write **180–220 words**.

Topic:

**How personality influences behaviour**

Include:

- examples  
- differences between people  
- challenges  
- your opinion
