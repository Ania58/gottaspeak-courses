# Revision – Unit 12 (B2)

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- describe identity in real-life situations  
- analyse how people are perceived  
- explain misunderstandings related to identity  
- reflect on how identity can change  

---

## 1. Warm-up (Discussion)

Discuss:

1. Can people be misunderstood easily?
2. How important is first impression?
3. Have you ever been judged incorrectly?
4. What shapes how others see you?

---

## 2. Identity in Practice

Read the situations:

1. Someone is judged based on appearance  
2. A person is mistaken for someone else  
3. Someone behaves differently in different places  
4. A person is seen differently by friends and strangers  

---

### Task

👉 For each:

- what is the situation?  
- what causes the misunderstanding?  

---

## 3. First Impression vs Reality

Look at the cases:

- someone looks confident but feels insecure  
- a person seems unfriendly but is just quiet  
- someone is judged too quickly  
- a person is misunderstood in a new environment  

---

### Task

👉 Choose TWO:

- explain the misunderstanding  
- explain how it could be avoided  

---

## 4. Phrasal Verbs – Identity & Perception

Use:

- come across as  
- be mistaken for  
- fit in  
- stand out  

---

### Task

👉 Create short scenarios:

1. someone is misunderstood  
2. someone doesn’t fit into a group  
3. someone is clearly different  
4. someone gives a strong impression  

---

## 5. Idioms – Perception

Use:

- judge a book by its cover  
- first impressions matter  
- get the wrong idea  
- see someone in a different light  

---

### Task

👉 Create TWO short examples showing:
- misunderstanding  
- change of perception  

---

## 6. Mini Case – Real Situation

Situation:

👉 Someone is accused of something they didn’t do.

---

### Task

👉 What happens next?

- how do they react?  
- how do others react?  
- what helps solve the situation?

## 8. Listening Tasks

### Part 1 – General Understanding

1. What happens to Jack?  
2. Why is he arrested?  

---

### Part 2 – Key Details

1. Where does the situation take place?  
2. What evidence do the police use?  
3. Why are the detectives convinced?  
4. Who helps Jack?  

---

### Part 3 – Understanding the Situation

1. Why is Jack mistaken for someone else?  
2. What makes the situation worse?  
3. What changes the outcome?  

---

### Part 4 – Interpretation

1. What does this story show about identity?  
2. How important is evidence vs appearance?  
3. What role do other people play in shaping identity?  

---

## 9. Decision Task

Situation:

👉 You are misunderstood in an important situation.

Options:

- explain everything immediately  
- stay calm and wait  
- ask others for support  

---

### Task

👉 Choose and explain:
- why  
- risks  
- best outcome  

---

## 10. Critical Thinking

Read:

👉 “People are often judged before they are understood.”

---

### Task

1. Do you agree?  
2. Give an example  
3. When can this be dangerous?  

---

## 11. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. Why do people judge others quickly?
2. Can identity be misunderstood easily?
3. How can people avoid wrong impressions?
4. Is it possible to control how others see you?

👉 Try to use:
- 2 phrasal verbs  
- 1 idiom  

---

## 12. Writing Task

Write **200–240 words**.

Topic:

**Why people are sometimes misunderstood**

Include:

- reasons  
- examples  
- consequences  
- possible solutions
