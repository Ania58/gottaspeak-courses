# Lesson 76 – Gerund vs Infinitive (B2)

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- use gerund and infinitive with key verbs  
- understand meaning changes depending on the structure  
- apply forms in real communication  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. What helps you learn a language effectively?
2. What do you find difficult when learning?
3. Do you enjoy studying grammar?
4. What motivates you to keep learning?

---

## 2. Meaning Changes

Compare:

- I regret saying that. → I’m sorry I said it  
- I regret to say that… → I’m sorry to inform you  

---

- She went on talking. → continued  
- She went on to explain. → did something next  

---

- This means working harder. → result  
- I didn’t mean to say that. → intention  

---

## 3. Choose the Correct Form

1. I regret (saying / to say) that I can’t attend the meeting.  
2. He went on (talking / to talk) for hours without stopping.  
3. After finishing one topic, she went on (explaining / to explain) another.  
4. This will mean (spending / to spend) more time studying.  
5. I didn’t mean (offending / to offend) anyone.  

---

## 4. Verb Focus – “Need”

Compare:

- This room needs cleaning. (= should be cleaned)  
- I need to clean my room. (= I will do it)  

---

### Task

👉 Complete:

1. The car needs __________ (wash).  
2. I need to __________ (finish) this task today.  
3. These documents need __________ (check).  

---

## 5. Verb Focus – “Help”

Compare:

- He helped me (to) understand the problem.  
- He helped with organising the event.  

---

### Task

👉 Complete:

1. She helped me __________ (prepare) for the exam.  
2. They helped with __________ (organise) the project.  
3. He helped me __________ (solve) the issue.  

---

## 6. Sentence Transformation

Rewrite using the correct structure:

1. I didn’t intend to interrupt you.  
👉 I didn’t mean __________  

2. I’m sorry I told him the truth.  
👉 I regret __________  

3. We continued discussing the problem.  
👉 We went on __________  

4. First she explained the basics, then she explained the details.  
👉 She went on __________  

---

## 7. Error Correction

Find and correct the mistake:

1. I regret to tell him that yesterday.  
2. This task needs to finishing soon.  
3. He went on to talking for too long.  
4. I didn’t mean upsetting you.  

---

## 8. Controlled Practice

Complete with correct form:

1. This will mean __________ (work) longer hours.  
2. I regret __________ (not / study) earlier.  
3. She went on __________ (describe) the situation in detail.  
4. I didn’t mean __________ (cause) any problems.  
5. The house needs __________ (paint).
