# Lesson 80 – Improving Fluency

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- build more complex and natural sentences  
- combine ideas effectively  
- express thoughts more fluently  

---

## 1. Warm-up

Discuss:

1. What does it mean to be fluent?
2. What helps people become fluent?
3. Is grammar or practice more important?
4. What slows down progress?

---

## 2. Structure 1 – The more…, the more…

👉 shows relationship

- The more you practise, the more confident you become.  

---

### Task

👉 Complete:

1. The more you speak, the more __________.  
2. The more you listen, the more __________.  
3. The more mistakes you make, the more __________.  

---

## 3. Structure 2 – Not only…, but also…

👉 adds emphasis

- Not only did I improve my grammar, but I also became more confident.  

---

### Task

👉 Complete:

1. Not only did I learn new words, but I also __________.  
2. Not only was it difficult, but it also __________.  

---

## 4. Structure 3 – What helps is…

👉 introduces key idea

- What helps is practising regularly.  

---

### Task

👉 Complete:

1. What helps is __________.  
2. What makes a difference is __________.  

---

## 5. Combining Ideas

Join the sentences:

1. I practised every day. I improved quickly.  
👉 The more __________  

2. I improved my speaking. I improved my listening.  
👉 Not only __________  

3. Practising regularly is important.  
👉 What helps is __________  

---

## 6. Phrasal Verbs – Learning

1. keep up with  
2. pick up  
3. go over  
4. work on  

Meanings:

a) improve  
b) review  
c) learn  
d) stay at the same level

👉 Create sentences about language learning

## 7. Idioms – Fluency

Match:

1. speak without thinking

👉 Use TWO idioms in short examples  

---

## 8. Reading

Read the text:

👉 *Becoming fluent in a language takes time and consistency. The more you expose yourself to the language, the more natural it becomes. Many learners focus only on grammar, but what really helps is regular practice and real communication.*

*Not only does speaking improve fluency, but it also builds confidence. At first, learners may struggle to express themselves, but over time they start to speak more naturally.*

*What makes the biggest difference is not perfection, but consistency. The more you use the language in real situations, the easier it becomes to communicate without thinking.*

---

## 9. Reading Comprehension

Answer:

1. What is important for fluency?  
2. What do many learners focus on?  
3. What improves confidence?  
4. What makes the biggest difference?  

---

## 10. Speaking (1:1)

Discuss:

1. What helps people become fluent faster?
2. Why do some learners struggle?
3. What is more important: accuracy or fluency?
4. How can people improve speaking skills?

👉 Try to use:
- complex structures  
- 1 phrasal verb  
- 1 idiom

**How to become fluent in a language**
