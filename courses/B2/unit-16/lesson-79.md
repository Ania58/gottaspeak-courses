# Lesson 79 – Communication Problems

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- use modal verbs to make logical deductions  
- describe misunderstandings clearly  
- explain possible reasons for communication problems

## 3. Grammar – Modal Verbs for Deduction

### Present

- He must be tired. (I’m sure)  
- She might be confused. (possible)  
- They can’t understand this. (impossible)  

---

### Past

- He must have misunderstood.  
- She might have missed the message.  
- They can’t have heard correctly.  

---

## 4. Choose the Correct Form

1. He must (be / have been) confused right now.  
2. She might (miss / have missed) the message earlier.  
3. They can’t (understand / have understood) what happened yesterday.  
4. He must (forget / have forgotten) about the meeting.

## 8. Reading

Read the text:

👉 *Misunderstandings are a natural part of communication, especially when people are using a second language. Sometimes a person might say something that sounds correct, but the meaning isn’t clear. In other cases, the listener might interpret the message differently from what was intended.*

*For example, if someone gives unclear instructions, others might misunderstand what they are supposed to do. They might have missed an important detail or interpreted the message in the wrong way.*

*In many situations, misunderstandings can be avoided by checking understanding. Asking questions and clarifying meaning can help prevent confusion before it becomes a bigger problem.*
