## 3. Grammar – Conditionals (Real Use)

### Second conditional
👉 unreal present

- If I spoke more often, I would improve faster.  

---

### Third conditional
👉 unreal past

- If I had started earlier, I would have become fluent.  

---

### Mixed conditional
👉 past → present

- If I had practised more, I would be more confident now.  

---

## 4. Choose the Correct Form

1. If I (practised / had practised) more, I would be better now.  
2. If I (start / had started) earlier, I would have learned faster.  
3. If I spoke more, I (would improve / would have improved).  
4. If I had used the language more, I (would be / would have been) more confident.

## 8. Reading

Read the text:

👉 *Being bilingual is often seen as an advantage, but it also comes with challenges. People who speak more than one language regularly switch between them, sometimes without even realising it. This can make communication easier, but it can also lead to confusion, especially when one language is used more often than the other.*

*Many bilingual speakers say that if they hadn’t practised both languages consistently, they would have lost fluency in one of them. Others feel that if they had been exposed to both languages earlier, they would be more confident now.*

*In everyday life, bilingual people often choose which language to use depending on the situation. If they are speaking to friends, they might switch freely, but in professional settings they tend to stick to one language. This ability to adapt is one of the key benefits of being bilingual.*
