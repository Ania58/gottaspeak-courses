## 3. Grammar – Narrative Tenses (REAL USE)

We don’t choose tenses randomly — we use them to show meaning.

### Background:
- He was working late that night  

### Main action:
- He left the office  

### Earlier action:
- He had forgotten something important  

---

👉 Example:

He was walking home when he realised he had left his notes in the office.

## 9. Reading

Read the text.

Stories are not just about what happens, but how events are presented over time. Writers use different forms to guide the reader through the narrative.

Background information helps create context, while main actions move the story forward. Earlier events are often used to explain motivations or consequences.

A well-structured story builds tension gradually. It introduces a situation, develops conflict and leads to a turning point, where something changes.

The way time is handled affects how the reader experiences the story. A carefully constructed narrative can make even simple events feel engaging and meaningful.
