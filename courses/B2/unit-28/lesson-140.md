## 9. Reading

Read the text.

The process of writing rarely follows a straight line. At an early stage, writers may begin with a simple idea, without knowing how it will develop.

As the process continues, the structure of the text starts to take shape. Writers experiment with different approaches, adjusting characters, events and tone.

Over time, the initial draft is revised multiple times. Some elements are expanded, while others are removed entirely. This process can be slow, but it allows the writer to refine the text.

In the later stages, attention shifts to detail. Small changes in wording or structure can significantly improve the final result. Eventually, the text reaches a point where further changes are no longer necessary.
