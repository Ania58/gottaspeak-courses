## 3. Grammar – Sentence Variation

Compare:

A:  
He was tired. He was stressed. He was overwhelmed.  

B:  
He was tired, stressed and completely overwhelmed.  

---

A:  
He opened the door. He stopped. He listened.  

B:  
He opened the door — and stopped, listening carefully.  

---

👉 Same meaning, different effect.

## 9. Reading

Read the text.

Style is not only about what is said, but how it is said. Writers shape their texts by controlling sentence length, rhythm and emphasis.

Short sentences can create tension or urgency. Longer sentences allow for more detail and reflection. By combining both, writers create variation and keep the reader engaged.

Repetition can reinforce an idea, while contrast can highlight differences. Small changes in structure can completely change how a message is perceived.

A well-written text flows naturally. It guides the reader without making the structure obvious. This balance between clarity and variation is what makes writing effective.
