## Reading – When a Service Breaks Down

Last autumn, a regional transport authority introduced a new ticketing system. The change was announced as a way to simplify payments and improve access. Unfortunately, serious problems were quickly reported.

Within the first week, hundreds of users complained that payments were taken twice. Others discovered that travel cards had been blocked without explanation. Requests were submitted, but responses were delayed. Call centres were overwhelmed and information was inconsistent.

An internal review was carried out and several technical errors were identified. Emergency procedures were introduced and additional staff were assigned. Customers were informed through official statements and refunds were gradually issued.

Although the system has since been stabilised, the case demonstrates how public trust can be damaged when procedures are poorly implemented. Transparency, clear communication and flexible response strategies are essential when services are redesigned.

## Grammar Review – Choose the Correct Form

1. Several technical errors __________ during the review.  
   (identified / were identified)

2. Customers __________ through official statements.  
   (were informed / informed)

3. Emergency procedures __________ immediately.  
   (introduced / were introduced)

4. Public trust __________ when systems fail.  
   (is damaged / damages)

5. Requests __________ but responses were delayed.  
   (were submitted / submitted)
