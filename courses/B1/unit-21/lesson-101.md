## Grammar Focus – Describing Change Over Time

When we talk about **trends and gradual change**, English often uses special comparative structures.

### ➤ more and more + adjective / noun

Used when something **continues to increase**.

Examples:

- People are becoming **more and more dependent on technology**.  
- Cities are getting **more and more crowded**.

---

### ➤ less and less

Used when something **continues to decrease**.

Examples:

- People read **less and less printed news**.  
- Young people spend **less and less time watching TV**.

---

### ➤ comparative repetition

Used to describe **continuous change**.

Examples:

- The problem is getting **worse and worse**.  
- Life is becoming **faster and faster**.

## Idioms & Phrasal Verbs

- **pick up** – improve or increase  
- **slow down** – become slower  
- **speed up** – become faster  
- **build up** – grow gradually  
- **fall apart** – collapse or stop working well

## Reading – How Daily Life Is Changing

Life today is very different from life twenty years ago. Technology has transformed how people work, communicate and relax.

Many people are becoming more and more dependent on smartphones. These devices allow us to work, shop and stay connected wherever we are. As a result, traditional habits are slowly disappearing.

For example, fewer people watch television at a fixed time every evening. Instead, they choose streaming services and watch programmes whenever they want. Printed newspapers are also becoming less and less common as more readers move online.

At the same time, the pace of life seems to be getting faster and faster. People expect instant replies to messages and quick solutions to problems. Businesses also need to speed up their services to keep customers satisfied.

Some experts believe this trend will continue. Others think people may eventually slow down and search for a better balance between technology and real life.
