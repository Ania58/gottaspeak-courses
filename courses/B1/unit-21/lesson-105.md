## Grammar Focus – Describing Recent Change

When we talk about **changes that happened recently and still affect the present**, we often use the **present perfect**.

### Structure

**have / has + past participle**

Examples:

- Online learning **has become** very popular.  
- Streaming platforms **have changed** the way people watch films.  
- Social media **has created** new types of jobs.

This tense connects **past changes with the present situation**.

- **take off** – suddenly become successful  
- **catch on** – become popular with many people  
- **blow up** – become famous very quickly  
- **spread quickly** – grow rapidly  
- **break through** – achieve success after difficulty

## Reading – The App That Suddenly Took Off

When the app “SnapTask” first appeared, very few people noticed it. The developers launched the platform quietly and hoped it would slowly attract users.

For several months, growth was slow. However, everything changed when a popular influencer mentioned the app in a video. Within days, thousands of people downloaded it.

Since then, the platform has grown rapidly. Millions of users have joined, and the company has expanded its services. What began as a small project has turned into a global business.

Experts say this kind of success often happens when a product solves a simple problem in a new way. Once people discover it, the idea can spread quickly and catch on worldwide.
