## Grammar Focus – used to vs get used to

These expressions help describe **change between the past and the present**.

### ➤ used to

Used for **past habits or states that are no longer true**.

Structure:  
**used to + base verb**

Examples:

- I **used to work** in an office.  
- People **used to write** letters more often.

---

### ➤ get used to

Means **become familiar or comfortable with something**.

Structure:  
**get used to + noun / -ing**

Examples:

- I **got used to working** remotely.  
- She **is getting used to living** in a big city.

---

### ➤ be used to

Means **something feels normal now**.

Examples:

- I **am used to working** late.  
- He **is used to travelling** for work.

## Idioms & Phrasal Verbs

- **turn things around** – improve a difficult situation  
- **step out of your comfort zone** – try something unfamiliar  
- **start from scratch** – begin something again from the beginning  
- **move on** – continue with life after a change  
- **figure out** – understand or solve something

## Reading – A Career Turning Point

Five years ago, Marta worked in a traditional office job. She used to spend nearly two hours every day commuting across the city.

At first, she believed this routine was normal. Everyone around her followed the same pattern. But over time, she started to feel exhausted and unmotivated.

Then the company introduced remote work. Marta found the change difficult at first. She had to get used to managing her time independently and organising her tasks without direct supervision.

However, after several months she became used to working from home. Her productivity improved and she discovered that she had more time for personal projects.

Looking back, Marta believes that this change was a turning point. It helped her rethink her priorities and build a healthier work–life balance.
