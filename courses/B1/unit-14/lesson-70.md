## Quick Review – Useful Language

### Talking about problems
- The main issue is…
- There seems to be a problem with…
- What worries me is…

### Making suggestions
- We should…
- We could…
- What if we…
- One possible solution is…

### Reacting
- That could work.
- I’m not sure that would work.
- I see your point, but…
- Maybe we could adjust it.

### Consequences
- If we…, it will…
- This could lead to…
- As a result…

## Case Study – Too Many Messages

Read the situation.

A small company has a communication problem.

Employees receive:
- 100+ emails a day  
- messages on three different apps  
- daily online meetings  

People feel stressed and distracted. Important information is often lost. Deadlines are missed.

The manager suggests adding **more meetings** to improve communication.

Many employees think this will make things worse.

You are asked to propose a better solution.

---

## Comprehension – Check Understanding

1. What is the main problem in the company?  
2. How does it affect employees?  
3. What solution does the manager suggest?  
4. Why might this solution fail?

---

## Language Practice – Choose the Best Option

1. The main problem __________ the number of messages employees receive.  
   (is / are)

2. Adding more meetings could lead __________ even more stress.  
   (to / for)

3. If people have fewer apps, communication __________ clearer.  
   (will be / was)

4. I’m not sure that __________ work in practice.  
   (would / will)

5. One possible solution __________ to use only one platform.  
   (is / are)

## Problem-Solving Task – Your Decision

Imagine you work in this company.

Do the tasks:

### Step 1 – Describe the problem
Write 2–3 sentences explaining the situation clearly.

### Step 2 – Suggest solutions
Write at least 3 ideas using:
- should
- could
- what if

### Step 3 – Evaluate consequences
For each idea, write one result:
- If we…, it will…
- This could lead to…

### Step 4 – Choose the best solution
Explain:
- which solution is best
- why it is realistic
- why other ideas are weaker

---

## Speaking Task – Out Loud Practice

Explain your final decision aloud as if you were talking to your team.

Include:
- the problem
- your solution
- two reasons
- one possible risk

Try to speak for 1–2 minutes without stopping.
