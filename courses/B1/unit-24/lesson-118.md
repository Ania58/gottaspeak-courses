## Language Focus – Talking About Uncertain Information

Sometimes we share information that is **not completely confirmed**.

We often use expressions that show **uncertainty**.

### Common expressions

**People say that…**

Example:  
People say that the company might open a new office.

**It seems that…**

Example:  
It seems that the meeting was cancelled.

**There are rumours that…**

Example:  
There are rumours that the team will change managers.

**It looks like…**

Example:  
It looks like the train is delayed again.

These expressions help us communicate information **without claiming it is definitely true**.

## Idioms & Phrasal Verbs

- **get the wrong idea** – misunderstand a situation  
- **mix something up** – confuse two things  
- **clear something up** – explain something so it becomes clear  
- **figure something out** – understand something  
- **spread around** – become widely known

Examples:

- I think we mixed up the dates.  
- The manager cleared up the confusion.

## Reading – The Office Rumour

One Monday morning, several employees arrived at work looking worried. Someone had heard that the company was planning to close one of its departments.

People quickly began discussing the situation. Some employees said the management had already made the decision, while others believed it was only a rumour.

As the morning continued, the story became more dramatic. One person claimed that several employees would lose their jobs.

Later that day, the director called a short meeting to clear up the confusion. He explained that the company was reorganising some teams, but nobody was losing their job.

In the end, everyone realised that the rumour had grown because people misunderstood a simple internal message.
