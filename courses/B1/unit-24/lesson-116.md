## Grammar Focus – Reporting What Someone Said

When we tell someone what another person said, we often use **reported speech**.

Instead of repeating the exact words, we **describe the message**.

### Direct speech

> Maria: "I'm tired today."

### Reported speech

Maria said **she was tired that day**.

---

### Common reporting verbs

- **say**
- **tell**
- **explain**
- **mention**
- **point out**

Examples:

- He said he was busy.  
- She told me she had finished the report.  
- They explained that the meeting had been cancelled.

---

### Time changes in reported speech

Sometimes time expressions change.

| Direct speech | Reported speech |
|---|---|
| today | that day |
| tomorrow | the next day |
| yesterday | the day before |

Example:

> "I'll call you tomorrow."

She said she **would call me the next day**.

## Idioms & Phrasal Verbs

- **bring something up** – start talking about something  
- **point something out** – explain or highlight something  
- **pass something on** – give information to another person  
- **clear something up** – explain something so it becomes clear  
- **find something out** – discover information

Examples:

- She brought up the issue during the meeting.  
- He pointed out an important mistake.

## Reading – The Confusing Message

Last week, Daniel received a message from his manager while he was working from home.

The manager wrote that the team meeting had moved to Thursday afternoon. Daniel understood the message and planned his schedule around it.

Later that day, one of his colleagues called and asked why Daniel had not joined the meeting. Daniel was confused because he believed the meeting had been postponed.

After checking the message again, he realised what had happened. His manager had written that the meeting **might move** to Thursday, not that it **had moved**.

Daniel explained the misunderstanding to the team and joined the meeting late. Fortunately, everyone laughed about the situation.
