## Language Focus – Checking Information

When we want to **confirm information**, we often use **question tags** or short confirmation questions.

### Question tags

These are short questions at the end of a sentence.

Examples:

- The meeting is tomorrow, **isn't it?**
- You sent the report already, **didn't you?**
- She works in the marketing department, **doesn't she?**

They help us **check if information is correct**.

### Confirmation expressions

We also use short questions to confirm something:

- **Right?**  
- **Correct?**  
- **Is that right?**  
- **Do you mean…?**

Example:

So the presentation starts at 10 a.m., **right?**

- **make sure**
- **check with someone**

Example:

---

## Idioms & Phrasal Verbs

**check something out** – investigate or verify something  
**make sure** – confirm something is correct  
**look over something** – review something carefully  
**get back to someone** – respond later with information  

Examples:

- I'll check it out and tell you tomorrow.  
- Please look over the document before sending it.

## Reading – The Wrong Date

Emma was organizing a training session for her colleagues. She sent an email saying that the training would happen on **Friday morning at 9:00**.

Later that day, one colleague asked her,  
"The training is on Thursday, **isn't it?**"

Emma was surprised.

Apparently, another employee had misunderstood the message and told several people the wrong date.

To avoid more confusion, Emma decided to double-check everything. She sent a new email confirming the correct details.

She wrote:  
"Just to confirm, the training session will take place on **Friday at 9:00 a.m.** Please make sure you are available."

After that message, everyone understood the correct schedule.
