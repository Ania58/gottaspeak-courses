## 4. Reading – Office Miscommunication

Last week, several employees believed that the company was planning to move its headquarters to another city. Apparently, someone had misunderstood a short internal message.

People began discussing the situation and spreading rumours. Some employees even started looking for new jobs because they thought the company would close their department.

However, according to the director, the message only referred to opening a **small additional office**, not moving the entire company.

The director held a meeting to clarify the situation and clear up the confusion. After that meeting, employees finally understood what the original message meant.

Answer in full sentences.

2. Why did the misunderstanding happen?
3. What did some employees do because of the rumour?
4. What was the real situation?
5. How did the director solve the problem?

## 8. Grammar – Reporting Information
