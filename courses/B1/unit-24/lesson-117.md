## Language Focus – Reporting Information

When we share information that we heard from someone else, we often use expressions that show **the source of the information**.

### Common expressions

**I heard that…**

Example:  
I heard that the meeting was postponed.

**Apparently…**

Example:  
Apparently, the company is planning to open a new office.

**According to…**

Example:  
According to the manager, the project will start next month.

**Someone told me that…**

Example:  
Someone told me that the train workers might strike tomorrow.

These expressions help listeners understand that the information **comes from another source**.

## Idioms & Phrasal Verbs

- **find out** – discover information  
- **spread the word** – tell many people something  
- **pass something on** – give information to another person  
- **get the message** – understand information  
- **look into something** – investigate something

Examples:

- We finally found out what had happened.  
- She spread the word about the event.

## Reading – News Around the Office

Yesterday morning there was a lot of confusion in the office. Several employees had heard that the company was planning major changes.

Apparently, the management team was considering opening a new branch in another city. According to one employee, some departments might move there next year.

However, nobody was completely sure what the plan actually was. Some people believed the changes would happen immediately, while others thought it was only an idea.

Later that afternoon, the director held a short meeting and explained the situation clearly. He confirmed that the company was exploring the possibility of expansion, but no final decisions had been made yet.

After the meeting, employees finally understood what was really happening.
