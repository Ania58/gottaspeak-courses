## Language Focus – Correcting Information

Sometimes we need to **correct something that someone misunderstood**.

Here are common expressions used in everyday communication.

### Actually…

Example:  
Actually, the meeting is tomorrow, not today.

### In fact…

Example:  
In fact, the project has already started.

### What I meant was…

Example:  
What I meant was that we should change the plan slightly.

### That's not exactly what I said.

Example:  
That's not exactly what I said. I suggested waiting a bit longer.

These expressions help us **clarify information politely**.

## Idioms & Phrasal Verbs

**clear something up** – explain something so it becomes clear  
**point something out** – mention an important detail  
**get something across** – communicate an idea successfully  
**sort something out** – resolve a problem  

Examples:

- The teacher cleared up the confusion.  
- She pointed out an important mistake.

## Reading – A Confusing Email

Yesterday morning, Daniel sent an email to his team about an upcoming project meeting. In the message, he wrote that the meeting would happen "later this week."

Several employees interpreted this differently. Some thought the meeting was on Wednesday, while others believed it was scheduled for Friday.

When Daniel arrived at the office, he noticed the confusion. Actually, he had planned the meeting for Thursday afternoon.

He quickly sent another message to the team to clarify the situation. In fact, the meeting had always been scheduled for Thursday, but his first email had not been clear enough.

After receiving the second message, everyone understood the correct plan.
