## Idioms & Phrasal Verbs Focus

### Idioms

- **put words in someone’s mouth** – say that someone said something they didn’t  
- **take something out of context** – remove words from their original situation  
- **speak one’s mind** – say what you really think  
- **hear something through the grapevine** – hear news informally

### Phrasal Verbs

- **speak up** – express an opinion, especially when others are silent  
- **pass on** (information) – give information to someone else  
- **clear up** (a misunderstanding) – make something understood  
- **go back on** (a promise) – not do what you promised

## Reading: When Communication Goes Wrong

In modern communication, misunderstandings happen more often than people expect. Messages are shared quickly, repeated by others and sometimes passed on without context. As a result, the original meaning can easily be lost.

Public figures often complain encourage that their words are taken out of context. A single sentence, when separated from a longer explanation, may appear more extreme or controversial than it really was. When this happens, speakers may feel that words were put in their mouth.

Social media increases this risk. Information is often heard through the grapevine rather than from original sources. People react before checking facts, and misunderstandings spread rapidly. Attempts to clear up the situation may come too late.

Clear communication requires responsibility from both speakers and listeners. Speaking one’s mind is important, but so is listening carefully. When people speak up to clarify and respect context, communication becomes more accurate and less damaging.
