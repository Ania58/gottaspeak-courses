---

# Language Focus – Talking About Unexpected Situations

When something unexpected happens, English speakers often explain:

1. **what happened**
2. **why the plan changed**
3. **what they decided to do instead**

Example:

We planned to travel on Friday, but the flight was cancelled.  
So we had to change our plans and leave on Saturday.

---

### Useful expressions

Something came up.  
Our plans changed.  
We had to change plans.  
Things didn’t go as planned.  
The plan fell through.

Example:

Our meeting fell through because the manager was sick.

# Phrasal Verbs

**come up**

something unexpected happens

Example  
Something came up at work.

---

**fall through**

a plan fails

Example  
Our travel plans fell through.

---

**work out**

end successfully

Example  
In the end, everything worked out.

---

# Idioms

**at the last minute**

very late, just before something happens

Example  
The meeting was cancelled at the last minute.

---

**expect the unexpected**

be prepared for surprises

Example  
When you travel, you should expect the unexpected.

# Reading – A Travel Problem

Last weekend, Emma planned a short trip to another city with her friends. They bought train tickets and booked a hotel.

However, on the morning of the trip, they received a message saying the train was cancelled due to technical problems.

At first, they were disappointed because everything had been planned carefully.

Instead of cancelling the trip completely, they quickly searched for another option. They found a bus leaving later that day.

The journey took longer than expected, but in the end the trip worked out well. They still enjoyed the weekend together.

Emma later said, “When you travel, you really have to expect the unexpected.”
