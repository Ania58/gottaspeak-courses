# Language Focus – Making Complaints Politely

When complaining in English, people usually try to sound **polite and calm**.

### Useful expressions

**I'm afraid there's a problem with…**

Example  
I'm afraid there's a problem with my order.

---

**I'm not happy with…**

Example  
I'm not happy with the service today.

---

**I think there might be a mistake.**

Example  
I think there might be a mistake on this bill.

---

**Could you check this, please?**

Example  
Could you check this for me, please?

---

# Responding to Complaints

If someone complains, these responses help solve the situation.

### Useful responses

I'm sorry about that.  
Let me check.  
We'll sort it out.  
I'll look into it right away.  
Thanks for letting us know.

Example:

Customer: I'm afraid there's a problem with my order.  
Staff: I'm sorry about that. Let me check.

# Reading – A Restaurant Problem

Last Saturday, Daniel went to a restaurant with his friends. When the food arrived, he noticed that his order was wrong.

Instead of the vegetarian pasta he had ordered, the waiter brought a chicken dish.

Daniel politely called the waiter and said,  
"I'm afraid there's a problem with my order."

The waiter apologized immediately.

"I'm very sorry about that. I'll sort it out right away."

A few minutes later, the correct dish arrived. The waiter also offered Daniel a free drink as an apology.

In the end, Daniel was happy with how the restaurant dealt with the problem.
