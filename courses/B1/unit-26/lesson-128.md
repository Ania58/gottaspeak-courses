# Language Focus – Asking for Help

In English, people often ask for help **politely and indirectly**.

### Useful expressions

**Can you help me with this?**

Example  
Can you help me with this report?

---

**Could you give me a hand?**

Example  
Could you give me a hand with these boxes?

---

**Would you mind helping me?**

Example  
Would you mind helping me with this form?

---

**I could really use some help.**

Example  
I could really use some help with this problem.

---

# Asking for Advice

Sometimes we don't need help with a task — we need **advice**.

### Useful expressions

**What would you do?**

Example  
What would you do in this situation?

---

**Do you have any advice?**

Example  
Do you have any advice for preparing presentations?

---

**What do you think I should do?**

Example  
What do you think I should do about this problem?

# Phrasal Verbs

**figure out**

understand or solve something

Example  
We need to figure out what went wrong.

---

**help out**

help someone informally

Example  
My friend helped me out with my homework.

---

**work something out**

find a solution

Example  
We worked out a solution together.

---

# Idioms

**lend a hand**

help someone

Example  
Could you lend me a hand with this?

---

**in the same boat**

in the same difficult situation

Example  
We're all in the same boat during exam season.

# Reading – Asking for Advice

Maria had a problem at work. Her manager asked her to give a presentation to an important client.

Although Maria was good at her job, she felt nervous about speaking in front of people.

She decided to ask her colleague Daniel for advice.

"Do you have any advice for presentations?" she asked.

Daniel smiled and said, "The most important thing is preparation. Practice a few times before the meeting."

He also suggested keeping the slides simple and speaking slowly.

Maria followed his advice. The presentation went very well, and the client was impressed.
