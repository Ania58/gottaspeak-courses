# Language Focus – Sequencing Instructions

When giving instructions, English speakers often organize information in **clear steps**.

### Sequencing expressions

**First…**

Example  
First, open the document.

---

**Then…**

Example  
Then add the information you need.

---

**After that…**

Example  
After that, check the details carefully.

---

**Finally…**

Example  
Finally, send the file to the team.

These expressions help listeners **follow the process easily**.

---

# Useful Instruction Expressions

Make sure you…  
Don't forget to…  
Be careful when…  
The next step is…

Example:

Make sure you save the document before closing the program.

# Phrasal Verbs

**set up**

prepare or install something

Example  
First, set up the account.

---

**log in**

enter a system

Example  
Then log in with your password.

---

**turn on**

activate a device

Example  
Turn on the computer before starting.

---

# Idioms

**step by step**

gradually and clearly

Example  
The instructor explained everything step by step.

---

**get the hang of something**

learn how to do something

Example  
After a few tries, you'll get the hang of it.

# Reading – Explaining a Task

James recently started a new job in an office. On his first day, his colleague Anna showed him how to use the company's project management system.

"First, log in with your company email," Anna explained.

"Then open the project dashboard and check your assigned tasks."

After that, she showed him how to update task progress and upload documents.

"Finally," she said, "make sure you save everything before logging out."

James followed the instructions step by step. At first it seemed complicated, but after practicing a few times, he got the hang of it.
