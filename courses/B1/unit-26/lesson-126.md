# Language Focus – Talking About Problems

When we explain a problem, we usually describe:

1. **what happened**
2. **what the problem was**
3. **how we solved it**

Example:

Yesterday my laptop stopped working while I was finishing a report.  
I restarted it and finally fixed the problem.

---

### Useful expressions

Something went wrong.  
There was a problem with…  
It stopped working.  
I ran into a problem.  
I had to fix it.

Example:

There was a problem with my internet connection yesterday.

# Vocabulary – Everyday Problems

problem  
mistake

Useful expressions:

fix a problem  
solve a problem  
deal with a situation  
find a solution  

Example:

They quickly found a solution to the problem.

---

# Phrasal Verbs

**run into a problem**

experience a difficulty

Example  
We ran into a problem during the meeting.

---

**sort something out**

solve a problem

Example  
Don't worry. We'll sort it out.

---

**deal with**

handle a situation

Example  
She dealt with the issue calmly.

---

# Idioms

**things go wrong**

problems happen

Example  
Sometimes things go wrong during a project.

---

**fix something in no time**

solve something very quickly

Example  
The technician fixed the computer in no time.

# Reading – A Small Problem

Yesterday morning, Alex was preparing an important presentation for work. While he was checking the slides, his computer suddenly froze.

At first, he tried restarting it, but the screen stayed black. Alex started to worry because the meeting was in less than an hour.

Fortunately, his colleague Sarah came over and helped him. She suggested using another computer and transferring the file from a USB drive.

In the end, Alex gave the presentation on time. The situation was stressful, but they managed to sort it out.
