# Language Focus – Small Talk Questions

Small talk usually begins with **simple open questions**.

These questions help people **start relaxed conversations**.

### Common questions

How’s your day going?

Example  
How’s your day going so far?

---

What have you been up to?

Example  
What have you been up to this week?

---

Any plans for the weekend?

Example  
Any plans for the weekend?

---

How was your weekend?

Example  
How was your weekend?

---

Have you been busy lately?

Example  
Have you been busy lately?

# Phrasal Verbs

**run into someone**

meet someone unexpectedly

Example  
I ran into an old colleague yesterday.

---

**hang out**

spend time with someone

Example  
We hung out with friends last weekend.

---

**drop by**

visit someone briefly

Example  
My neighbour dropped by for a quick chat.

---

# Idioms

**long time no see**

used when meeting someone after a long time

Example  
Long time no see! How have you been?

---

**keep in touch**

continue communicating with someone

Example  
We should keep in touch after the project ends.

# Reading – A Quick Conversation

Yesterday morning, Daniel stopped at a small café before work. While waiting for his coffee, he noticed a colleague from another department.

"Hey, long time no see!" Daniel said.

His colleague smiled.

"Yeah, it's been a while. How have you been?"

Daniel replied, "Pretty busy lately. We've been working on a new project."

They talked for a few minutes about work and their weekend plans.

Before leaving, Daniel said, "We should grab coffee again sometime."

"Definitely," his colleague replied.

It was only a short conversation, but it helped them stay connected.
