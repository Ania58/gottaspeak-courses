# Language Focus – Ending Conversations

In English, people usually **end conversations politely** instead of stopping suddenly.

These expressions help make the ending **friendly and natural**.

### Useful expressions

**I should get going.**

Example  
I should get going. I have an early meeting tomorrow.

---

**It was nice talking to you.**

Example  
It was nice talking to you. Let's do this again sometime.

---

**Let's catch up later.**

Example  
Let's catch up later and talk more about your trip.

---

**I'll see you around.**

Example  
I'll see you around. Take care!

---

**Anyway, I won't keep you any longer.**

Example  
Anyway, I won't keep you any longer. Good luck with your project.

# Phrasal Verbs

**head out**

leave a place

Example  
I think I'll head out soon.

---

**wrap up**

finish something

Example  
Let's wrap up this discussion.

---

**catch up**

talk again later

Example  
We should catch up next week.

---

# Idioms

**call it a day**

decide to stop working or talking

Example  
It's getting late. Let's call it a day.

---

**be on the same page**

understand something in the same way

Example  
Before we finish, let's make sure we're on the same page.

# Reading – Ending a Conversation

Yesterday evening, Daniel met his friend Laura for coffee. They talked about work, travel, and their plans for the weekend.

After about an hour, Daniel looked at his watch.

"I should get going," he said. "I have to wake up early tomorrow."

Laura smiled.

"It was really nice catching up," she replied.

Daniel stood up and put on his coat.

"Let's catch up again next week," he said.

"Sure," Laura answered. "See you around!"

They left the café feeling happy about their conversation.
