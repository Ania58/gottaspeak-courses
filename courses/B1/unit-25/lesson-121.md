# Language Focus – Conversation Starters

When starting a conversation, English speakers often use **soft introductions**.

These expressions make conversation sound **natural and friendly**.

### Useful openers

**By the way…**

Example  
By the way, did you see the new café near the station?

---

**Speaking of…**

Example  
Speaking of travel, have you planned your summer holiday yet?

---

**Have you heard about…?**

Example  
Have you heard about the new project at work?

---

**I was wondering if…**

Example  
I was wondering if you had time to talk later.

---

**Guess what?**

Example  
Guess what? I finally finished that big project.

Example:

---

# Phrasal Verbs

**bring up** – introduce a topic

Example  
He brought up the idea of a team meeting.

---

**run into someone**

meet someone unexpectedly

Example  
I ran into my old teacher yesterday.

---

**catch up**

talk with someone after a long time

Example  
We met for coffee to catch up.

---

# Idioms

**break the ice**

start a conversation in a social situation

Example  
He told a joke to break the ice.

---

**get the conversation going**

start people talking

Example  
A simple question can get the conversation going.

# Reading – Starting a Conversation

Last week, Maria attended a networking event for students and young professionals. At first, she felt a little nervous because she didn't know anyone there.

She decided to start with small talk. She walked up to someone and said, "Hi, I think we were in the same presentation earlier."

The other person smiled and replied, "Yes, I think so."

After that, the conversation became easier. They talked about their studies, their future plans, and even discovered that they both liked travelling.

Later that evening, Maria realized that starting the conversation had been the hardest part.
