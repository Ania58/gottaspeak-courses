# Language Focus – Showing Interest

To keep a conversation going, it’s important to **react and ask follow-up questions**.

These reactions show that you are listening.

### Reaction expressions

Really?  
That sounds interesting.  
No way!  
That's great.  
I didn't know that.

Example:

I just came back from Italy.

→ Really? How was it?

---

### Follow-up questions

Follow-up questions help the conversation continue.

Examples:

What happened next?  
How did that happen?  
Why did you decide to do that?  
How did you feel about it?

Example:

I started learning Japanese last year.

→ That’s interesting. Why did you decide to learn it?

# Phrasal Verbs

**go on**

continue speaking

Example  
Go on, what happened after that?

---

**bring something up**

introduce a topic

Example  
She brought up the idea of travelling together.

---

**talk over**

discuss something

Example  
Let's talk it over later.

---

# Idioms

**tell me about it**

used when you strongly agree with someone

Example  
"This week has been so stressful."  
"Tell me about it!"

---

**you can say that again**

used when you completely agree

Example  
"This traffic is terrible."  
"You can say that again."

# Reading – A Conversation That Continued

Last Friday, Tom met his old classmate Daniel at a café. They hadn’t seen each other in years.

At first, the conversation was simple.

“How have you been?” Tom asked.

Daniel started telling him about his new job in another city. Tom listened carefully and reacted with interest.

“Really? That sounds exciting,” he said. “What kind of projects are you working on?”

Daniel explained that he was working on several international projects. The conversation continued easily because Tom kept asking questions and showing interest.

By the end of the evening, they realized they had talked for nearly three hours.
