# Language Focus – Moving to a New Topic

In natural conversation, people often **shift topics**.  
English speakers usually signal this change with **discourse markers**.

These small expressions help conversations feel **natural and polite**.

---

### Common Topic-Shift Expressions

**Anyway…**  
Used to return to the main topic or move forward.

Example  
Anyway, we should probably talk about the meeting tomorrow.

---

**By the way…**  
Used to introduce new information.

Example  
By the way, did you finish the report?

---

**Speaking of…**  
Used when the new topic is related to something just mentioned.

Example  
Speaking of travel, have you planned your holiday yet?

---

**That reminds me…**  
Used when something makes you remember another topic.

Example  
That reminds me, I need to send you those photos.

# Phrasal Verbs

**move on**  
change to another subject

Example  
Let’s move on to the next topic.

---

**bring up**  
introduce a topic

Example  
She brought up the issue during the meeting.

---

**come up**  
appear in conversation

Example  
The problem came up during the discussion.

---

# Idioms

**get back to the point**

return to the main topic

Example  
Let’s get back to the point.

---

**change the subject**

move to another topic

Example  
He quickly changed the subject.

# Reading – A Conversation Shift

Last night, Emma and her colleague Lucas were talking after work.

At first, they discussed a project deadline that was coming up next week. Lucas explained some problems the team had encountered.

After a few minutes, Emma said,  
“Anyway, we’ll solve it tomorrow.”

Then she added,  
“By the way, have you seen the new café near the office?”

The conversation quickly became lighter. They started talking about food, restaurants, and weekend plans.

Lucas laughed and said,  
“Speaking of weekends, are you doing anything interesting?”

Emma smiled.  
“That reminds me – I promised to help my sister move.”

The conversation had completely shifted from work to personal plans.
