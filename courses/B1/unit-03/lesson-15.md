## Language Focus: Expressing Opinions and Proposing Solutions (8 min)

When discussing society and change, people often:
- describe **current trends**,  
- explain **causes and consequences**,  
- suggest **realistic solutions**,  
- express agreement or concern.

Useful language:
- **People are becoming more aware of…**
- **If this continues, it will lead to…**
- **We need to deal with this problem before it gets worse.**
- **raise awareness**
- **make a difference**
- **cut down on**
- **turn a blind eye**

Being clear and respectful helps conversations lead to action, not conflict.

## Reading: Building a Responsible Future Together (16–18 min)

Modern societies face complex challenges that cannot be solved by individuals alone. Climate change, inequality, mental health issues and rapid technological development are all influencing the way people live and interact. These changes are happening quickly, and many people feel uncertain about the future.

At the same time, awareness is growing. People are becoming more willing to speak up, point out problems and demand change. Social movements, community initiatives and educational campaigns are playing an important role in shaping public attitudes. When individuals feel that their actions matter, motivation increases.

However, real progress requires cooperation. If governments, institutions and citizens work together, solutions become more realistic. Cutting down on harmful behaviours, investing in education and supporting vulnerable groups can lead to long-term improvement. As a result, trust in society may slowly return.

The future of society depends not only on policies, but also on everyday choices. When people refuse to turn a blind eye and take responsibility, even small actions can make a meaningful difference.
