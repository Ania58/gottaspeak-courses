## Language Focus – Narrative Connectors

When telling stories, we use **connectors** to help the listener follow the sequence of events.

Common connectors:

**suddenly** – something unexpected happens  
**after that** – the next step in the story  
**meanwhile** – something happens at the same time  
**eventually** – the final result after some time  

Examples:

- We were waiting for the train when **suddenly** the lights went out.  
- **After that**, everyone started looking for information.  
- **Meanwhile**, the staff were trying to fix the problem.  
- **Eventually**, the train arrived and the journey continued.

These connectors help stories feel **more natural and dynamic**.

## Idioms & Phrasal Verbs

- **run into someone** – meet someone unexpectedly  
- **show up** – arrive unexpectedly  
- **turn up** – appear or arrive  
- **end up doing something** – finally do something after a situation develops  
- **figure something out** – find a solution

Examples:

- I ran into my old teacher at the station.  
- He didn’t show up for the meeting.  
- We eventually figured out what had happened.

## Reading – The Missing Presentation

Emma had spent several days preparing an important presentation for work. She had checked the slides many times and felt confident about the meeting.

On the morning of the presentation, she arrived early at the office and opened her laptop. Suddenly, she realised that the presentation file was missing.

At first she thought she had saved it on the wrong device. Meanwhile, her colleagues were arriving in the meeting room and waiting for the presentation to begin.

Emma quickly searched through her email and cloud storage. After that, she remembered that she had sent a copy of the presentation to her manager the previous evening.

Her manager opened the email and shared the file with the team. Eventually, the meeting started only ten minutes late.

Emma was relieved. She had learned that keeping backup copies of important files can save a lot of stress.
