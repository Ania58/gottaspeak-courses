## Grammar Focus – Introducing Past Perfect

When we tell stories, we sometimes need to talk about **something that happened before another past event**.

We use **past perfect** for that.

Structure:

**had + past participle**

Examples:

- I realised I **had forgotten** my keys.  
- She noticed someone **had taken** her seat.  
- They discovered the train **had already left**.

Timeline example:

I arrived at the station → but the train **had already left**.

Past perfect helps us explain **what happened earlier in the story**.

## Idioms & Phrasal Verbs

- **learn the hard way** – learn through a difficult experience  
- **one thing led to another** – events happened one after another  
- **before I knew it** – something happened very quickly  
- **run into someone** – meet someone unexpectedly  
- **end up doing something** – finally do something after a situation develops

Examples:

- Before I knew it, we were talking for hours.  
- I ran into an old friend at the airport.

## Reading – The Day Everything Went Wrong

Last summer, Daniel travelled to Lisbon for a short holiday. He had planned the trip carefully and expected everything to go smoothly.

However, when he arrived at the airport, he suddenly realised he **had forgotten** his passport at home.

At first, he panicked. The flight was leaving in two hours, and returning home would take at least an hour. For a moment, he thought the trip was completely ruined.

Then something unexpected happened. While he was explaining the situation to the airline staff, he ran into a colleague from work who lived near the airport. She offered to drive him home quickly.

One thing led to another, and somehow Daniel managed to return with his passport just in time. He boarded the plane a few minutes before departure.

Later, he laughed about the situation. But he also learned the hard way that checking your documents before leaving home is always a good idea.

## Grammar Practice – Choose the Correct Form

Choose the option that completes the sentence logically.

1. When I arrived at the cinema, the film ______ already started.  
   (had / was)

2. She realised she ______ left her phone in the taxi.  
   (has / had)

3. We arrived late because the train ______ delayed earlier.  
   (was / had been)

4. He understood the situation only after someone ______ explained it.  
   (had / was)

5. They were tired because they ______ travelled all night.  
   (have / had)
