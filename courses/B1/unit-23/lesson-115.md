## Language Focus – Talking About Turning Points

Sometimes one moment or decision can **change the direction of our life**.  
We use certain expressions to describe these moments.

### Common expressions

**It was a turning point.**  
Example:  
Getting that job was a turning point in her career.

**That’s when everything changed.**  
Example:  
That’s when everything changed for me.

**From that moment on…**  
Example:  
From that moment on, he started working harder.

**It opened new opportunities.**  
Example:  
The experience opened many opportunities later.

These expressions help speakers describe **important life transitions**.

## Idioms & Phrasal Verbs

- **take a chance** – try something risky or uncertain  
- **step out of your comfort zone** – try something new or difficult  
- **give something a shot** – try something  
- **turn things around** – improve a bad situation  
- **end up doing something** – finally do something after events develop

Examples:

- She decided to give the idea a shot.  
- That decision helped him turn things around.

## Reading – A Chance Conversation

When Sofia finished university, she wasn’t sure what career to choose. She had studied economics, but she didn’t feel excited about working in a typical office job.

One evening, she met a friend who worked in a small technology start-up. During their conversation, the friend explained how the company was developing educational apps.

Sofia became curious and decided to give the idea a shot. She started helping the team with small projects in her free time.

At first, everything was new and challenging. However, she quickly realised she enjoyed solving problems and designing learning tools.

That conversation became a turning point in her life. From that moment on, Sofia focused on developing educational technology, and a few years later she became a project manager in the company.
