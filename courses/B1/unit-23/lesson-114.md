## Language Focus – How Stories Are Built

Good stories usually follow a **clear structure**.

### 1. Setting  
Introduce the situation.

Example:  
Last winter I was travelling through Italy with a friend.

### 2. The Situation Changes  
Something unexpected happens.

Example:  
While we were waiting for the train, we realised we had boarded the wrong platform.

### 3. Turning Point  
The most important moment.

Example:  
Suddenly the train started moving and we understood we had made a mistake.

### 4. Resolution  
The story ends and the situation is solved.

Example:  
Eventually we got off at the next station and found the correct train.

---

## Grammar Reminder – Narrative Tenses

When telling stories we often combine different past forms.

**Past simple** – main events  
- We missed the train.

**Past continuous** – background actions  
- We were waiting on the platform.

**Past perfect** – earlier events  
- We had checked the timetable incorrectly.

Example story sentence:

While we **were waiting** for the train, we realised we **had chosen** the wrong platform.

## Idioms & Phrasal Verbs

- **figure something out** – understand or solve something  
- **work something out** – find a solution  
- **get through something** – survive a difficult situation  
- **make up a story** – invent a story

Examples:

- We eventually figured out what had happened.  
- Somehow we got through the situation.

## Reading – The Wrong Train

A few years ago, I was travelling through Germany with two friends. We were excited because it was the first long trip we had taken together.

We arrived at the station early and were waiting for our train when something strange happened. The platform number suddenly changed on the information screen.

Meanwhile, another train arrived on the platform and people started boarding quickly. We thought it was our train, so we got on without checking carefully.

About twenty minutes later, we realised something was wrong. The train was moving in the opposite direction from our destination.

At that moment we understood that we had taken the wrong train.

Eventually we got off at the next station, laughed about the situation and found the correct train.

Even today we still tell that story when we travel together.
