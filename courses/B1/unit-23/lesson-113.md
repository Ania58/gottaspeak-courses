## Language Focus – Expressing Lessons

When we talk about experiences, we often explain **what we learned from them**.

Common structures:

**What I learned was…**

Example:  
What I learned was that preparation is essential.

**The important thing is…**

Example:  
The important thing is to stay calm in stressful situations.

**From that moment on…**

Example:  
From that moment on, I always double-check my work.

**That experience taught me that…**

Example:  
That experience taught me that patience is important.

These expressions help speakers **reflect on experiences and explain personal growth**.

## Idioms & Phrasal Verbs

- **live and learn** – we learn through experience  
- **a wake-up call** – a moment that makes you realise something important  
- **figure something out** – find a solution or understand something  
- **bounce back** – recover from a difficult situation  
- **mess something up** – do something badly

Examples:

- Failing the exam was a wake-up call.  
- She bounced back after losing the job.

## Reading – A Small Mistake, A Big Lesson

When Alex started his first job, he wanted to impress everyone. He worked quickly and tried to complete tasks as fast as possible.

One day, his manager asked him to prepare an important report for a client meeting. Alex finished the report in a hurry and sent it immediately.

Later that afternoon, his manager called him into the office. Alex quickly realised he had made several small mistakes in the document.

At first he felt embarrassed and worried about the situation. However, his manager stayed calm and explained that mistakes happen, especially when people are new.

That experience taught Alex an important lesson. Speed is useful, but accuracy matters more. From that moment on, he always reviewed his work carefully before sending it.

Over time, Alex became one of the most reliable employees in the team.
