## Grammar Focus: Gerunds and Infinitives (8 min)

Some verbs are followed by a **gerund (-ing)**, others by an **infinitive (to + verb)**.  
They are very common when talking about **emotions, preferences and decisions**.

### Verbs followed by a gerund:
- enjoy  
- avoid  
- keep  
- consider  

> I enjoy talking openly about problems.  
> She avoids arguing when she’s tired.

### Verbs followed by an infinitive:
- decide  
- hope  
- want  
- refuse  

> He decided to talk things over.  
> They want to improve communication.

Some verbs can be followed by **both forms** with little or no change in meaning (at B1 level):
- like  
- love  
- hate  

> I like talking about feelings.  
> I like to talk about feelings.

## Reading: Choosing How to Communicate (16–18 min)

Communication in relationships often depends on conscious choices. People constantly decide whether to speak openly, stay silent or avoid uncomfortable topics. These decisions influence trust, understanding and emotional connection.

Many adults enjoy sharing their thoughts but avoid talking about feelings that seem difficult or risky. Fear of conflict or rejection can stop people from opening up. At the same time, refusing to communicate often leads to misunderstandings and emotional distance.

Healthy relationships require effort and intention. Deciding to listen carefully, choosing honest words and being willing to apologise can transform communication. Even small changes, such as avoiding negative reactions or making time to talk, have a strong impact over time.

Understanding why we choose certain communication patterns helps improve relationships. When people become aware of their habits and preferences, they are better prepared to build respectful and supportive connections.
