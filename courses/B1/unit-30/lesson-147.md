## 6. Reading – Are First Impressions Always Right?

Read the text.

First impressions can be very powerful. When we meet someone for the first time, we often form an opinion within a few seconds. This opinion is usually based on appearance, body language, and the way the person speaks. For example, someone who smiles and makes eye contact may come across as friendly and confident.

However, first impressions are not always accurate. People may feel nervous when meeting someone new, which can affect their behaviour. A shy person might seem unfriendly, while someone who talks a lot might appear confident but actually feel insecure.

Research shows that first impressions can influence our decisions, especially in situations like job interviews or social events. Employers often decide quickly whether they like a candidate, and this can affect the final result.

On the other hand, relationships that develop over time are often based on deeper understanding. As we get to know someone better, we may change our opinion. Many people have experienced meeting someone they didn’t like at first but later became good friends with.

This is why it is important not to judge people too quickly. Giving others time to show their true personality can lead to stronger and more meaningful relationships.
