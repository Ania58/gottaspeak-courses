## Reading – When Good Ideas Fail

Many workplace initiatives are introduced with positive intentions. However, even well-designed ideas can fail if they are poorly communicated.

Last year, a company launched a flexible working policy which allowed employees to choose their schedules. Although the system was designed to improve work–life balance, confusion quickly appeared. Managers who were responsible for approving requests used different criteria. As a result, employees felt the process was unfair.

What made the situation worse was the lack of clear explanation. The policy document, which had been written in technical language, was difficult to understand. In fact, many staff members admitted they had never read it fully.

Despite several attempts to solve the problem, dissatisfaction continued. Only after workshops were organised and feedback was actively collected did the situation begin to improve. What mattered most was not the policy itself, but how it was presented and discussed.

The case demonstrates that change requires more than good ideas. Above all, it demands clarity, consistency and communication.

## Grammar & Discourse Review – Choose the Best Option

1. The policy failed __________ it was badly explained.  
   (because / despite)

2. The document, __________ was written in technical language, confused employees.  
   (which / where)

3. Managers used different criteria. __________, staff felt the system was unfair.  
   (However / As a result)

4. __________ several meetings, the problem remained unsolved.  
   (Although / Despite)

5. __________, clear communication was the key factor.  
   (Above all / In spite of)

## Speaking Task – Structured Explanation

Choose one situation:

- a change at work or school  
- a social problem  
- a personal decision  
- a system you use regularly  

Explain it clearly:

1. what happened  
2. why it happened  
3. what the result was  
4. what mattered most  

Use:
- reasons and results  
- contrast  
- one relative clause  
- one emphasis expression  

---

## Writing Task – Organised Text

Write a structured text (170–200 words):

**“Why a well-intended idea did not work as expected.”**

Use:
- linking words (because, as a result, however…)  
- contrast (although, despite…)  
- at least **two relative clauses**  
- emphasis expressions (what matters most, above all…)
