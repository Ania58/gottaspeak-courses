# Language Focus – Talking About Balance

We often describe balance using expressions like:

too much  
not enough  
spend time doing something  

Example:

I spend too much time online.

Example:

She doesn’t spend enough time with friends.

---

# Phrasal Verbs

**cut down on**

reduce something

Example  
I want to cut down on screen time.

---

**switch off**

stop using technology / relax

Example  
It’s important to switch off after work.

---

**spend time on**

use time doing something

Example  
He spends a lot of time on social media.

---

**hang out**

spend time with people

Example  
I like to hang out with friends on weekends.

---

# Idioms

**in real life**

in the real world, not online

Example  
We finally met in real life.

---

**too much of a good thing**

something good becomes bad if you have too much of it

Example  
Social media is great, but it can be too much of a good thing.

---

**time flies**

time passes quickly

Example  
Time flies when you’re having fun offline.

---

**disconnect from something**

stop being involved with something

Example  
It’s important to disconnect from work sometimes.

---

**find a balance**

manage different parts of life well

Example  
You need to find a balance between work and free time.

# Reading – Finding Balance

Nowadays, many people spend a large part of their day online.

They check their phones, scroll through social media, and communicate through apps.

Although technology makes life easier, it can also take too much of our time.

Some people realise that they need to cut down on screen time and spend more time offline.

They try to hang out with friends, exercise, or enjoy hobbies.

Finding a balance between online and real life is important for mental health and well-being.
