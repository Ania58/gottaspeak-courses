Example:

# Language Focus – Describing Problems

When people talk about problems, they often describe:

- what happened  
- what caused the problem  
- how they solved it  

Example:

My phone stopped working, so I restarted it.

---

# Phrasal Verbs

**break down**

stop working

Example  
My laptop broke down yesterday.

---

**fix up**

repair something

Example  
He fixed up my computer.

---

**set up**

prepare or install something

Example  
I set up a new account.

---

**run out of**

have no more of something

Example  
My phone ran out of battery.

---

# Idioms

**drive someone crazy**

make someone very annoyed

Example  
Slow internet drives me crazy.

---

**back to square one**

start again from the beginning

Example  
After the error, I was back to square one.

---

**a waste of time**

something not useful

Example  
Waiting for the update was a waste of time.

---

**give it another try**

try again

Example  
The app didn’t work, so I gave it another try.

---

**not working properly**

something doesn’t function correctly

Example  
My phone is not working properly.

# Reading – A Frustrating Problem

Yesterday, Anna tried to log in to her online account, but something went wrong.

First, she entered her password, but the system showed an error.

She tried again, but nothing worked.

After several attempts, she had to reset her password.

Unfortunately, her phone ran out of battery in the middle of the process.

Anna felt frustrated because everything was taking too long.

Finally, she managed to fix the problem, but she said the experience drove her crazy.
