We use **adverbs of frequency** to talk about habits.

always  
usually  
often  
sometimes  
rarely  
never  

Example:

# Phrasal Verbs

**post something**

share content online

Example  
She posted a video yesterday.

---

**reply to**

answer someone

Example  
I replied to his message immediately.

---

**check out**

look at something online

Example  
Check out this profile.

---

**sign up**

create an account

Example  
I signed up for a new platform.

---

**follow / unfollow**

start or stop following someone

Example  
I followed her because I liked her content.

---

# Idioms

**go viral**

become very popular online

Example  
That video went viral in one day.

---

**keep in touch**

stay in contact

Example  
We keep in touch through social media.

---

**lose track of time**

not notice how much time passes

Example  
I lose track of time when I scroll through apps.

---

**on the same page**

have the same understanding

Example  
We need to be on the same page about this.

---

**share something with someone**

communicate or show something

Example  
I shared the news with my friends.

# Reading – Social Media Habits

For many people, social media is part of everyday life.

They use it to keep in touch with friends, share photos, and follow interesting content.

Some users post regularly, while others rarely share anything.

It is easy to lose track of time when scrolling through social media platforms.

Sometimes posts go viral and reach thousands of people.

Although social media can help people stay connected, it can also be distracting.

That is why many people try to limit their screen time.
