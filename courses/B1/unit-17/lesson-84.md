## Grammar Focus ‚Äì First Conditional

## Reading ‚Äì What Happens If We Do Nothing?

A company notices small problems in customer service. At first, managers think they will disappear on their own.

However, if they **don‚Äôt deal with** complaints quickly, customers become frustrated. When clients feel ignored, they **will stop** trusting the brand.

If negative reviews increase, sales **will drop**. As profits fall, budgets are cut. When teams lose support, motivation **falls apart**.

One small issue can **run into** another, and the situation can quickly **get out of hand**. If management waits too long, the company **will end up** spending more money fixing problems than preventing them.

The lesson is simple: if you fix issues early, you avoid bigger consequences later.
