# Language Focus – Talking About Travel Experiences

People often explain **why they travel** and **what they experience**.

Example:

I travel because I enjoy discovering new cultures.

Examples:

Many people travel to relax and escape their daily routine.  
Others travel to explore new places and learn about different cultures.  

---

# Phrasal Verbs

**set off**

start a journey

Example  
We set off early in the morning.

---

**get away**

leave your normal environment for a short holiday

Example  
I love getting away for a weekend.

---

**look around**

explore a place

Example  
We spent the afternoon looking around the old town.

---

**check out**

visit or examine something

Example  
We checked out several museums.

---

# Idioms

**get the travel bug**

start to love travelling

Example  
After my first trip abroad, I got the travel bug.

---

**hit the road**

start travelling

Example  
We packed our bags and hit the road.

---

**travel light**

travel with very little luggage

Example  
I prefer to travel light when I go on short trips.

---

**off the beaten track**

a place that tourists do not usually visit

Example  
We found a beautiful village off the beaten track.

# Reading – Discovering the World

For many people, travelling is more than just a holiday. It is a way to learn about the world.

Some travellers enjoy visiting famous cities and popular attractions. Others prefer places that are off the beaten track, where they can experience local life.

Emma, a university student from Canada, got the travel bug when she travelled to Spain for the first time.

Since then, she has visited many countries. She loves exploring new cultures, trying local food, and meeting people from different backgrounds.

Emma says that travelling helps people understand the world better and become more open-minded.
