# Language Focus – Describing Places

When people talk about places, they often describe:

- what the place **looks like**
- what people **can do there**
- why it is **special**

Example:

Barcelona is a city **which is famous for its architecture**.

Example:

We visited a village **where people still follow old traditions**.

---

# Phrasal Verbs

**walk around**

explore a place on foot

Example  
We spent the afternoon walking around the old town.

---

**come across**

find something unexpectedly

Example  
We came across a small café in a quiet street.

---

**stop by**

visit a place for a short time

Example  
We stopped by a local market.

---

**try out**

test or experience something new

Example  
We tried out several traditional dishes.

---

# Idioms

**hidden gem**

a wonderful place that not many people know

Example  
That beach is a hidden gem.

---

**worth the trip**

a place that is good enough to travel to

Example  
The castle is definitely worth the trip.

---

**take your breath away**

extremely beautiful

Example  
The mountain view took my breath away.

---

**a place to remember**

a place that leaves a strong impression

Example  
The village was truly a place to remember.

# Reading – A Hidden Gem

During a trip to Portugal, Laura decided to visit a small coastal village.

It wasn’t a famous tourist destination, but she had heard that it was a hidden gem.

When she arrived, the view of the ocean took her breath away.

Laura spent the afternoon walking around the village and exploring narrow streets.

She also stopped by a small restaurant where she tried out several traditional dishes.

Although the village was small, it was definitely worth the trip.

Laura says it is one of the most beautiful places she has ever visited.
