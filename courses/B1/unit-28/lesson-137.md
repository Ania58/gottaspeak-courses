# Language Focus – Describing Unexpected Situations

When telling travel stories, people often describe:

- **what happened**
- **why it was a problem**
- **how they solved it**

Example:

Our train was delayed, so we had to wait at the station for two hours.

Another example:

While we were walking through the city, we realised we were lost.

---

# Phrasal Verbs

**run into**

meet something unexpected

Example  
We ran into a big problem at the airport.

---

**break down**

stop working (for vehicles or machines)

Example  
Our bus broke down in the mountains.

---

**sort out**

solve a problem

Example  
The hotel staff helped us sort out the reservation.

---

**deal with**

handle a situation

Example  
You have to stay calm and deal with the problem.

---

# Idioms

**in the middle of nowhere**

a place far from towns or people

Example  
The bus stopped in the middle of nowhere.

---

**miss the boat**

miss an opportunity

Example  
If you arrive late, you might miss the boat.

---

**things didn’t go as planned**

something unexpected happened

Example  
Our trip was exciting because things didn’t go as planned.

---

**learn the hard way**

learn through a difficult experience

Example  
I learned the hard way to check my passport before travelling.

# Reading – A Difficult Journey

Last summer, Mark travelled to a small town in Italy. At first, everything went well.

However, things didn’t go as planned when his train was delayed for several hours.

Later that evening, he realised he had lost his luggage.

Mark had to deal with the situation quickly. He spoke to the station staff, who helped him sort out the problem.

The next day, his luggage was returned.

Although the journey was stressful, Mark says he learned the hard way to keep important items in his backpack.
