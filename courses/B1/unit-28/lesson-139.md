# Language Focus – Talking About Plans

When people plan trips, they often talk about:

- **where they want to go**
- **how they will travel**
- **how much they want to spend**

Example:

We’re going to visit three cities in Spain.

Example:

I’m planning to travel in September because it’s cheaper.

---

# Phrasal Verbs

**figure out**

find a solution or understand something

Example  
We need to figure out the best route.

---

**plan ahead**

prepare things in advance

Example  
It’s always better to plan ahead.

---

**cut down on**

reduce something

Example  
We had to cut down on expenses.

---

**end up**

finally arrive at a result

Example  
We ended up choosing a small hotel.

---

# Idioms

**on a tight budget**

having very little money to spend

Example  
We travelled across Europe on a tight budget.

---

**last-minute decision**

a decision made very late

Example  
The trip was a last-minute decision.

---

**a once-in-a-lifetime trip**

a very special trip that probably happens only once

Example  
Visiting the Great Wall was a once-in-a-lifetime trip.

---

**make the most of something**

use something as well as possible

Example  
We tried to make the most of our time in the city.

# Reading – Planning the Perfect Trip

Tom and his friends decided to organise a summer trip together.

At first, they had many different ideas about where to go. Some wanted to visit a big city, while others preferred the mountains.

They spent several days trying to figure out the best destination.

Because they were on a tight budget, they decided to cut down on expensive hotels and book a small apartment instead.

In the end, they planned a two-week trip across northern Italy.

They wanted to make the most of their time by visiting several towns and trying local food.

Although the trip required a lot of planning, they believed it would be a once-in-a-lifetime experience.
