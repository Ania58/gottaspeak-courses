# Language Focus – Telling a Story

When people tell travel stories, they often organise them in steps.

Useful expressions:

first  
then  
after that  
later  
in the end  

Example:

First we visited the city centre. Then we explored the old streets.

After that we found a small restaurant with local food.

---

# Phrasal Verbs

**turn out**

have a particular result

Example  
The trip turned out to be amazing.

---

**come back**

return to a place

Example  
I would love to come back to that city.

---

**bring back**

make someone remember something

Example  
This photo brings back many memories.

---

**look back on**

think about something in the past

Example  
I look back on that trip with great happiness.

---

# Idioms

**one for the books**

a very memorable event

Example  
That trip was definitely one for the books.

---

**a trip to remember**

a very special journey

Example  
Our visit to Iceland was a trip to remember.

---

**the time of my life**

a wonderful experience

Example  
I had the time of my life during that holiday.

---

**an eye-opening experience**

something that changes how you see the world

Example  
Travelling alone was an eye-opening experience.

# Reading – An Unexpected Adventure

Last year, Sophie travelled to Greece with two friends.

At first, the trip seemed simple. They planned to relax on the beach and visit a few historic sites.

However, things quickly became more adventurous.

One afternoon they decided to explore a small island nearby. They took a local boat and spent the day discovering quiet beaches and small villages.

In the evening they watched the sunset over the sea.

The trip turned out to be one for the books.

Sophie says it was the time of her life and an experience she will always remember.
