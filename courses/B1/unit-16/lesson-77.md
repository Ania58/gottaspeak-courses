## Language Focus – Showing Contrast

When we compare options, we often contrast ideas.

### ➤ Useful linkers

- **while / whereas** → contrast two facts  
- **however** → contrast a previous idea  
- **on the other hand** → alternative view  

Examples:

- Working from home is cheaper, **whereas** commuting costs money.  
- The salary is high. **However**, the hours are long.  
- It’s flexible. **On the other hand**, it can feel lonely.

## Reading – Remote Work vs Office Work  

More companies now allow employees to work remotely. For many people, this sounds like the perfect solution. However, it isn’t always the best choice for everyone.

### Remote work – advantages
You save time and money because you don’t commute. You can **cut down on** travel expenses and spend more time with your family. It’s also easier to organise your day, and many people say they are more productive at home.

### Remote work – disadvantages
On the other hand, working alone can feel isolating. Some employees **end up** working longer hours because they never fully disconnect. Communication may also be slower while teams wait for emails or online meetings.

### Office work – advantages
Office jobs offer structure and direct contact with colleagues. Problems are solved faster, whereas remote workers sometimes struggle with delayed responses. You also build stronger relationships with your team.

### Office work – disadvantages
However, commuting can be stressful and time-consuming. You might **miss out on** personal time or hobbies. Some people feel tired before the workday even begins.

In the end, choosing between remote and office work is often **a tough call**. It really **comes down to** your personality, lifestyle and priorities. Some people prefer flexibility, while others prefer stability. The ideal solution might be a hybrid system – the **best of both worlds**.

## Grammar & Discourse Practice – Choose the Best Option  

1. Working remotely saves time, __________ some people feel lonely.  
   (however / because)

   (whereas / although)

   (Because / On the other hand)

4. He likes the team, __________ he dislikes the commute.  
   (but / while)
