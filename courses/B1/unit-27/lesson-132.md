---

# Language Focus – Talking About Opportunities

When people talk about opportunities, they often describe:

- the **chance itself**
- the **decision they made**
- the **result**

Example:

I had the opportunity to work abroad, so I decided to try it.

---

### Useful expressions

I had a chance to…  
I got the opportunity to…  
I didn’t want to miss the chance.  
It was a great opportunity.  
I almost missed the opportunity.

Example:

She had the chance to study in another country.

---

# Vocabulary – Opportunities

opportunity  
chance  
possibility  
risk  
offer  

Useful expressions:

take an opportunity  
miss a chance  
accept an offer  
create opportunities  

Example:

Sometimes opportunities appear when you least expect them.

---

# Phrasal Verbs

**come across**

find something by chance

Example  
I came across an interesting job advertisement online.

---

**miss out on**

lose an opportunity

Example  
I didn’t want to miss out on the chance to travel.

---

**take on**

accept responsibility or a challenge

Example  
She decided to take on a new role at work.

---

**turn down**

reject an offer

Example  
He turned down the job offer.

---

# Idioms

**once in a lifetime**

a very rare opportunity

Example  
Travelling to Antarctica is a once-in-a-lifetime experience.

---

**take a chance**

try something even if there is risk

Example  
He took a chance and started his own company.

# Reading – An Unexpected Opportunity

Two years ago, Olivia was working in a small marketing company when she came across an advertisement for a job abroad.

At first, she wasn’t sure if she should apply. Moving to another country felt risky.

However, her friends encouraged her not to miss out on the opportunity.

Olivia decided to take a chance and apply for the job. A few weeks later, she received an offer.

Although it was a difficult decision, she didn’t want to turn down such a unique opportunity.

Today, Olivia says it was one of the best decisions she has ever made.

---

# Reading Comprehension

Answer in full sentences.

2. How did she discover the job advertisement?
3. Why was she unsure at first?
