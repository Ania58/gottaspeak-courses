# Lesson 134 – Weighing Pros and Cons

## Warm-up – Before You Decide

1. Do you usually decide quickly or do you analyse the situation first?

3. What is the last decision you had to think about seriously?

- choosing a university

---

# Language Focus – Comparing Advantages and Disadvantages

When people analyse decisions, they often compare **positive and negative sides**.

Example:

The job pays well, **but** the working hours are very long.

Common connectors:

although  
even though  
however  
while  

Example:

Although the salary is high, the job is very stressful.

---

# Useful Expressions

consider the pros and cons  
look at both sides  
think something through  
reach a conclusion  

Example:

Before accepting the offer, she carefully thought it through.

---

# Vocabulary – Evaluating Choices

advantage  
disadvantage  
benefit  
drawback  
factor  

Example:

One major drawback of the job is the long commute.

---

# Phrasal Verbs

**think something through**

consider something carefully before deciding

Example  
Before making the decision, she thought everything through.

---

**cut out**

remove something unnecessary

Example  
We cut out several options that didn’t make sense.

---

**come down to**

the final reason for a decision

Example  
In the end, the decision came down to money.

---

**stick with**

continue with a decision or plan

Example  
After thinking carefully, he decided to stick with his original plan.

---

# Idioms

**look at the bigger picture**

consider the overall situation

Example  
If you look at the bigger picture, the decision makes sense.

---

**weigh the pros and cons**

analyse advantages and disadvantages

Example  
Before moving abroad, she weighed the pros and cons.

# Reading – A Difficult Decision

Maria recently received two different job offers.

The first job offered a high salary. However, it required travelling constantly and working long hours.

The second job paid less, although it offered flexible working hours and a better work-life balance.

Maria spent several days weighing the pros and cons. She wrote a list of advantages and disadvantages for both jobs.

In the end, the decision came down to lifestyle rather than money.

Maria decided to accept the second job because she wanted more time for family and personal projects.

Looking at the bigger picture helped her make the right decision.
