# Language Focus – Talking About Decisions

When speaking about decisions, English speakers often describe:

1. **options**
2. **the thinking process**
3. **the final choice**

Example:

I had two job offers, so I thought it over carefully before deciding.

---

### Useful expressions

I need to think about it.  
I’m still deciding.  
I finally decided to…  
I chose to…  
I haven’t made up my mind yet.

Example:

I haven’t made up my mind yet about the trip.

---

# Vocabulary – Choices and Decisions

decision  
choice  
option  
opportunity  
result  

Useful expressions:

make a decision  
choose between options  
consider something  
reach a decision  

Example:

She had to choose between two good options.

---

# Phrasal Verbs

**think something over**

consider something carefully

Example  
I need a day to think it over.

---

**go for**

choose something

Example  
In the end, I went for the cheaper option.

---

**decide on**

make a final choice

Example  
They decided on a restaurant near the office.

---

**rule out**

remove an option from consideration

Example  
We ruled out travelling in winter.

---

# Idioms

**make up your mind**

make a decision

Example  
You need to make up your mind soon.

---

**sleep on it**

wait before making a decision

Example  
I’ll sleep on it and tell you tomorrow.

# Reading – A Difficult Choice

Last month, Daniel had to make an important decision. He received two job offers from different companies.

One job offered a higher salary, but it was in another city. The other job paid less, but it was close to home.

Daniel didn’t want to decide too quickly. He thought the situation over and spoke with friends and family.

At first he considered moving to the other city, but eventually he ruled out that option.

In the end, he decided on the job close to home because work-life balance was more important to him.

Daniel felt relieved once he finally made up his mind.
