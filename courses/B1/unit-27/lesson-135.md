# Language Focus – Reconsidering Decisions

Sometimes people make a decision and later realise they want something different.

Example:

I planned to move abroad, but I changed my mind.

---

### Useful expressions

I changed my mind.  
I reconsidered the decision.  
In the end, I decided differently.  
Things didn’t turn out as expected.  
I ended up doing something else.

Example:

At first I wanted to change jobs, but I changed my mind.

---

# Vocabulary – Revising Decisions

decision  
plan  
alternative  
doubt  
reflection  

Useful expressions:

have second thoughts  
reconsider a decision  
change direction  
review a plan  

Example:

After thinking about it again, she reconsidered the decision.

---

# Phrasal Verbs

**back out (of something)**

decide not to do something you had planned

Example  
He backed out of the agreement at the last moment.

---

**end up**

finally reach a situation that wasn’t planned

Example  
We ended up staying in a different hotel.

---

**call off**

cancel something

Example  
They called off the meeting.

---

**stick to**

continue following a decision or plan

Example  
She decided to stick to her original plan.

---

# Idioms

**have second thoughts**

start doubting a decision

Example  
He had second thoughts about moving abroad.

---

**sleep on it**

wait before making a final decision

Example  
I’m not sure yet. I’ll sleep on it.

# Reading – A Change of Plan

Last year, Kevin planned to move abroad to start a new job.

At first, he was very excited about the idea. However, after a few weeks he started having second thoughts.

Moving to another country meant leaving his friends and family behind.

Kevin decided to sleep on it and think carefully about the decision.

Eventually, he realised that he wasn’t ready for such a big change.

In the end, he backed out of the job offer and decided to stay where he was.

Although it was a difficult choice, Kevin felt more confident after making the final decision.
