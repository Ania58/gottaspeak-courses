# Language Focus – Talking About Risk

When people describe risks, they usually explain:

- **the situation**
- **the possible consequences**
- **their decision**

Example:

I knew it was risky, but I decided to try anyway.

---

### Useful expressions

It was a risky decision.  
I didn’t want to risk it.  
It seemed like a good opportunity.  
I decided to take the risk.  
In the end, it paid off.

Example:

She decided to take the risk and start her own company.

---

# Vocabulary – Risk and Safety

risk  
danger  
uncertainty  
reward  
consequence  

Useful expressions:

take a risk  
avoid a risk  
calculate the risk  
face the consequences  

Example:

Before investing money, you should calculate the risk.

---

# Phrasal Verbs

**go for it**

try something confidently

Example  
If you believe in the idea, go for it.

---

**back out**

withdraw from a plan

Example  
He wanted to start the project but backed out at the last moment.

---

**weigh up**

consider carefully

Example  
She weighed up the advantages and disadvantages.

---

**end up**

finally reach a situation

Example  
They took a risk and ended up succeeding.

---

# Idioms

**play it safe**

avoid risk

Example  
Instead of investing, he decided to play it safe.

---

**nothing ventured, nothing gained**

you must take risks to achieve something

Example  
Starting a business is risky, but nothing ventured, nothing gained.

# Reading – A Big Risk

Three years ago, Marcus was working in a stable office job. The salary was good, but he felt bored and unmotivated.

One day, a friend suggested starting an online business together. Marcus wasn’t sure at first because it seemed risky.

He spent several weeks weighing up the advantages and disadvantages. Eventually he decided to go for it.

At the beginning things were difficult, and there were moments when he wanted to back out.

However, Marcus continued working hard. Two years later the business became successful.

Looking back, Marcus says the risk was worth it.
