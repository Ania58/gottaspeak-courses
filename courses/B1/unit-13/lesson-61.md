## Grammar Focus – Opinions and Reasons

We often share opinions about work, relationships, decisions and society.  
Real communication usually includes **an opinion + a reason**.

### ➤ Expressing opinions

- I think…  
- I believe…  
- In my opinion,…  
- It seems to me that…

Examples:  
- I think teamwork is essential.  
- In my opinion, communication at work is very important.

### ➤ Giving reasons

- because…  
- this is because…  
- the main reason is…  
- one reason is…

Examples:  
- I think remote work is useful **because it saves time**.  
- In my opinion, mental health matters. **The main reason is balance.**

## Reading – Why Opinions Change

People often believe that opinions are fixed. In reality, opinions change more often than we expect. They are influenced by new information, personal experiences and the people around us.

Someone may believe that working long hours is the only way to succeed. After experiencing stress, illness or family difficulties, however, they may start to think differently. Personal experience often has the strongest influence on how people see the world.

Social pressure also plays an important role. Friends, colleagues and even online communities can slowly shape our views. Sometimes people adapt their opinions to fit in. At other times, they change them because they discover new facts.

Changing an opinion is not a weakness. It is often a sign of learning. People who are open to different points of view are more likely to make thoughtful decisions and understand others.

## Grammar Practice – Choose the Best Option

1. I think working in a team is important, __________ it builds trust.  
   (so / because)

2. In my opinion, people often feel stressed, __________ they still accept more tasks.  
   (although / because)

3. She believes online learning is useful; __________, it can be difficult to stay motivated.  
   (however / because)

4. It seems to me that people change their opinions __________ they gain more experience.  
   (when / so)

5. He thinks communication matters, __________ misunderstandings can cause serious problems.  
   (because / but)

## Speaking Task – Opinion Exchange

Work with a partner.

1. Share one strong opinion.  
2. Give at least **two reasons**.  
3. Ask your partner: *“Why do you think that?”*  
4. Respond using **because**, **the main reason is**, or **one reason is**.
