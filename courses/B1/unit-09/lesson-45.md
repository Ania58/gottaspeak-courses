## Idioms & Phrasal Verbs Focus

### Idioms

- **be under pressure** – feel stress because of expectations or difficulties  
- **cross the line** – go beyond what is acceptable  
- **face the music** – accept unpleasant consequences  
- **take something seriously** – treat something as important  

### Phrasal verbs

- **give in (to)** – stop resisting  
- **stand up for** – defend  
- **deal with** – manage, handle  
- **carry on** – continue  
- **back out (of)** – withdraw from a decision or agreement

## Reading: When Pressure Shapes Decisions

Pressure rarely arrives suddenly. More often, it builds quietly. Expectations increase, responsibilities grow, and the space for hesitation disappears. In such moments, people are forced to confront who they are and what they value.

Some choose to give in. They adapt, accept conditions they never planned to accept, and carry on in situations that no longer feel right. Others stand up for themselves or for others, even when doing so involves risk. Both reactions come at a cost.

Problems often arise when boundaries are crossed. When people are pushed beyond what they consider acceptable, frustration turns into resistance. At this stage, avoiding conflict usually makes things worse. Sooner or later, everyone has to face the music.

Mature decisions are not made without discomfort. They involve reflection, courage and the willingness to take something seriously even when the outcome is uncertain. Pressure does not only test character. It reveals it.
