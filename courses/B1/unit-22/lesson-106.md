## Grammar Focus – Strong Comparatives

At B1 level we already know comparatives like:

- cheaper
- better
- faster
- easier

Now we add **intensifiers** to compare options more precisely.

### ➤ much / far + comparative

Used when the difference is **big**.

Examples:

- This solution is **much better**.  
- Remote work is **far more flexible**.  
- The new system is **much faster**.

---

### ➤ slightly / a little + comparative

Used when the difference is **small**.

Examples:

- This option is **slightly cheaper**.  
- The second plan is **a little easier**.

---

### ➤ way + comparative (informal)

Examples:

- This idea is **way more practical**.  
- That method is **way faster**.

## Idioms & Phrasal Verbs

- **weigh something up** – carefully consider options  
- **look into something** – investigate or research  
- **go with something** – choose a particular option  
- **rule something out** – decide something is not possible  
- **come down to something** – be determined by something

## Reading – Two Job Offers

After finishing university, Daniel received two job offers. Both seemed attractive, but they were very different.

The first offer came from a large international company. The salary was much higher, and the office was located in the city centre. However, the work environment seemed more formal and competitive.

The second offer was from a small startup. The salary was slightly lower, but the company offered flexible hours and the chance to work on creative projects. The team also seemed more relaxed.

Daniel spent several days weighing up the options. The first job looked far more stable, but the second one seemed much more exciting.

In the end, his decision came down to one important question: what kind of work environment would make him happier in the long run?
