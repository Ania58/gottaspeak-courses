## Grammar Focus – Showing Results and Consequences

When explaining decisions, we often connect **actions and results**.

### ➤ so

Used in everyday speech to show a direct result.

Examples:

- The offer was very good, **so** she accepted it.  
- He forgot the deadline, **so** the application was rejected.

---

### ➤ therefore / as a result

More formal ways to show consequences.

Examples:

- The company lost several clients; **therefore**, it changed its strategy.  
- The project was delayed. **As a result**, the launch happened later than planned.

---

### ➤ otherwise

Used to warn about negative consequences.

Examples:

- We need to decide soon, **otherwise** we might miss the opportunity.  
- Check the details carefully, **otherwise** mistakes can happen.

## Idioms & Phrasal Verbs

- **think something through** – consider carefully before deciding  
- **end up doing something** – finally do something after a process  
- **turn something down** – refuse an offer  
- **miss out on something** – lose an opportunity  
- **go ahead with something** – continue with a plan

## Reading – A Decision That Changed Everything

When Maya finished university, she received two job offers.

The first job was stable and well-paid, so many people told her she should accept it immediately. The second job was at a small start-up company with a lot of uncertainty.

Maya decided to think things through before making a decision. The safe job offered stability, but the start-up offered creativity and rapid learning.

She analysed the possible consequences. If the start-up failed, she might need to find another job quickly. However, if it succeeded, the experience could open many doors in the future.

In the end, Maya chose the start-up role. The risk was higher, but the potential rewards were greater. As a result, she gained valuable experience and later became a project leader in the company.

## Grammar Practice – Choose the Correct Connector

Choose the option that logically completes the sentence.

1. The project failed, ______ the team changed its strategy.  
   (so / otherwise)

2. The company lost important data; ______ it improved its security systems.  
   (therefore / although)

3. We must compare all the options carefully, ______ we might regret the decision later.  
   (so / otherwise)

4. The new system was easier to use, ______ productivity increased quickly.  
   (so / however)

5. The plan was too expensive; ______ the company looked for an alternative.  
   (because / therefore)
