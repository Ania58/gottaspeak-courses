## Language Focus – Expressing Decisions

English has several common ways to talk about **decisions and plans**.

### ➤ decide to

Used when a decision is **already made**.

Examples:

- She **decided to accept** the job offer.  
- They **decided to change** the strategy.

---

### ➤ be thinking of / be considering

Used when someone is **still analysing options**.

Examples:

- I’m **thinking of moving** to another city.  
- The company is **considering expanding** the project.

---

### ➤ plan to

Used when someone has **a clear intention**.

Examples:

- We **plan to launch** the new product next year.  
- He **plans to continue** his studies abroad.

## Idioms & Phrasal Verbs

- **weigh something up** – consider options carefully  
- **rule something out** – eliminate a possibility  
- **go with something** – choose a particular option  
- **look into something** – investigate further  
- **come down to something** – be determined by something

## Reading – Choosing the Right Path

After several years in the same company, Laura began to feel that her career was not progressing. She enjoyed her work, but she wanted new challenges.

At first, she started looking into other opportunities in the same field. Some positions offered higher salaries, but the responsibilities were very similar to her current job.

Then she began thinking of a completely different direction. She had always been interested in environmental projects, and she started considering jobs in that sector.

The decision was not easy. Changing careers involves risk, and Laura wanted to weigh up the advantages and disadvantages carefully.

In the end, her decision came down to one important factor: personal motivation. She decided to accept a position in a small environmental organisation and start a new professional chapter.
