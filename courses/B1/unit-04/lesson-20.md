## Language Focus: Talking About Choices and Balance (8 min)

When discussing healthy lifestyles, people often:
- describe **recent changes** (present perfect),
- give **advice** (should / shouldn’t / ought to),
- use **natural expressions** to talk about energy and stress.

Useful language:
- **I’ve changed my routine recently…**
- **You should focus on small, realistic steps.**
- **People shouldn’t ignore early warning signs.**
- **slow down / cut out / look after**
- **burn out / recharge your batteries / take it easy**

## Reading: Choosing Health in Everyday Life (18–20 min)

Making healthy lifestyle choices is not about perfection, but about consistency. Many adults have tried extreme diets or intense exercise plans, only to give up after a short time. In contrast, sustainable changes are usually small and realistic.

People have become more aware of how daily habits affect both physical and mental health. Some have adjusted their routines by getting more sleep, cutting out unnecessary stress and learning to slow down. Others have focused on balance, understanding that pushing too hard can lead to burnout.

Advice plays an important role, but it works best when it respects individual circumstances. People should choose habits that fit their lifestyle rather than follow strict rules. Listening to the body, taking it easy when needed and recharging your batteries are essential for long-term wellbeing.

Ultimately, healthy choices are personal. When people commit to gradual change and look after themselves consistently, progress becomes more achievable and lasting.
