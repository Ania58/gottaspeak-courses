## Grammar Focus: Present Perfect for Habits and Changes (8 min)

We use the **present perfect** to talk about **changes and habits that started in the past and continue now**, or about **recent changes** that affect the present.

**Structure:**
- **have / has + past participle**

> I’ve changed my eating habits.  
> She’s started exercising more.

### Common time expressions:

- **for** + period of time  
  > I’ve slept better **for** the last few weeks.

- **since** + starting point  
  > He’s avoided sugar **since** January.

- **recently / lately**  
  > People have become more aware of mental health **recently**.

We often use the present perfect to describe **lifestyle improvements** and **ongoing efforts**, not finished actions.

## Reading: Small Changes, Healthier Lives (14–16 min)

In recent years, many adults have started to rethink their lifestyle choices. Busy schedules, long working hours and constant online activity have made people more aware of the importance of health and balance. As a result, small changes in daily habits have become more common.

Some people have improved their diet by cooking at home more often and avoiding processed food. Others have focused on physical activity, even if it means short daily walks instead of intense workouts. Mental health has also received more attention. Many adults have tried to slow down, reduce stress and create healthier routines.

These changes are rarely dramatic, but they are often sustainable. People who have focused on consistency rather than perfection have noticed positive effects over time. Feeling more energetic, sleeping better and coping with stress more effectively are some of the benefits they mention.

Healthy habits usually develop gradually. What matters most is awareness and commitment. When people understand how their choices affect their wellbeing, they are more likely to maintain a balanced lifestyle in the long term.
