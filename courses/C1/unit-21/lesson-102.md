# Lesson 102 – How Much Risk Should We Take?

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:

- discuss the role of risk in personal and professional life
- evaluate the advantages and disadvantages of taking risks
- distinguish between reckless and calculated decisions
- defend opinions about uncertainty and opportunity

# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- calculated risk – carefully considered decision involving uncertainty
- risk tolerance – willingness to accept uncertainty or possible loss
- opportunity cost – benefit lost by choosing one option over another
- downside exposure – extent of possible negative consequences
- probability assessment – evaluation of how likely an outcome is
- contingency plan – alternative strategy prepared in case something goes wrong
- uncertainty management – process of making decisions despite incomplete information
- potential payoff – possible benefit gained if a decision succeeds
- margin for error – amount of flexibility before serious problems occur
- risk mitigation – actions taken to reduce potential negative consequences

# 7. Reading

Read.

Every important decision involves uncertainty. Whether choosing a career, making an investment or starting a new relationship, people rarely possess complete information about future outcomes. As a result, effective decision-making depends not on eliminating risk but on managing it intelligently.

Psychologists distinguish between reckless behaviour and calculated risk-taking. Reckless decisions ignore available evidence and potential consequences, whereas calculated risks involve careful planning, probability assessment and preparation for possible setbacks.

Interestingly, research suggests that people often regret opportunities they never pursued more than unsuccessful attempts. Although failure can be painful, missed opportunities frequently remain sources of long-term reflection because people continue wondering what might have happened.
