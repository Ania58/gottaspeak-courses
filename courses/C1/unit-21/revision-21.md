# 4. Language From the Listening

Work with these expressions.

- heuristic – mental shortcut used to make decisions quickly without analysing every detail
- cognitive bias – systematic error in thinking that influences judgement and decision-making
- unconscious processing – mental activity that occurs automatically without conscious awareness
- ethical reasoning – careful evaluation of what is morally right or wrong before making a decision
- reflective practice – deliberate process of examining one's thoughts, actions and decisions in order to improve future judgement
- value-driven decision-making – making choices based primarily on personal principles and beliefs
- contextual awareness – ability to consider the broader circumstances surrounding a decision
- unconscious influence – hidden factor that affects judgement without the person's awareness
- moral justification – explanation demonstrating why a decision is ethically acceptable
- decision framework – structured approach used to evaluate alternatives before choosing
