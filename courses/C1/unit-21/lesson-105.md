# Lesson 105 – Would You Decide Differently Today?

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:

- reflect on how experience influences decision-making
- discuss how priorities change throughout life
- evaluate important life choices from different perspectives
- express nuanced opinions about wisdom, maturity and personal growth

---

# 1. Opening Question

Read the statement.

## "If you knew then what you know now, your life would probably be very different."

Do you agree?

---

## Discussion

1. What decision would you make differently today?
2. Does age always make people wiser?
3. Why do priorities change over time?
4. Is there such a thing as the "right" decision?

---

# 2. Five Life Decisions

Read each situation.

Your task:

- Decide what advice you would give.
- Explain whether your advice would be different if the person were older or younger.
- Discuss how experience might influence the decision.

---

## Situation 1 – Choosing a Career

An eighteen-year-old student cannot decide between:

- following a passion with uncertain career prospects,
- or choosing a stable profession with excellent job security.

---

## Situation 2 – Buying a Home

A couple has enough money to buy a small apartment now.

Alternatively, they could continue renting and wait several years to afford a larger home.

---

## Situation 3 – Having Children

A successful professional wants children one day but keeps postponing the decision because their career is progressing rapidly.

---

## Situation 4 – Starting Over

A fifty-year-old employee wants to leave a secure job and start their own business.

Friends believe it is too late.

---

## Situation 5 – The Long Friendship

Someone realises that a close friendship has become one-sided and emotionally exhausting.

They are unsure whether to continue investing in the relationship or move on.

---

## Discussion

1. Which decision is the hardest?
2. Does every decision have a "perfect" moment?
3. Which choices become more difficult with age?
4. Should logic or emotion guide life-changing decisions?

# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- life-altering decision – choice that significantly changes the course of someone's life
- long-term perspective – considering consequences far into the future
- shifting priorities – gradual change in what someone considers most important
- accumulated wisdom – knowledge gained through experience over many years
- informed judgement – opinion formed after careful reflection and experience
- personal fulfilment – deep sense of satisfaction with one's life
- emotional maturity – ability to manage emotions and make balanced decisions
- life satisfaction – overall evaluation of one's quality of life
- value system – set of principles that guide behaviour and decisions
- defining moment – event or decision that permanently shapes a person's future

# 7. Reading

Read.

As people grow older, their decisions often reflect changing priorities rather than increasing certainty. Younger adults frequently focus on achievement, exploration and future opportunities, while older adults tend to value stability, meaningful relationships and personal fulfilment more highly.

Researchers suggest that wisdom is not simply the result of age but of reflective experience. Individuals who regularly examine their decisions, learn from mistakes and remain open to new perspectives are more likely to develop sound judgement over time.

Interestingly, many people report that they would make different choices if they could relive their lives. However, they also recognise that their present values exist largely because of those earlier experiences. In this sense, mistakes and successes contribute equally to personal growth.
