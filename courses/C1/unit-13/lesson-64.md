# Lesson 64 – Customs That Surprise Foreigners

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- discuss social norms and cultural expectations naturally
- talk about politeness and misunderstandings
- describe surprising customs and traditions
- compare behaviours across cultures without stereotyping

---

# 1. Case Study

Three people experienced unexpected cultural differences.

Your task:

- Identify what surprised each person.
- Decide whether the misunderstanding was avoidable.
- Suggest how they could adapt.

---

## Case A – Jake in Japan

Jake moved to Japan for work.

On his first day, he entered a crowded train and started talking loudly on the phone. People around him stared, and some passengers looked visibly uncomfortable.

Later, his colleague explained that speaking loudly or making phone calls on public transport is generally considered impolite.

Jake felt embarrassed because he had no idea he had broken a social rule.

---

## Case B – Sofia in Germany

Sofia was invited to dinner by German friends.

She arrived twenty minutes late because, in her culture, arriving slightly late is considered normal.

Her hosts had already started eating and seemed annoyed.

Only later did she learn that punctuality is taken very seriously in Germany.

---

## Case C – Ethan in Brazil

Ethan was surprised by how physically expressive people were.

People hugged, touched his arm during conversations and stood much closer than he was used to.

At first, he felt uncomfortable and interpreted this behaviour as intrusive.

Eventually, he realised that it was simply a different way of expressing warmth and friendliness.

# 3. Language From the Cases

Work with these expressions.

- social norm – accepted way of behaving
- etiquette – rules of polite behaviour
- personal boundaries – limits regarding physical or emotional closeness
- punctuality – habit of being on time
- body language – communication through movements and gestures
- cultural expectation – behaviour considered normal in a culture
- misunderstanding – failure to understand correctly
- politeness – respectful behaviour
- gesture – movement with a meaning
- first impression – opinion formed when meeting someone for the first time

# 7. Reading

Read.

People often assume that their own habits are universal. However, many everyday behaviours are shaped by culture rather than by common sense.

Greeting styles, punctuality, personal space and communication patterns vary widely around the world.

Because people naturally interpret behaviour through the lens of their own culture, misunderstandings are common.

Fortunately, curiosity and empathy often help people adapt. Rather than judging unfamiliar customs, many experienced travellers recommend observing, asking questions and keeping an open mind.

After all, different does not necessarily mean wrong.
