# Lesson 62 – Why Do People Travel?

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- discuss different motivations for travelling
- express nuanced opinions about tourism and personal growth
- talk about experiences, adventure and escaping routine
- defend arguments and challenge ideas naturally

---

# 1. Debate Starter

Read the statement.

## "Travel broadens the mind."

---

## Initial Reaction

Choose your position.

- Strongly agree
- Mostly agree
- Unsure
- Mostly disagree
- Strongly disagree

Explain your answer.

# 2. Four Travellers

Read about four people.

## Emma

Emma travels because she loves discovering new cultures and learning about history. She believes travel makes people more open-minded.

---

## Daniel

Daniel enjoys luxury holidays. For him, travelling is mainly about relaxation and escaping stress.

---

## Mia

Mia works remotely and spends several months a year in different countries. She enjoys freedom and new experiences.

---

## Alex

Alex rarely travels. He believes people romanticise travel and that happiness depends more on relationships and everyday life than on seeing new places.

---

## Discussion

1. Which person do you relate to most?
2. Is travel about learning or escaping?
3. Can someone be happy without travelling?
4. Is travelling overrated?

# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- broaden your horizons – expand your knowledge and experiences
- get out of your comfort zone – do something unfamiliar
- immerse yourself in something – become deeply involved in a culture or activity
- tourist trap – place designed mainly to attract tourists
- wanderlust – strong desire to travel
- once-in-a-lifetime experience – rare and unforgettable event
- cultural awareness – understanding other cultures
- routine – regular way of living
- adventure – exciting and unusual experience
- perspective – way of seeing the world

# 7. Reading

Read.

People travel for many different reasons. Some seek adventure, while others simply want to relax and escape everyday responsibilities.

Research suggests that experiences often provide more lasting happiness than material possessions. Travelling can create memories, broaden perspectives and encourage people to appreciate different ways of life.

However, travelling is not always life-changing. Sometimes expectations are unrealistic, and people discover that changing locations does not automatically solve personal problems.

Ultimately, travel may teach people as much about themselves as it does about the world around them.
