# Lesson 63 – The Best Place to Live

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- discuss different lifestyles and living environments
- evaluate priorities and quality of life
- express preferences and justify decisions
- compare city life, rural life and location independence

---

# 1. The Investigation

Three people live very different lives.

Your task:

- Decide who seems happiest.
- Identify the advantages and disadvantages of each lifestyle.
- Decide which lifestyle suits you best.

---

## Case A – Olivia

Olivia lives in the centre of London.

She loves the energy of the city. Restaurants, theatres and museums are all within walking distance.

Her salary is good, but rent is extremely expensive. She rarely has time to relax and often complains about noise, traffic and stress.

Despite that, she cannot imagine living anywhere else.

---

## Case B – Ethan

Ethan lives in a small village.

He works remotely and enjoys a peaceful lifestyle. He spends his free time hiking and gardening.

His cost of living is relatively low, but he sometimes feels isolated and misses the excitement and opportunities that cities provide.

---

## Case C – Mia

Mia is a digital nomad.

She spends several months in each country and works online. She loves freedom and constantly experiences new places.

However, she finds it difficult to maintain long-term friendships and often misses having a permanent home.

---

# 2. First Conclusions

Discuss.

4. Is there such a thing as a perfect place to live?

# 3. Language From the Cases

Work with these expressions.

- cost of living – amount of money needed for daily life
- quality of life – overall wellbeing and satisfaction
- pace of life – speed and style of everyday life
- sense of community – feeling of belonging to a group
- convenience – ease and accessibility
- isolation – lack of social connection
- work-life balance – healthy balance between work and personal life
- location independence – ability to work from anywhere
- permanent home – stable place where someone lives
- lifestyle preference – personal choice regarding how to live

# 7. Reading

Read.

People often assume there is one ideal place to live, but preferences vary enormously.

Some people thrive in fast-paced environments full of opportunities and social activities. Others value peace, nature and a slower rhythm of life.

Modern technology has also created new possibilities. Remote work and digital nomadism allow people to combine work with travel and location independence.

However, every lifestyle involves trade-offs. Convenience may come with stress, while peace and simplicity may come with isolation.

Ultimately, the best place to live depends less on geography and more on personal priorities.
