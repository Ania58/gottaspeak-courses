# Lesson 57 – The Myth of Being Busy

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- discuss busyness and productivity naturally
- evaluate modern attitudes towards work and free time
- express agreement and disagreement in a nuanced way
- reflect on rest, priorities and quality of life

# 2. A Week in Three Lives

Read the situations.

## Person A – Emma

Emma's calendar is always full.

She constantly talks about how busy she is, answers emails late at night and feels guilty whenever she has free time.

People admire her dedication, but she often feels exhausted.

---

## Person B – Ryan

Ryan works hard, but he protects his evenings and weekends.

He enjoys hobbies, exercise and spending time with family.

Some people think he lacks ambition, but he rarely feels overwhelmed.

---

## Person C – Olivia

Olivia used to believe that being busy meant being successful.

Recently, however, she has started questioning whether constant activity actually improves her life.

---

## Discussion

1. Which person do you admire most?
2. Which lifestyle seems healthiest?
3. Can busyness become addictive?
4. Why do people glorify being busy?

# 7. Reading

Read.

Modern society often associates busyness with importance and success.

People who are constantly occupied are sometimes viewed as more ambitious, productive or valuable.

However, psychologists warn that activity and productivity are not always the same thing.

Many people fill their schedules with tasks, meetings and distractions without asking whether these activities truly contribute to their goals or happiness.

Research increasingly suggests that rest, reflection and unstructured time are essential for creativity, decision-making and emotional wellbeing.

Perhaps the challenge is not learning how to do more, but learning how to do less without feeling guilty.
