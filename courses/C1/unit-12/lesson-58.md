# Lesson 58 – Why Friendships Change With Age

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- discuss friendships and changing priorities naturally
- talk about drifting apart and maintaining relationships
- express feelings about loneliness and connection
- reflect on quality versus quantity in friendships

---

# 1. Story Starter

Read the story.

When Chloe was in her twenties, she had a large group of friends. There was always someone available for dinner, weekends away or spontaneous plans.

Over the years, however, life became more complicated.

Some friends moved abroad. Others got married or started families. Careers became more demanding and free time became harder to find.

Without any arguments or dramatic events, Chloe slowly lost touch with many people she had once been very close to.

At first, she felt guilty and wondered whether she had done something wrong. But eventually she realised that friendships, like people, change over time.

Today she has fewer friends than she used to, but the relationships she still has are deeper and more meaningful.

# 7. Reading

Read.

Many adults are surprised to discover that friendships often become more difficult to maintain with age.

Unlike school or university, adult life provides fewer opportunities for regular contact. Careers, relationships and family responsibilities compete for people's time and attention.

As a result, friendships may fade without any conflict or disagreement.

Researchers suggest that while social circles tend to become smaller with age, emotional closeness often increases. In other words, people may have fewer friends, but stronger relationships.

Rather than viewing this change negatively, many experts consider it a natural part of life.
