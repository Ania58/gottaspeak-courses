# Lesson 60 – What Actually Makes People Happy?

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- discuss happiness and life satisfaction naturally
- evaluate different sources of happiness
- express and defend opinions about what makes life meaningful
- reflect on priorities, purpose and personal values

# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- life satisfaction – overall feeling that life is good
- fulfilment – deep sense of meaning and purpose
- wellbeing – physical and emotional health
- purpose – reason for doing things and direction in life
- relationships – connections with other people
- sense of belonging – feeling accepted and connected
- emotional support – help and understanding from others
- gratitude – appreciation for what you have
- resilience – ability to recover from difficulties
- meaningful life – life guided by values and purpose

# 7. Reading

Read.

For centuries, people have searched for the secret to happiness.

While money and success can improve quality of life, research consistently shows that lasting happiness depends on many factors beyond material wealth.

Strong relationships, a sense of purpose, physical health and emotional support all contribute to long-term wellbeing.

Interestingly, happiness is not a permanent state. Even people who describe themselves as happy still experience stress, sadness and disappointment.

Perhaps happiness is less about feeling good all the time and more about finding meaning, connection and balance throughout life.
