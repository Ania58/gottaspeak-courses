# Lesson 2 – Losing Face: Social Mistakes and Their Consequences

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- describe social mistakes with precision  
- analyse consequences of public embarrassment  
- express opinions using hedging and nuanced language  
- discuss social perception in a sophisticated way

## 4. Grammar – Hedging & Stance

At C1 level, we avoid sounding too direct or absolute.

Examples:

- It could be argued that people overreact to small mistakes.  
- There seems to be a tendency to judge others too quickly.  
- In many cases, what appears to be a mistake may simply be a misunderstanding.  
- It might not be entirely fair to assume negative intentions.

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

Social mistakes are rarely about the action itself; rather, they are about how that action is interpreted by others. A comment that seems harmless in one context may be perceived as inappropriate in another.

What makes these situations particularly challenging is the unpredictability of social norms. People rely on shared expectations, but these expectations are not always clear or consistent.

In many cases, individuals are judged not only for what they say or do, but for how well they read the situation. Failing to recognise subtle cues can lead to misunderstandings that escalate quickly.

However, it is worth noting that most people are more focused on their own behaviour than on the mistakes of others. While a moment may feel catastrophic, its actual impact is often far less significant than it seems.
