## 4. Grammar – Emphasis and Perspective

We often use inversion and emphasis to describe strong emotions:

- Never had I felt so embarrassed.  
- Rarely do people forget moments like that.  
- What made it worse was everyone laughing.  
- It wasn’t the mistake itself that bothered me, but the reaction.

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

Embarrassment is often dismissed as a minor inconvenience, yet it can feel overwhelmingly intense in the moment. What makes it particularly powerful is not the situation itself, but the awareness of being observed and judged.

In many cases, people are not reacting to the mistake, but to how they believe others perceive them. This internal narrative can quickly spiral into overthinking, causing individuals to replay the same moment repeatedly in their minds.

Interestingly, embarrassment tends to linger far longer than it should. Even when others have forgotten, the person involved may continue to feel self-conscious, as if the situation were still unfolding.

That said, embarrassment also reveals something important: a desire to be accepted. Without that social awareness, such moments would not carry the same emotional weight.
