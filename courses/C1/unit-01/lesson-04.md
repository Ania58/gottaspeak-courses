## 8. Reading

Read the text.

Embarrassing moments often become far more interesting when they are told as stories rather than remembered as isolated events. What initially feels uncomfortable can later be reframed as something almost humorous.

A well-told story does not simply describe what happened; it recreates the experience. By setting the scene, building tension and carefully choosing details, the speaker allows others to feel the situation as if they were there.

Interestingly, storytelling also changes perception. What once felt overwhelming can, over time, seem less significant when placed within a narrative structure. The act of telling the story gives the speaker control over the experience.

In this sense, storytelling is not just about communication — it is about reinterpretation.
