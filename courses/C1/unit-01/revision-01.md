# Revision – Unit 1

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- understand and analyse real-life embarrassing situations  
- interpret speaker intention and emotional nuance  
- use advanced vocabulary, idioms and narrative structures naturally  
- reflect critically on social behaviour and perception  

---

## 1. Vocabulary Activation

Complete naturally:

1. I completely __________ myself in front of everyone  
2. It was a minor mistake, but I managed to __________ it completely  
3. I couldn’t stop __________ the moment in my head  
4. What made it worse was that I __________ the situation  
5. At the time, I thought it was humiliating, but now I just __________ it  

---

## 2. Expression Upgrade

Rewrite using more advanced language:

1. It was very embarrassing  
2. I made a mistake  
3. People were looking at me  
4. I didn’t understand what was happening  
5. It became worse  

---

## 3. Narrative Control

Complete the ideas:

1. It all started when…  
2. At first, everything seemed…  
3. Then, out of nowhere…  
4. What made it even worse was…  
5. Looking back, I realise that…  

---

## 4. Idiomatic Precision

Use as many as possible:

- make a fool of yourself  
- lose face  
- get yourself into a mess  
- be caught off guard  
- make matters worse  
- blow something out of proportion  
- laugh something off  
- brush something off

Write a short paragraph (5–6 sentences) describing an embarrassing situation using **at least four idioms**.

## 8. Listening – Deep Analysis

Answer:

1. Why did Fiona’s mistake escalate so quickly?  
2. What made Amanda’s mistake particularly awkward?  
3. Why is Connor’s story both embarrassing and absurd?  
4. What role does misunderstanding play in each story?

## 9. Listening – Interpretation & Speculation

Use modal verbs:

1. Fiona might have thought that…  
2. The driver must have felt…  
3. Amanda may not have realised that…  
4. John could have reacted by…  
5. Connor must have been feeling…  

---

## 10. Critical Thinking

Reflect:

1. Why do small mistakes sometimes have big consequences?  
2. Are these situations objectively serious or just perceived that way?  
3. Why do people later laugh at embarrassing moments?  
4. How does storytelling change the emotional impact of these events?  

---

## 11. Speaking

Discuss:

1. Which story did you find the most memorable? Why?  
2. Have you ever experienced something similar?  
3. How would you react in each situation?  
4. Is it better to laugh situations off or analyse them?  

Use:
- idioms  
- narrative structures  
- speculation
