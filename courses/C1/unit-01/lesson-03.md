# Lesson 3 – Overthinking Social Situations

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- describe overthinking and internal reactions with precision

## 4. Grammar – Speculation & Modal Nuance

We use modal verbs to speculate about thoughts and reactions:

- They might have thought I was being rude.  
- I must have looked really uncomfortable.  
- She could have misunderstood what I meant.  
- I may have overreacted.  
- They can’t have taken it that seriously.

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

Overthinking is often less about the situation itself and more about the interpretation of it. A simple interaction can become a source of stress when it is analysed repeatedly and from multiple perspectives.

People tend to focus on small details, interpreting them as significant, even when they are not. This can lead to distorted conclusions, where neutral reactions are perceived as negative.

What makes overthinking particularly difficult to manage is the feeling of certainty it creates. The more a person reflects on a situation, the more convinced they become that their interpretation is correct.

However, this certainty is often misleading. In reality, most people are not analysing others to the same extent. The perceived judgment is frequently imagined rather than real.
