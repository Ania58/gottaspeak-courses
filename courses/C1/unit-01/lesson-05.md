## 4. Grammar – Advanced Reflection

We use conditionals and inversion to reflect:

- If I hadn’t gone through that, I wouldn’t have learned anything.  
- Had I reacted differently, the outcome might have changed.  
- Only later did I realise how insignificant it was.  
- Not until much later did I understand the situation.

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

Letting go of embarrassing moments is rarely immediate. In fact, people often hold on to such experiences long after they have lost their relevance. What keeps them alive is not the event itself, but the meaning attached to it.

Reframing allows individuals to reinterpret what happened. Instead of viewing a situation as a failure, it can be seen as part of a learning process. This shift in perspective does not erase the memory, but it changes its emotional impact.

Over time, what once felt overwhelming may come to seem insignificant. As people gain distance, they begin to understand that their past reactions were often influenced by insecurity rather than reality.

Ultimately, letting go is not about forgetting — it is about understanding.
