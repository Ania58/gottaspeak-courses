# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- persuasion – attempt to convince someone through reasoning or evidence
- manipulation – influencing someone unfairly or dishonestly for personal advantage
- emotional appeal – strategy that targets emotions rather than logical reasoning
- coercion – forcing someone to act through threats or pressure
- informed consent – agreement made after receiving sufficient information
- hidden agenda – undisclosed personal motive
- deceptive practice – intentionally misleading behaviour
- psychological pressure – emotional or social influence limiting free choice
- moral responsibility – ethical obligation to act fairly
- undue influence – excessive pressure that compromises independent decision-making

# 7. Reading

Read.

Influence is an unavoidable part of everyday life. Parents encourage children, teachers motivate students, advertisers promote products and politicians seek public support. Not all influence is unethical. In fact, persuasion often plays a positive role by helping people make informed decisions through evidence, logical reasoning and transparent communication.

Manipulation differs because it limits genuine freedom of choice. It may involve withholding information, exploiting emotions or deliberately creating misleading impressions. While manipulation sometimes appears effective in the short term, it often damages trust once people realise they have been deceived.

Ethicists therefore argue that the key distinction lies not only in the outcome but also in the method. Honest communication respects autonomy by allowing individuals to make informed decisions. Manipulation, by contrast, seeks to control decisions without fully respecting the other person's ability to choose independently.
