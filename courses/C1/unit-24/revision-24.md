# 4. Language From the Listening

Work with these expressions.

- binary mindset – tendency to interpret complex issues as consisting of only two opposing categories, ignoring nuance and intermediate possibilities
- epistemic humility – recognition that one's own knowledge is inherently limited and therefore open to revision
- confirmation bias – tendency to seek, interpret and remember information that supports existing beliefs while disregarding contradictory evidence
- cognitive flexibility – capacity to adapt one's thinking when presented with new evidence or alternative perspectives
- intellectual openness – willingness to seriously consider ideas that challenge one's existing beliefs without dismissing them prematurely
- implicit bias – unconscious attitude or stereotype that influences judgement without deliberate awareness
- perspective-taking – deliberate attempt to understand another person's viewpoint based on their experiences and beliefs
- evidence-based reasoning – process of reaching conclusions through careful evaluation of reliable evidence rather than intuition or emotion
- polarisation – process by which opinions become increasingly divided into opposing and uncompromising positions
- dogmatism – tendency to maintain beliefs with excessive certainty while resisting evidence that challenges them
