## 4. Grammar – Conditional Evaluation 

We evaluate situations hypothetically:

- Had overwork not become socially admired, burnout might be less widespread today  
- Were people encouraged to prioritise wellbeing, emotional exhaustion would likely decrease  
- If achievement were not tied so strongly to identity, many individuals might feel less pressure  
- Had society recognised burnout earlier, healthier work habits might have developed

## 5. Grammar – Cumulative Escalation 

We describe progressive psychological deterioration:

- Constant pressure gradually develops into emotional exhaustion, eventually leading to burnout  
- What begins as ambition may evolve into chronic stress and psychological depletion  
- Repeated overwork reinforces exhaustion, reducing resilience over time

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

Hustle culture promotes the belief that constant work and continuous achievement are signs of ambition and personal value. In many professional environments, exhaustion is no longer viewed as a warning sign, but as evidence of dedication.

What makes this mindset particularly dangerous is its psychological impact. Individuals begin to internalise the idea that slowing down reflects weakness or lack of discipline.

Over time, the boundary between healthy ambition and self-destructive behaviour becomes increasingly unclear. People continue pushing themselves despite emotional exhaustion, often ignoring clear signs of burnout.

As a result, burnout is becoming normalised rather than recognised as a serious emotional and psychological problem.
