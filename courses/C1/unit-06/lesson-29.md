## 4. Grammar – Counterfactual Evaluation 

We evaluate unreal or alternative realities:

- Had people not tied self-worth so strongly to achievement, failure might feel less threatening  
- Were mistakes viewed as part of growth, perfectionism would likely lose much of its power  
- If individuals did not fear judgment so intensely, they might take more creative risks  
- Had perfectionistic expectations not become normalised, many people might experience less anxiety

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

Perfectionism is often misunderstood as a positive trait associated with ambition and discipline. In reality, however, it is frequently driven by fear rather than motivation.

Many perfectionists tie their self-worth to performance, which means that mistakes are experienced not as normal setbacks, but as evidence of personal inadequacy. As a result, even minor failures can trigger intense anxiety and self-criticism.

What makes perfectionism particularly damaging is that it often prevents action altogether. Individuals become so afraid of producing imperfect results that they avoid taking risks or starting tasks entirely.

Over time, the pursuit of perfection may create chronic dissatisfaction, emotional exhaustion and a constant sense of never being good enough.
