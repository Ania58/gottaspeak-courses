## 8. Reading

Read the text.

Modern culture often presents success as something measurable through income, productivity and public recognition. However, increasing numbers of people are beginning to question whether these external markers truly lead to fulfilment.

Many individuals achieve professional success while still experiencing dissatisfaction, emotional exhaustion or a lack of purpose. In such cases, achievement may provide recognition without creating genuine wellbeing.

What makes this issue particularly significant is the growing awareness that fulfilment is deeply personal. Rather than pursuing socially approved definitions of success, some people are choosing to prioritise balance, relationships and emotional health.

As a result, success is gradually being redefined not as constant achievement, but as the ability to live meaningfully and sustainably.
