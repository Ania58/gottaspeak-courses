## 8. Reading

Read the text.

Modern society increasingly treats success as something visible, measurable and publicly displayed. Achievement is no longer simply personal; it has become performative and socially comparative.

Social media intensifies this process by exposing individuals to constant examples of success, wealth and productivity. As a result, many people begin measuring their own value against carefully curated representations of other people’s lives.

What makes this particularly harmful is that comparison rarely leads to satisfaction. Instead, success becomes a moving target, constantly reshaped by the achievements of others.

Over time, identity itself may become tied to external recognition, creating a cycle in which self-worth depends on visibility, status and achievement.
