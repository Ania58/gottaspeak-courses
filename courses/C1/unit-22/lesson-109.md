# Lesson 109 – Should We Work Fewer Hours?

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:

- discuss the advantages and disadvantages of shorter working hours
- evaluate different working models
- analyse the relationship between productivity and working time
- express informed opinions about the future of employment

---

## Situation 1 – The Four-Day Week

Your company offers two options:

### Option A

- five days
- 8 hours per day

### Option B

- four days
- 10 hours per day

Salary remains identical.

Which option would you choose?

---

## Situation 2 – Unlimited Overtime

Your employer pays double for every hour of overtime.

Many colleagues regularly work sixty hours per week.

Would you join them?

---

## Situation 3 – Flexible Hours

Employees may choose when they start work.

Some begin at 6:00 a.m.

Others start after midday.

Team collaboration becomes more difficult.

Should flexibility have limits?

---

## Situation 4 – Productivity Targets

A company announces:

"If employees complete all their work early, they may leave immediately without losing pay."

Would this improve productivity?

---

## Situation 5 – Artificial Intelligence

AI now completes half of your daily tasks.

Management must decide whether employees should:

- work fewer hours,
- produce more,
- or reduce staff numbers.

Which solution seems fairest?

---

## Discussion

1. Which situation would create the biggest challenge?
2. Can companies remain competitive while reducing working hours?
3. Would fewer hours improve mental health?
4. Should salary depend on hours worked or results achieved?

# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- compressed workweek – working the usual number of hours over fewer days
- labour productivity – amount of work produced within a given period
- flexible scheduling – allowing employees to choose when they work
- occupational stress – pressure caused by workplace demands
- employee retention – ability of an organisation to keep its employees
- workforce wellbeing – overall physical and mental health of employees
- absenteeism – repeated absence from work, often because of illness or dissatisfaction
- output-based performance – evaluation based on results rather than time spent working
- work intensity – amount of effort required during working hours
- organisational efficiency – ability of a company to achieve goals while using resources effectively

# 7. Reading

Read.

For more than a century, the standard working week has remained relatively stable despite enormous technological progress. However, increasing automation has prompted many economists and business leaders to question whether traditional working patterns remain appropriate.

Several organisations that have experimented with shorter working weeks report improvements in employee wellbeing, job satisfaction and labour productivity. Employees often experience lower stress levels and fewer absences while maintaining similar levels of output.

Critics argue that reduced working hours may not be suitable for every profession. Healthcare, emergency services and manufacturing often require continuous staffing, making shorter schedules more difficult to implement. Nevertheless, many experts believe that future workplaces will increasingly prioritise flexibility, efficiency and employee wellbeing over simply measuring hours spent at work.
