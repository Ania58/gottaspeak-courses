# Lesson 110 – What Does Success Mean Today?

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:

- discuss different definitions of success
- analyse how personal values influence career choices
- evaluate the relationship between money, achievement and happiness
- express nuanced opinions about ambition and fulfilment

---

# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- personal fulfilment – deep sense of satisfaction gained from living according to one's values and goals
- intrinsic motivation – desire to do something because it is personally meaningful rather than externally rewarded
- extrinsic motivation – desire driven by external rewards such as money, recognition or status
- life satisfaction – overall assessment of one's quality of life
- career achievement – significant professional accomplishment or advancement
- social recognition – respect or admiration received from other people
- value-driven lifestyle – way of living guided primarily by personal beliefs and principles
- professional accomplishment – important success within one's career
- meaningful contribution – positive impact made on other people or society
- personal legacy – lasting influence someone leaves behind after their lifetime

# 7. Reading

Read.

Success has traditionally been associated with wealth, status and professional achievement. However, modern research increasingly suggests that these external indicators explain only part of overall life satisfaction. While financial security contributes to wellbeing, its influence gradually decreases once basic needs have been met.

Psychologists distinguish between intrinsic and extrinsic motivation. People driven primarily by intrinsic motivation often pursue meaningful goals, personal growth and strong relationships. Those motivated mainly by external rewards may achieve impressive careers but sometimes experience lower long-term fulfilment if those rewards become their only measure of success.

Consequently, many researchers argue that genuine success depends less on public recognition and more on living consistently with one's values, maintaining healthy relationships and finding purpose in everyday life.
