# Lesson 106 – Will AI Replace Your Job?

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:

- discuss the impact of artificial intelligence on employment
- evaluate which jobs are most vulnerable to automation
- distinguish between human and machine strengths
- express informed opinions about the future labour market

---

# 1. Opening Question

Read the statement.

## "Artificial intelligence will transform every profession, but not necessarily eliminate it."

Do you agree?

---

## Discussion

1. How has AI already changed your work or studies?
2. Which professions are most likely to disappear?
3. Which human skills will remain valuable?
4. Does AI make you feel optimistic or concerned?

# 2. Five Workplace Scenarios

Read each situation.

Your task:

- Decide what you would do.
- Explain your reasoning.
- Discuss the possible consequences.

---

## Situation 1 – The Accountant

An experienced accountant discovers that new AI software can complete most routine financial reports in a few minutes.

The company plans to reduce the number of employees.

Should the accountant stay or retrain?

---

## Situation 2 – The Translator

A freelance translator notices that many clients are switching to AI translation tools.

The translations are not perfect, but they are much cheaper.

How should the translator respond?

---

## Situation 3 – The Doctor

An AI system identifies medical conditions more accurately than many doctors.

However, patients still prefer discussing diagnoses with a human physician.

Should AI make the final decision?

---

## Situation 4 – The Teacher

A school introduces an AI tutor that provides personalised lessons and instant feedback.

Some parents argue that teachers will soon become unnecessary.

Do you agree?

---

## Situation 5 – The Job Interview

A company announces that every CV and first-round interview will be evaluated entirely by AI.

Candidates never speak to a human until the final stage.

Is this fair?

---

## Discussion

1. Which profession is safest from automation?
2. Which situation raises the biggest ethical concern?
3. Should AI replace repetitive work?
4. Which decisions should always remain human?

# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- workforce automation – replacement of human labour with automated technologies
- technological disruption – significant change caused by new technology that transforms industries or professions
- transferable skills – abilities that remain valuable across different jobs and careers
- human oversight – supervision by people to ensure technology functions responsibly
- reskilling – learning completely new skills for a different profession
- upskilling – improving existing professional skills to remain competitive
- labour market – supply of workers and available employment opportunities
- routine task automation – use of technology to perform repetitive activities
- competitive advantage – quality that makes someone or something more successful than others
- human-centred work – work requiring empathy, creativity, ethical judgement or complex interpersonal interaction

# 7. Reading

Read.

Artificial intelligence is transforming the workplace at an unprecedented pace. While many routine tasks can now be completed more quickly and accurately by machines, experts generally agree that AI is more likely to change jobs than eliminate them entirely.

Historically, technological revolutions have both displaced workers and created entirely new professions. The key difference today is the speed of change. As automation expands into knowledge-based occupations, employees increasingly need to develop transferable skills such as critical thinking, communication, creativity and adaptability.

Rather than competing directly with AI, many professionals are learning how to collaborate with it. Human judgement, ethical reasoning and emotional intelligence remain difficult to automate, making these abilities increasingly valuable in the modern labour market.
