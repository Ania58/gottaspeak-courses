## 4. Grammar – Intensity Scaling 

We describe gradual escalation:

- The more emotionally available people are expected to be, the more psychologically exhausted they become  
- Constant exposure to stimulation gradually increases emotional fatigue  
- What begins as ordinary stress can develop into long-term burnout  
- Repeated emotional pressure intensifies the feeling of exhaustion over time

## 5. Grammar – Evaluative Inversion 

We use inversion for emphasis and evaluation:

- Rarely have people been expected to remain socially available at all times  
- Never before has emotional accessibility been so constant  
- Only later do many individuals realise how emotionally exhausted they have become  
- Not only does constant connectivity increase pressure, but it also reduces recovery time

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

Modern communication creates the expectation of constant emotional availability. Messages arrive continuously, social interaction never fully stops, and many individuals feel pressure to remain responsive at all times.

What makes this particularly exhausting is the cumulative nature of emotional stimulation. Even brief interactions require attention, interpretation and emotional energy.

Over time, this constant demand can lead to emotional burnout. Individuals may begin to feel detached, mentally depleted and unable to recover fully.

Paradoxically, a society built around connection may also be contributing to widespread emotional exhaustion.
