## 5. Grammar – Evaluative Concession

- Although online platforms encourage creativity, they also reward performance over authenticity

- Even though people appear more open online, many interactions remain carefully controlled

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

Modern social media platforms encourage constant self-presentation. Individuals are expected not only to communicate, but also to construct identities that attract attention and approval.

What complicates this process is the growing pressure to appear authentic while simultaneously managing perception. As a result, authenticity itself can become performative.

Rather than expressing identity naturally, many users curate carefully controlled versions of themselves. Imperfections are hidden, emotions are filtered and experiences are shaped for visibility.

The consequence is a growing tension between genuine self-expression and social performance. In some cases, individuals may become so focused on maintaining an image that they gradually lose connection with their authentic identity.
