# 4. Language From the Listening

Work with these expressions.

- circular reporting – repeated citation of information that ultimately comes from the same unverified source
- primary source – original source from which information first originates
- information integrity – reliability and accuracy of information throughout its transmission
- unsupported claim – statement presented without sufficient evidence
- information cascade – situation in which people accept information because others already have
- credibility assessment – process of evaluating whether a source is trustworthy
- confirmation bias – tendency to accept information that supports existing beliefs
- media amplification – process through which media significantly increase the reach of information
- critical scrutiny – careful examination of information before accepting it
- factual verification – process of checking whether information is accurate
