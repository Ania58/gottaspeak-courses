## 4. Grammar – Evaluative Concession

- While digital platforms provide valuable access to information, they may simultaneously encourage compulsive behaviour

- Although technology offers convenience, it may also fragment attention and reduce mental recovery

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

Modern digital platforms compete intensely for human attention. Their design frequently relies on reward systems, emotional stimulation and personalised content that encourages users to remain engaged for extended periods.

One increasingly common behaviour associated with this environment is doomscrolling — the compulsive consumption of negative or emotionally distressing information. Although individuals often begin scrolling in search of understanding or certainty, the experience may ultimately reinforce stress and emotional fatigue.

What makes doomscrolling particularly difficult to resist is that negative information captures attention more effectively than neutral content. Algorithms may therefore prioritise emotionally intense material because it generates stronger engagement.

Over time, constant exposure to stimulating information may contribute to fragmented attention, cognitive overload and reduced psychological recovery.
