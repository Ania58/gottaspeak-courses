## 4. Grammar – Psychological Causation 

We explain mental and emotional mechanisms:

- The tendency to overthink often stems from uncertainty and fear of making mistakes  
- Rumination may be reinforced by unresolved emotional experiences  
- Decision paralysis tends to emerge when individuals fear negative consequences excessively  
- Mental loops are often sustained by the need for certainty and control

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

Overthinking is often mistaken for careful analysis or responsibility. However, psychological research suggests that rumination rarely produces effective solutions and instead tends to reinforce stress and emotional discomfort.

Many people replay conversations, decisions or imagined future scenarios repeatedly, believing that additional thinking will eventually produce certainty or control. In reality, this process often intensifies anxiety rather than reducing it.

What makes overthinking particularly difficult is that the brain may interpret uncertainty as a threat requiring immediate resolution. As a result, individuals become trapped in cognitive loops that feel productive but lead nowhere.

Over time, chronic rumination may contribute to emotional exhaustion, indecision and reduced psychological wellbeing.
