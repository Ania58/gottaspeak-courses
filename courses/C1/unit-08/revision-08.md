# Revision – Unit 8

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- analyse overthinking and anxiety critically  
- discuss distraction and mental wellbeing using advanced language  
- express balanced opinions and emotional explanations naturally  
- reflect on decision-making and psychological wellbeing  

---

## 1. Vocabulary Activation

Complete naturally:

1. Overthinking often leads to decision __________  
2. Constant stimulation may contribute to cognitive __________  
3. Many people struggle with uncertainty and emotional __________  
4. Digital distraction can fragment our __________  
5. Recovery often depends on healthy emotional __________  

---

## 2. Expression Upgrade

Rewrite using more advanced language:

1. People think too much  
2. Anxiety affects decisions  
3. Phones distract us  
4. People struggle to relax  
5. Recovery takes time  

---

## 3. Analytical Development

Complete the ideas:

1. What makes overthinking difficult is…  
2. Anxiety often becomes stronger when…  
3. One possible reason people struggle to switch off is…  
4. The issue may not be technology itself, but…  
5. Mental recovery becomes harder when…  

---

## 4. Idiomatic & Natural Language

Use as many as possible:

- dwell on something  
- get stuck in your head  
- lose your train of thought  
- struggle to switch off  
- feel mentally drained  
- go around in circles  
- bounce back  
- take a step back  

---

### Task

Write a short paragraph (6–7 sentences) about stress or overthinking using **at least four idioms/phrasal verbs**.

## 6. Listening – General Understanding

Answer:

1. What is overthinking according to the speakers?  
2. What is analysis paralysis?  
3. Why does overthinking make decisions harder?  
4. What strategies do the speakers suggest to reduce overthinking?  

---

## 7. Listening – Key Details

Complete:

1. Overthinking means thinking too much about a __________ or situation  
2. Analysis paralysis happens when people try to make the __________ choice  
3. Overthinking often makes people feel more worried and __________  
4. One suggested strategy is asking whether something is really such a big __________  
5. Giving yourself a time __________ may help decision-making  
6. The speakers mention trusting your __________ or instinct  
7. Taking a break and writing things down may help organise your __________  
8. The speakers say balance is important when making __________  

---

## 8. Listening – Deep Analysis

Answer:

1. Why does overthinking create stress and self-doubt?  
2. Why can trying to make the perfect decision become counterproductive?  
3. Why might instinct sometimes be useful?  
4. What role does balance play in decision-making?  

---

## 9. Listening – Interpretation & Evaluation

Use natural advanced language:

1. What I found interesting was…  
2. The listening suggests that…  
3. While thinking carefully is important, it can also…  
4. One possible problem with overthinking is…  
5. In some situations, people may benefit from…

## 10. Cause–Effect Mapping

Create chains.

👉 Example:

uncertainty → repetitive thinking → stress → decision paralysis  

Now create **2 more chains** based on:
- overthinking  
- digital distraction

## 11. Critical Thinking

Reflect:

1. Why do people often distrust their first instinct?  
2. Is overthinking becoming more common today?  
3. Can too much thinking become a form of avoidance?  
4. How can people make decisions more confidently?  

---

## 12. Speaking

Discuss:

1. Why do people overthink?  
2. Is uncertainty becoming harder to tolerate?  
3. How does distraction affect emotional wellbeing?  
4. What helps people stay mentally balanced?  

Use:
- balanced opinions  
- cause and effect  
- qualified opinions

- explanation
