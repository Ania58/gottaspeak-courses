## 4. Grammar – Giving Balanced Opinions

We avoid sounding too extreme:

- Resilience is important, although people respond to stress differently  
- Recovery may take longer for some individuals than for others  
- While emotional strength matters, support systems can be equally important  
- Stress is not always harmful, but chronic stress may become damaging

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

Modern life often rewards productivity and constant availability, leaving little space for emotional recovery. As a result, many people struggle not only with stress itself, but also with the absence of meaningful recovery.

Resilience does not necessarily mean avoiding emotional difficulty. Instead, it often involves adapting to challenges, processing emotions and gradually rebuilding balance after stressful experiences.

What makes recovery difficult is that emotional exhaustion can be invisible. Individuals may continue functioning outwardly while feeling psychologically overwhelmed internally.

Over time, however, healthy boundaries, supportive relationships and sustainable routines may strengthen emotional resilience and improve wellbeing.
