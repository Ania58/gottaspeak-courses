# Lesson 72 – Things We Wish We Knew Earlier

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- discuss life lessons and regrets naturally
- talk about personal growth and maturity
- give advice and reflect on the past
- express hypothetical thoughts about different choices

---

# 2. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- hindsight – understanding a situation better after it happened
- life lesson – important thing learned through experience
- regret – sadness about something done or not done
- perspective – way of looking at life
- missed opportunity – chance that was not taken
- overthinking – thinking excessively about something
- priorities – things considered most important
- self-doubt – lack of confidence in yourself
- maturity – ability to make sensible decisions
- personal growth – development as a person

# 6. Reading

Read.

People often believe that wisdom comes from age, but it may come more from experience and reflection.

Many older adults report that their biggest regrets are not things they did, but opportunities they failed to take. Fear, self-doubt and the desire to avoid failure sometimes prevent people from pursuing what matters most.

Interestingly, psychologists suggest that regret can be useful. Reflecting on past mistakes helps people understand themselves better and make wiser decisions in the future.

Perhaps the goal is not to avoid regrets completely, but to learn from them and avoid becoming trapped by them.
