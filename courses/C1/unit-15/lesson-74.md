# Lesson 74 – Would You Want to Be Young Forever?

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- discuss immortality and ageing naturally
- express opinions about life, death and purpose
- analyse hypothetical situations involving eternal youth
- reflect on the importance of change and limited time

---

# 1. Thought Experiment

Scientists have invented a treatment.

You can remain physically 25 years old forever.

You will:

- never age
- never become ill
- keep your memories
- continue living indefinitely

Would you accept it?

---

## Initial Reaction

Discuss.

1. Would you say yes immediately?
2. What would be the biggest advantage?
3. What would worry you?
4. Would eternal youth eventually become boring?

---

# 2. Four Possibilities

## Scenario A

---

## Scenario B

---

## Scenario C

---

## Scenario D

---

## Discussion

1. Which scenario seems most attractive?
2. Which one seems frightening?
3. Does death give life meaning?

# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- mortality – the fact that humans eventually die
- finite lifespan – limited amount of time someone lives
- outlive someone – live longer than another person
- fear of mortality – anxiety about death and ageing
- existential question – question about meaning and purpose
- sense of purpose – feeling that life has meaning
- come to terms with something – gradually accept an unpleasant reality
- passage of time – the way time moves and affects life
- fleeting moment – something beautiful that lasts only briefly
- legacy – what people leave behind after they die

# 6. The 500-Year Life

Imagine humans naturally lived for 500 years.

People would:

- start careers at 100
- have children at 200
- retire at 450

---

## Questions

1. Would people become wiser?
2. Would they procrastinate more?
3. Would relationships become stronger or weaker?
4. Would life become less exciting?

# 7. Reading

Read.

Throughout history, humans have searched for ways to extend life and overcome death.

Yet many philosophers argue that mortality gives life urgency and meaning. Knowing that time is limited encourages people to value relationships, pursue goals and appreciate fleeting moments.

Some scientists believe radical life extension may become possible in the future. However, many questions remain unanswered.

Would immortality bring happiness or boredom? Would society become stagnant? Would people still dream, take risks and value experiences?

Perhaps the meaning of life lies not in escaping death, but in learning how to live well despite it.
