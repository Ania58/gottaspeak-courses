# Lesson 73 – Midlife Crisis: Myth or Reality?

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- discuss major life changes and dissatisfaction naturally
- talk about identity, routine and personal fulfilment
- analyse different responses to ageing
- express opinions about change and self-discovery

---

# 1. The Investigation

Three people reached their late forties and reacted very differently.

Your task:

- Decide who is experiencing a midlife crisis.
- Identify what each person is searching for.
- Decide whose approach seems healthiest.

---

## Case A – Mark

After twenty years in banking, Mark suddenly quit his job and became a photographer.

His friends think he has lost his mind, but Mark says he has never felt happier.

---

## Case B – Laura

Laura bought an expensive sports car and started dressing like she was twenty years younger.

She constantly talks about getting older and worries about losing her attractiveness.

---

## Case C – James

James stayed exactly the same.

He has a stable routine, enjoys family life and says he doesn't understand why people suddenly want to reinvent themselves.

---

# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- reinvent yourself – completely change your lifestyle or identity
- hit a plateau – stop making progress or feeling excited
- feel stuck in a rut – feel trapped in a repetitive and boring routine
- have unfinished business – feel that something important remains undone
- fear missing out – worry that life opportunities are disappearing
- seek fulfilment – actively search for meaning or satisfaction
- reassess priorities – reconsider what matters most
- chase youth – desperately try to remain young
- shake things up – introduce change to avoid boredom
- settle into a routine – become comfortable with regular habits

# 7. Reading

Read.

Although the term "midlife crisis" is widely used, psychologists disagree about whether it truly exists.

Many people experience periods of self-reflection during middle age. Children become independent, careers reach a plateau and people become increasingly aware of their own mortality.

As a result, some individuals begin reassessing their priorities and searching for greater fulfilment.

However, dramatic changes are not necessarily signs of crisis. They may simply reflect personal growth and a desire to live more intentionally.

Rather than being a breakdown, midlife can sometimes become a period of renewal.
