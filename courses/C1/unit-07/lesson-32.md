## 8. Reading

Read the text.

Social media platforms increasingly rely on algorithms designed to maximise engagement. Rather than exposing users to balanced perspectives, these systems often prioritise content that reinforces existing beliefs or provokes strong emotional reactions.

As a result, users may gradually become trapped in informational bubbles where alternative viewpoints are rarely encountered. This process contributes to ideological polarisation and weakens critical engagement with opposing perspectives.

What makes this phenomenon particularly concerning is that users often perceive these experiences as natural or self-directed. In reality, however, information exposure may be heavily shaped by invisible recommendation systems.

Over time, repeated exposure to emotionally charged content can distort perception, intensify division and reduce openness to disagreement.
