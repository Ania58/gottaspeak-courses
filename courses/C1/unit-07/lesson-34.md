## 4. Grammar – Discursive Contrast 

We contrast appearance with reality:

- What appears to be concern may in fact function as emotional control  
- While manipulation is often subtle, its psychological effects can be severe  
- Gaslighting does not necessarily involve obvious lies; rather, it gradually distorts perception  
- What makes manipulation difficult to identify is that it is frequently disguised as care, humour or honesty

## 5. Grammar – Interpretive Distancing & Psychological Inference 

We analyse behaviour cautiously and intelligently:

- The behaviour could reasonably be interpreted as emotionally manipulative  
- What is presented as “honesty” may in fact serve to undermine confidence  
- Repeated emotional invalidation is likely to weaken self-trust over time  
- The individual may gradually become dependent on external emotional reassurance

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

Psychological manipulation often operates subtly rather than aggressively. Instead of relying on direct control, manipulators may gradually influence emotions, perception and self-confidence over time.

One particularly damaging form of manipulation is gaslighting. In these situations, individuals are repeatedly encouraged to doubt their own memory, interpretation or emotional reactions. As a result, they may become increasingly uncertain about their perception of reality.

What makes manipulation especially dangerous is that it is frequently disguised as concern, honesty or emotional sensitivity. Victims may therefore struggle to recognise the behaviour clearly.

Over time, repeated emotional invalidation can weaken confidence, increase dependency and create long-term psychological distress.
