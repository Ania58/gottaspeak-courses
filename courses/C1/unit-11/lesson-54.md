# Lesson 54 – Luck vs Hard Work

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- discuss success and opportunity in a nuanced way
- analyse the role of luck and effort in life outcomes
- challenge assumptions and defend opinions
- talk about privilege, talent, timing and persistence

---

# 1. The Big Question

Read the statement.

## "People are far more successful because of hard work than because of luck."

---

## Initial Reaction

Choose your position.

- Strongly agree
- Mostly agree
- Unsure
- Mostly disagree
- Strongly disagree

Explain why.

# 2. The Investigation

Read the situations.

## Person A

Maria grew up in a wealthy family.

She attended excellent schools, received financial support throughout university and had professional contacts before starting her career.

Today she runs a successful company.

---

## Person B

Daniel grew up in a low-income household.

He worked part-time while studying, graduated with debt and spent years building his career without professional connections.

Today he earns a comfortable living but still feels he had to work twice as hard as others.

---

## Person C

Sophie launched a business shortly before demand for her product suddenly exploded because of an unexpected global trend.

She became financially successful within a year.

---

# Discussion

1. Who benefited most from luck?
2. Who relied most on effort?
3. Can success ever be explained by a single factor?
4. Are these examples fair comparisons?

# 7. Reading

Read.

People often prefer simple explanations for success. Some believe hard work determines everything, while others argue that luck is the deciding factor.

In reality, success is usually influenced by multiple elements working together. Effort may create opportunities, but timing, circumstances and external support can also play important roles.

One difficulty is that luck is often invisible. People can clearly see their own hard work, but they may be less aware of advantages they received along the way.

At the same time, luck alone rarely produces long-term success without persistence, adaptability and effort.

For this reason, many experts argue that success is neither entirely earned nor entirely accidental.
