# Lesson 53 – Taking Risks

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- discuss risk-taking and uncertainty naturally
- evaluate opportunities and potential consequences
- defend opinions during a debate
- talk about courage, caution and personal growth

---

# 1. Debate Starter

Read the statement.

## "The biggest risk in life is not taking any risks."

---

## Initial Reaction

Choose your position.

- Strongly agree
- Mostly agree
- Unsure
- Mostly disagree
- Strongly disagree

Explain your answer.

# 2. Risk Scenarios

Read the situations.

## Situation A

You are offered a job abroad.

The salary is good and the opportunity is exciting, but you would need to leave your family, friends and familiar environment.

Would you go?

---

## Situation B

You have enough savings to start a business.

The idea has potential, but there is no guarantee of success.

Would you take the risk?

---

## Situation C

You have strong feelings for someone but are afraid of rejection.

Would you tell them?

---

## Situation D

You are considering returning to education after many years.

The course is expensive and demanding.

Would you enrol?

# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- calculated risk – risk taken after careful consideration
- uncertainty – lack of certainty about the future
- comfort zone – familiar and safe situation
- opportunity cost – what you give up by choosing something else
- potential reward – possible positive outcome
- downside – negative aspect or possible disadvantage
- risk tolerance – willingness to accept uncertainty
- life-changing decision – decision with major consequences
- fear of failure – concern about not succeeding
- leap of faith – decision taken despite uncertainty

# 7. Reading

Read.

Risk is often associated with danger, but many of life's biggest opportunities involve uncertainty.

Changing careers, moving abroad, starting a business or beginning a relationship all involve outcomes that cannot be predicted completely.

People differ greatly in their tolerance for risk. Some are comfortable making decisions despite uncertainty, while others prefer stability and predictability.

Neither approach is automatically better. Excessive caution may lead to missed opportunities, while excessive risk-taking may create unnecessary problems.

The challenge is finding a balance between courage and good judgement.

---
