# Lesson 51 – Why We Procrastinate

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- discuss procrastination and avoidance behaviours naturally
- analyse the reasons people delay important tasks
- talk about motivation, perfectionism and fear of failure
- suggest practical solutions to common productivity problems

---

# 1. Case Study

Read the situation.

Oliver has been talking about launching his own online business for almost two years.

He has read books, watched videos, listened to podcasts and even created a detailed business plan. Whenever someone asks about the project, he says he is "still preparing".

The problem is that very little actual progress has been made.

Every week he finds a new reason to postpone the launch. Sometimes he says he needs more research. Sometimes he decides the website is not ready. Other times he convinces himself that the timing is wrong.

Deep down, Oliver knows he is avoiding something. He worries that if he launches the business and it fails, he will feel embarrassed and disappointed.

As a result, he remains stuck in preparation mode while telling himself that he is being productive.

# 3. Language From the Situation

Work with these expressions.

- procrastination – delaying something that needs to be done
- avoidance behaviour – actions that help someone avoid discomfort
- perfectionism – wanting everything to be flawless
- fear of failure – worry about not succeeding
- preparation mode – constantly planning without taking action
- self-sabotage – behaviour that damages your own progress
- comfort zone – a familiar and safe situation
- take action – begin doing something practical
- overprepare – prepare more than necessary
- missed opportunity – a chance that was not taken

# 7. Reading

Read.

Many people assume procrastination is caused by laziness. However, research suggests that procrastination is often emotional rather than practical.

People delay tasks because the task creates discomfort. The discomfort may come from fear, uncertainty, boredom or self-doubt.

Ironically, procrastination often provides short-term relief while creating long-term stress. Avoiding a task feels better temporarily, but the problem remains unresolved.

For this reason, overcoming procrastination often involves managing emotions rather than managing time.
