# Lesson 55 – The Roads Not Taken

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- discuss regret and missed opportunities naturally
- reflect on important life choices
- talk about alternative outcomes and life paths
- express complex feelings about past decisions

---

# 1. Story Starter

Read the story.

Ten years ago, Ethan was offered a chance to move abroad.

At the time, it seemed like the opportunity of a lifetime. The salary was excellent, the city was exciting and the position offered significant career growth.

However, accepting the offer would have meant leaving behind his family, friends and long-term partner.

After weeks of thinking, Ethan decided to stay.

Life continued. He built a successful career, got married and remained close to the people he cared about.

Yet every now and then, he catches himself wondering what his life would look like if he had taken that opportunity.

He does not necessarily regret his decision, but he remains curious about the version of himself that never existed.

---

3. Can someone be happy and still wonder "what if"?
4. Does wondering about the past mean regret?

# 3. Language From the Story

Work with these expressions.

- missed opportunity – a chance that was not taken
- alternative path – a different direction life could have taken
- turning point – important moment that changes future events
- life-changing decision – decision with major consequences
- uncertainty – lack of certainty about future outcomes
- hindsight – understanding gained after an event
- regret – sadness about a past decision or action
- curiosity – desire to know or understand something
- crossroads – moment requiring an important choice
- perspective – way of viewing a situation

# 7. Reading

Read.

Most people experience regret at some point in their lives. Regret often emerges when people imagine how life might have been different if they had made another choice.

Interestingly, psychological research suggests that people tend to regret inaction more than action over the long term. In other words, people often feel more regret about opportunities they never pursued than about mistakes they actually made.

However, regret is not always negative. It can encourage reflection, personal growth and better decision-making in the future.

The challenge lies in learning from the past without becoming trapped by it.
