# Lesson 98 – Why Do We Care So Much About Other People's Opinions?

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

- discuss the influence of social approval on behaviour
- analyse the role of reputation and public image
- evaluate the effects of external judgement on decision-making
- express nuanced opinions about confidence and authenticity

# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- social validation – approval or acceptance received from other people
- fear of negative evaluation – anxiety about being judged unfavourably by others
- self-consciousness – heightened awareness of how one appears to other people
- reputational risk – possibility of damaging one's public image
- impression management – deliberate effort to influence how others perceive us
- external validation – confidence based primarily on other people's approval
- perceived social norm – behaviour that people believe is expected by society
- authentic self-expression – behaving according to one's genuine beliefs and values
- social conformity – tendency to adjust behaviour to match the expectations of others
- identity construction – ongoing process of shaping one's personal and social identity

# 7. Reading

Read.

Human beings are naturally sensitive to the opinions of others because social acceptance has long been essential for survival. Throughout history, belonging to a group increased the chances of protection, cooperation and access to resources. As a result, concern about reputation became deeply rooted in human behaviour.

In modern societies, however, this instinct can become excessive. Constant exposure to social media, public feedback and comparison with others may encourage people to measure their worth through external validation rather than personal values or achievements.

Psychologists emphasise that confidence does not require ignoring other people's opinions entirely. Instead, emotionally healthy individuals learn to distinguish between constructive criticism, which supports personal growth, and unnecessary judgement, which should not determine their self-worth.
