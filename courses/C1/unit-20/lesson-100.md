# Lesson 100 – Can You Really Change Someone?

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:

- discuss whether people can fundamentally change
- analyse the influence of relationships and life experiences
- evaluate the role of motivation in personal development
- defend complex opinions using evidence and examples

---

# 1. Opening Question

Read the statement.

## "People never really change—they only reveal who they truly are."

Do you agree?

---

## Discussion

1. Can people fundamentally change?
2. What influences personality the most?
3. Can love change someone?
4. Do people change because they want to or because they have to?

# 2. Five Situations

Read each situation.

Your task:

- Decide whether lasting change is realistic.
- Explain what factors could influence the outcome.
- Discuss whether you would behave differently.

---

## Situation 1 – The Promise

Your partner promises to become more reliable after repeatedly cancelling plans at the last minute.

They genuinely apologise and seem determined to improve.

Would you believe them?

---

## Situation 2 – The Employee

A colleague has been disorganised for years.

After receiving a promotion, they suddenly become highly responsible and dependable.

Can people change that quickly?

---

## Situation 3 – The Former Bully

Someone who bullied classmates at school apologises publicly twenty years later.

They now work for a charity supporting young people.

Should their past continue to define them?

---

## Situation 4 – The Addict

A man who struggled with addiction for many years has been sober for a decade.

Some people still refuse to trust him.

Is that fair?

---

## Situation 5 – The Friend

Your best friend has become increasingly negative and pessimistic.

You have tried encouraging them for years without success.

Do you continue trying to help or accept that only they can change themselves?

---

## Discussion

1. Which situation is the most difficult?
2. Should trust be rebuilt gradually?
3. Can people truly leave the past behind?
4. What usually motivates genuine change?

# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- behavioural transformation – significant and lasting change in someone's behaviour
- intrinsic motivation – desire to change driven by internal values rather than external pressure
- personality trait – relatively stable characteristic influencing behaviour
- self-awareness – understanding of one's own thoughts, emotions and behaviour
- behavioural pattern – repeated way of thinking or acting over time
- personal accountability – willingness to accept responsibility for one's actions
- emotional resilience – ability to recover from setbacks and adversity
- growth mindset – belief that abilities and behaviour can improve through effort
- identity shift – significant change in the way someone sees themselves
- sustainable change – improvement that continues over a long period rather than temporarily

# 7. Reading

Read.

Psychologists generally agree that human beings are capable of change, but not all aspects of personality are equally flexible. Stable personality traits often remain relatively consistent throughout adulthood, whereas habits, attitudes and patterns of behaviour can change considerably.

Long-term behavioural transformation usually requires more than good intentions. Researchers highlight the importance of intrinsic motivation, self-awareness and repeated practice. External pressure may produce temporary improvements, but lasting change is more likely when individuals genuinely believe in the value of changing.

Relationships can also play an important role. Supportive environments encourage growth, while unhealthy relationships may reinforce negative behavioural patterns. Ultimately, meaningful change depends on both personal commitment and external circumstances.
