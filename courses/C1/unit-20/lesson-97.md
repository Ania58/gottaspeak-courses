# Lesson 97 – Why Is Saying "No" So Difficult?

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:

- discuss why people struggle to refuse requests
- analyse situations involving social pressure and guilt

- express opinions about assertiveness and responsibility

# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- assertive communication – expressing your needs clearly and respectfully without being aggressive
- emotional blackmail – using guilt, fear or obligation to manipulate another person's behaviour
- interpersonal boundaries – personal limits that protect emotional wellbeing and healthy relationships
- people-pleasing behaviour – habit of prioritising others' approval over one's own needs
- conflict avoidance – tendency to avoid disagreement even when it causes personal disadvantage
- emotional labour – effort required to manage emotions in social or professional situations
- reciprocal obligation – expectation that kindness or support will eventually be returned
- self-advocacy – ability to defend your own interests and needs appropriately
- guilt-driven decision-making – making choices primarily to avoid feeling guilty
- boundary violation – situation in which someone's personal limits are ignored or disrespected

# 7. Reading

Read.

Many people find it surprisingly difficult to refuse requests, even when agreeing causes stress or inconvenience. Psychologists suggest that this behaviour often stems from a desire to maintain positive relationships, avoid conflict or gain social approval.

While cooperation is an important part of healthy relationships, constantly prioritising other people's needs may lead to emotional exhaustion, resentment and burnout. Individuals who regularly ignore their own boundaries often experience increasing pressure as others begin to expect their availability.

Assertiveness offers an alternative. Rather than choosing between aggression and passive compliance, assertive communication allows people to express their needs honestly while continuing to treat others with respect. Research suggests that clear boundaries often strengthen relationships by creating realistic expectations and reducing misunderstandings.
