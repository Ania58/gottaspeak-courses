# Lesson 85 – What Makes Someone Charismatic?

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- discuss charisma and leadership naturally
- analyse communication styles
- describe personal qualities and influence
- express opinions about confidence and likeability

---

# 1. Who Would You Follow?

Imagine you have to choose one team leader.

Read the profiles.

---

## Person A – Emma

Emma speaks confidently and enjoys being the centre of attention.

She motivates people with enthusiasm, but sometimes talks more than she listens.

---

## Person B – David

David is quiet and rarely speaks first.

However, people trust him because he listens carefully and always keeps his promises.

---

## Person C – Olivia

Olivia isn't the loudest person in the room, but she makes everyone feel comfortable and included.

People naturally enjoy working with her.

---

## Discussion

1. Which person seems the most charismatic?
2. Which person would you choose as a leader?
3. Can quiet people be charismatic?
4. Is charisma something people are born with?

# 2. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- command attention – naturally attract people's focus
- put people at ease – help others feel relaxed
- come across as – appear to be in a particular way
- lead by example – influence others through your own behaviour
- genuine interest – sincere curiosity about other people
- natural presence – ability to attract attention without trying too hard
- emotional intelligence – ability to understand and manage emotions
- active listening – carefully listening while showing engagement
- build rapport – develop a positive relationship quickly
- quiet confidence – calm self-assurance without arrogance

# 6. Reading

Read.

Many people believe charisma is simply a natural gift. However, research suggests that charisma is often based on behaviours rather than personality.

People who are seen as charismatic tend to listen carefully, show genuine interest in others and communicate clearly. They also project confidence without making others feel unimportant.

Interestingly, some of the world's most respected leaders are not particularly outgoing. Their influence comes from consistency, empathy and the ability to earn trust over time.

This suggests that charisma is not about being the loudest person in the room, but about making other people feel valued.
