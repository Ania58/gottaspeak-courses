# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- judge a book by its cover – form an opinion based only on appearance
- make a good first impression – create a positive initial opinion
- give someone the benefit of the doubt – choose to trust someone despite uncertainty
- jump to conclusions – reach a decision too quickly
- stereotype someone – make assumptions about a person based on a group they belong to
- preconceived idea – opinion formed before knowing the facts
- read too much into something – attach more meaning to something than it deserves
- earn someone's trust – gradually become trusted
- put someone at ease – make someone feel comfortable
- leave a lasting impression – be remembered for a long time

# 7. Reading

Read.

Psychologists have found that people often form first impressions within seconds of meeting someone. These early judgements are influenced by appearance, body language, facial expressions and tone of voice.

Although first impressions can sometimes be surprisingly accurate, they are also shaped by stereotypes and unconscious biases. As a result, people may overlook important information or misjudge someone's personality.

Research suggests that while first impressions are difficult to ignore, they should be treated as a starting point rather than a final judgement.

Getting to know someone over time usually provides a much more reliable picture of who they really are.
