# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- compare yourself to others – judge yourself against other people
- measure your worth by something – decide your value based on something
- social comparison – judging yourself by comparing yourself with others
- highlight reel – carefully selected positive moments rather than reality
- unrealistic expectations – standards that are almost impossible to achieve
- self-worth – opinion you have about your own value
- seek validation – look for approval from other people
- feel inadequate – believe you are not good enough
- keep up appearances – try to make everything look perfect
- lose confidence – become less sure of yourself

# 7. Reading

Read.

Comparing ourselves to others is a natural human behaviour. It helps us understand where we stand and can sometimes motivate us to improve.

However, social media has changed the way people compare themselves. Instead of seeing ordinary daily life, we are often exposed to carefully edited highlights that present an unrealistic picture of success, beauty and happiness.

As a result, many people begin measuring their own lives against unrealistic standards.

Psychologists suggest that lasting confidence develops not by comparing ourselves with others, but by recognising our own progress and personal values.
