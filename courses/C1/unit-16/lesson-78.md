# Lesson 78 – Would You Quit Your Job If You Won the Lottery?

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- discuss work and financial freedom naturally
- reflect on ambition, purpose and lifestyle
- express opinions about money and motivation
- analyse how people spend their time when work becomes optional

---

# 1. Thought Experiment

Imagine you win €50 million.

The money is guaranteed.

You never need to work again.

---

## Initial Reaction

Discuss.

1. Would you quit your job immediately?
2. What would you do first?
3. Would your life change dramatically?
4. Would you tell anyone?

---

# 2. Four Lottery Winners

## Person A – Sarah

Sarah quit her job immediately.

She travelled around the world and enjoyed life for several years.

Eventually, she became bored and started volunteering.

---

## Person B – Michael

Michael continued working.

He reduced his hours and says work gives him structure and purpose.

---

## Person C – Olivia

Olivia started her own business because she finally had enough money to pursue her dream.

---

## Person D – James

James retired at thirty-five.

After a few years, he became lonely and struggled to find meaning in life.

---

## Discussion

1. Which person made the best decision?
2. Could too much free time become a problem?
3. Why do people continue working even when they don't need money?
4. What gives life meaning?

---

# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- financial freedom – ability to live without depending on a salary
- pursue a dream – try to achieve something important to you
- lose your sense of purpose – no longer feel that life has direction
- work-life balance – healthy balance between work and personal life
- early retirement – stopping work much earlier than usual
- golden handcuffs – situation where a high salary makes it difficult to leave a job
- burnout – state of physical and emotional exhaustion caused by stress
- passion project – activity done because of personal interest rather than money
- feel unfulfilled – feel that something important is missing
- sense of achievement – satisfaction from accomplishing something

---

# 7. Reading

Read.

Many people dream of becoming rich enough to stop working. However, research suggests that work provides more than income.

Careers often give people a sense of identity, purpose and social connection. For this reason, some lottery winners continue working even after gaining financial independence.

Others decide to pursue long-forgotten dreams, volunteer or start businesses.

Psychologists suggest that humans need meaning and goals just as much as they need comfort and security.

Without challenges and responsibilities, life can become surprisingly empty.

---

Answer.
