# Lesson 80 – Rich, Successful or Content?

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- discuss different definitions of success naturally
- reflect on personal values and priorities
- compare wealth, achievement and contentment
- express nuanced opinions about ambition and happiness

---

# 1. What Does Success Mean?

Three people define success differently.

## Olivia

"I don't need millions. I just want enough money, good health and people I love."

---

## Daniel

"I want to build something important and leave a mark on the world."

---

## Emma

"I simply want peace of mind. I don't need to impress anyone."

---

## Discussion

1. Which person do you relate to most?
2. How has your definition of success changed over time?
3. Is contentment the same as happiness?
4. Can ambition and contentment coexist?

---

# 2. Three Paths

Imagine you can choose only one.

## Option A – Rich

You become extremely wealthy.

People admire your lifestyle.

However, your work is stressful and leaves little time for relationships.

---

## Option B – Successful

You achieve recognition and influence.

People respect your accomplishments.

But you are constantly busy.

---

## Option C – Content

You live comfortably and peacefully.

You have enough money, strong relationships and time for yourself.

Very few people know your name.

---

## Discussion

1. Which option would you choose?
2. Would your answer have been different ten years ago?
3. Why do some people chase recognition?
4. Can contentment be mistaken for lack of ambition?

# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- peace of mind – state of being free from worry
- leave a mark – have a lasting influence
- seek validation – want approval from others
- sense of accomplishment – satisfaction from achieving something
- define success on your own terms – decide for yourself what success means
- climb the social ladder – improve your social or professional position
- compare yourself to others – judge yourself based on other people's lives
- feel fulfilled – feel deeply satisfied with life
- external validation – approval from other people
- intrinsic motivation – doing something because it is personally meaningful

# 7. Reading

Read.

People often grow up believing that success means wealth, recognition and achievement.

However, different individuals define success in different ways. Some seek status and influence, while others prioritise peace, relationships and meaningful experiences.

Psychologists distinguish between external rewards and intrinsic motivation. Although praise and recognition can be satisfying, long-term wellbeing often depends on living according to personal values rather than other people's expectations.

Perhaps success is not something society gives us, but something each person defines for themselves.
