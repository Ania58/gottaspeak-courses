# Revision – Unit 16

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:

- discuss money and happiness naturally
- reflect on consumerism and material possessions
- analyse spending habits and priorities
- express opinions about wealth, success and contentment

# 4. Language From the Listening

Work with these expressions.

- clutter – large amounts of things in an untidy way
- stockpile something – accumulate large quantities of something
- dual-income household – family where both parents work
- convenience food – food that is quick and easy to prepare
- child-centred – focused mainly on children
- restore your batteries – regain energy and strength
- refuge – place that provides comfort and protection
- household goods – everyday items used at home
- material culture – physical objects people own
- accumulation of possessions – process of gathering more and more things
