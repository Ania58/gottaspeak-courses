# Lesson 86 – Is Technology Making Us Less Patient?

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- discuss how technology influences patience and attention
- analyse modern habits and expectations
- compare life before and after smartphones
- express opinions about instant gratification

---

# 1. The Social Experiment

Imagine two groups of people.

## Group A

For one week they:

- use smartphones normally
- check notifications whenever they appear
- watch short videos
- shop online
- use GPS everywhere

---

## Group B

For one week they:

- switch off all notifications
- avoid social media
- wait before replying to messages
- read printed books
- avoid using GPS whenever possible

---

## Discussion

1. Which group would find the week more difficult?
2. Which group would probably feel less stressed?
3. Would either group become more productive?
4. Could your own habits change after a week?

# 2. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- instant gratification – immediate satisfaction without waiting
- attention span – length of time someone can concentrate
- constant stimulation – continuous exposure to information or entertainment
- digital distraction – interruption caused by technology
- delayed reward – benefit received after waiting
- information overload – having more information than you can process
- dopamine hit – brief feeling of pleasure caused by a rewarding activity
- notification fatigue – feeling overwhelmed by constant alerts
- multitask – perform several activities at the same time
- mindful use of technology – using technology consciously and intentionally

# 6. Reading

Read.

Modern technology has dramatically reduced the amount of time people spend waiting. Messages arrive instantly, films begin with a single click and almost any product can be ordered within minutes.

While these innovations make everyday life more convenient, psychologists suggest they may also reduce our tolerance for delays. When people become accustomed to immediate results, even small inconveniences can feel surprisingly frustrating.

Researchers also argue that constant digital stimulation makes sustained attention more difficult. Frequent notifications and endless streams of new content encourage people to switch their focus repeatedly instead of concentrating on one task.

For this reason, some experts recommend creating technology-free moments during the day to improve concentration and wellbeing.
