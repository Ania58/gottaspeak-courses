# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- digital footprint – the trail of data created through online activity
- data harvesting – large-scale collection of personal information
- behavioural profiling – analysing someone's behaviour to predict future actions
- informed consent – permission given after fully understanding how information will be used
- algorithmic bias – unfair outcomes caused by the way algorithms are designed or trained
- pervasive surveillance – continuous monitoring that extends into many areas of daily life
- personal autonomy – ability to make independent decisions free from external control
- data minimisation – principle of collecting only the information that is genuinely necessary
- predictive analytics – use of data to forecast future behaviour or decisions
- erosion of privacy – gradual loss of personal privacy over time

# 7. Reading

Read.

Advances in artificial intelligence and big data have transformed the way organisations collect and process information. Every online search, purchase and interaction contributes to a growing digital footprint that can reveal surprisingly detailed patterns of behaviour.

Supporters argue that this information allows companies to improve services, personalise recommendations and strengthen security. Critics, however, warn that large-scale data collection threatens personal autonomy and creates opportunities for manipulation.

Another concern is algorithmic bias. Even sophisticated systems can unintentionally reinforce discrimination if they are trained on incomplete or unbalanced data.

As technology becomes increasingly integrated into everyday life, societies must decide how to balance innovation with fundamental rights such as privacy and individual freedom.
