## 4. Grammar – Expressing Nuanced Opinions

Real conversations often involve complexity rather than absolute opinions.

Compare:

Too absolute:
- People should always be themselves.

More nuanced:
- In principle, I think authenticity matters, although social situations can be more complicated than that.

Too simple:
- Fitting in is bad.

More balanced:
- Fitting in is not necessarily negative, provided people do not compromise their core values.

---

### Useful Language

Partial agreement:

- To some extent,…  
- I can see both sides…  
- In principle,…  
- That depends on…  
- There is some truth in that, although…  
- I wouldn't go that far, but…  
- Broadly speaking,…

## 7. Reading

Read the text.

Human beings are naturally social, and the desire to belong influences behaviour from an early age. People often adapt their appearance, language or behaviour to feel accepted within a particular group.

However, belonging can sometimes create tension between social acceptance and personal authenticity. Individuals may feel pressure to fit expectations that conflict with their own values or identity.

Interestingly, fitting in is not always negative. Social adaptation helps people navigate relationships and communities successfully. Problems tend to arise when acceptance becomes dependent on hiding or suppressing important aspects of oneself.

As a result, many people spend years negotiating the balance between social belonging and self-expression.
