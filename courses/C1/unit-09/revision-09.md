# Revision – Unit 9

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- describe awkward and embarrassing situations naturally  
- discuss social behaviour and interpersonal communication  
- use advanced spoken English in storytelling and opinion sharing  
- reflect on social confidence and human imperfection  

---

## 1. Vocabulary Activation

Complete naturally:

1. A social __________ can stay in your memory for years  
2. Some awkward moments create long-lasting __________  
3. Embarrassment often leads to emotional __________  
4. Diplomatic communication may help reduce interpersonal __________  
5. Many people fear leaving a bad __________  

---

## 2. Expression Upgrade

Rewrite using more advanced language:

1. I was embarrassed  
2. People looked at me  
3. I felt uncomfortable  
4. I made a mistake  
5. I wanted to leave quickly  

---

## 3. Storytelling Language

Complete naturally:

1. Little did I know…  
2. To make matters worse…  
3. Before I knew it…  
4. Looking back,…  
5. I honestly wished…  

---

## 4. Idioms & Natural Expressions

Use as many as possible:

- put your foot in it  
- laugh something off  
- wish the ground would swallow you up  
- save face  
- leave a bad impression  
- hit it off  
- feel out of place  
- brush something off  

---

### Task

Write a short paragraph (6–7 sentences) about an awkward social moment using **at least four expressions**.

## 11. Speaking

Discuss:

1. Why are embarrassing moments so memorable?  
2. Should people laugh at themselves more?  
3. Is embarrassment socially useful?  
4. Do people judge us as much as we think they do?  

Use:
- storytelling language  
- idioms  
- nuanced opinions
