## 4. Grammar – Telling Stories Vividly and Naturally

Strong storytelling sounds emotional and detailed.

Compare:

Basic:

- I was embarrassed.

More natural:

- I was absolutely mortified and honestly didn't know where to look.

Basic:

- It was awkward.

More vivid:

- The whole room went silent and I could practically feel the awkwardness hanging in the air.

## 7. Reading

Read the text.

Embarrassment is often viewed as a negative emotion, yet psychologists argue that it serves an important social function. People experience embarrassment when they feel they have violated social expectations or damaged the image they present to others.

What makes embarrassment particularly memorable is its emotional intensity. Even minor social mistakes can replay repeatedly in the mind long after other people have forgotten them.

Interestingly, embarrassment may help maintain social harmony. Feeling uncomfortable after a social mistake encourages reflection, apology and behavioural adjustment.

However, excessive self-consciousness can become unhelpful. Some people replay awkward moments so intensely that they begin avoiding social situations altogether.
