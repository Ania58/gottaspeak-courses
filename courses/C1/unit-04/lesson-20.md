## 4. Grammar – Mixed Conditionals 

We connect past behaviour with present consequences:

- If people had developed stronger habits earlier, they would find it easier to control usage now  
- If she had set clear boundaries, she wouldn’t be struggling with overuse today  
- If users were more aware of their habits, they wouldn’t have become so dependent  

---

### Task 1 – Transformation

Rewrite:

1. He didn’t control his habits → now he is dependent  
2. She didn’t set limits → she struggles now  
3. They didn’t think → they developed bad habits  
4. People ignored the problem → it became worse  
5. He didn’t act earlier → now it is difficult

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

Regaining control over digital behaviour is not simply a matter of reducing screen time. It requires a deeper understanding of how habits are formed and maintained.

One of the key challenges is that digital environments are designed to encourage continuous engagement. As a result, individuals must actively create boundaries rather than rely on external limitations.

What is often overlooked is the importance of intentional behaviour. Without conscious effort, habits tend to reinforce themselves, making change increasingly difficult.

Ultimately, achieving balance is not about eliminating technology, but about using it in a controlled and purposeful way.
