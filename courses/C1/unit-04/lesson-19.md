- describe how digital environments affect attention and focus

## 4. Grammar – Multi-layer Cause-Effect 

We describe complex chains:

- Constant notifications interrupt attention, **which fragments focus, which in turn reduces cognitive depth, ultimately affecting long-term concentration**.  
- Frequent task-switching prevents sustained attention, **leading to superficial processing and reduced memory retention**.  

---

### Task 1 – Transformation

Rewrite using cause-effect chains:

1. Notifications appear → people check → they lose focus  
2. People switch tasks → they don’t concentrate → they make mistakes  
3. They use phones → attention decreases → thinking becomes shallow  
4. They get distracted → they don’t finish tasks → performance drops  
5. They multitask → they lose control → they feel overwhelmed

## 5. Grammar – Contrastive Framing 

We challenge assumptions:

- While multitasking may appear efficient, it often reduces overall performance  
- What seems like productivity is often constant distraction  
- The issue is not that people cannot focus, but that focus is constantly interrupted  
- Far from improving efficiency, digital multitasking weakens cognitive control

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

In the digital age, attention has become a limited resource. Constant exposure to notifications, messages and updates creates an environment in which sustained focus is increasingly difficult to maintain.

Rather than engaging deeply with a single task, individuals are encouraged to switch rapidly between multiple sources of stimulation. This results in fragmented attention and reduced cognitive depth.

What is particularly concerning is that this pattern may become normalised. As people become accustomed to constant interruption, their ability to concentrate for extended periods weakens.

In the long term, this shift may affect not only productivity, but also the way individuals process information and think.
