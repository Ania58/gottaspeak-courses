## 8. Reading

Read the text.

Social comparison is a natural part of human behaviour, yet in the digital age it has become constant and unavoidable. Platforms encourage users to present idealised versions of their lives, creating a distorted perception of reality.

This leads to the construction of identity based not on internal values, but on external approval. The more individuals compare themselves to others, the more likely they are to experience dissatisfaction and insecurity.

What is particularly concerning is the cumulative effect of this process. Over time, repeated comparison can reshape self-perception and weaken confidence.

In this environment, identity becomes something that is not discovered, but constructed in response to external influence.
