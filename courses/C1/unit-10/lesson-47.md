## 4. Grammar – Talking About Pressure

People often explain pressure indirectly or emotionally.

Compare:

Simple:
- Work stresses me.

More natural:
- Work has been taking a toll on me lately.

Simple:
- I have too much work.

More developed:
- I've had a lot on my plate recently and it's becoming difficult to switch off.

---

### Useful Language

## 5. Grammar – Talking About Limits & Consequences

Healthy English includes talking about boundaries.

Compare:

Simple:
- People work too much.

More nuanced:
- Constant overwork can eventually lead to emotional exhaustion and reduced motivation.

Simple:
- Rest is important.

More natural:
- Without proper recovery, productivity often becomes unsustainable.

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

Modern work culture often celebrates productivity, ambition and constant achievement. In many industries, being busy is associated with commitment and professional value.

However, this mindset can create unhealthy expectations. Employees may feel pressure to remain constantly available, work longer hours or prioritise performance over wellbeing.

Burnout differs from ordinary tiredness. It involves emotional exhaustion, reduced motivation and a growing sense of detachment from work.

Ironically, excessive pressure may eventually reduce productivity rather than improve it. Sustainable performance often depends not only on effort, but also on recovery and realistic boundaries.
