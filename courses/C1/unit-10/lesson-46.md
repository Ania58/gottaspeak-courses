## 4. Grammar – Expressing Priorities

People rarely value just one thing professionally.

Compare:

Simple:
- Salary is important.

More natural:
- Salary matters, but it probably wouldn't be my top priority.

Simple:
- I want a flexible job.

More developed:
- Flexibility matters to me more than status or prestige.

---

### Useful Language

Talking about priorities:

- What matters most to me is…  
- I tend to prioritise…  
- I'd be willing to sacrifice… for…  
- One thing I wouldn't compromise on is…  
- I'd rather… than…  
- At this stage of life,…

## 5. Grammar – Talking About Trade-Offs

Adult decisions often involve compromise.

Compare:

Basic:
- High salaries are good but stressful.

More natural:
- Higher salaries often come with greater responsibility and pressure.

Basic:
- Flexible jobs pay less.

More balanced:
- Flexibility can sometimes mean slower career progression or lower income.

---

### Useful Language

- comes at a cost  
- involves compromise  
- has its drawbacks  
- may be worth it depending on…  
- often goes hand in hand with…  
- can be rewarding but demanding

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

People often talk about finding a dream job, yet the reality of working life is usually more complicated. Professional decisions rarely depend on passion alone.

Some people prioritise financial security and stability, while others seek creativity, flexibility or personal fulfilment. What feels meaningful or rewarding differs significantly from one person to another.

Interestingly, dissatisfaction at work does not always result from salary or workload. Workplace culture, recognition and a sense of purpose may influence job satisfaction just as strongly.

As careers become less predictable and more flexible, many professionals are increasingly rethinking what success at work actually means.
