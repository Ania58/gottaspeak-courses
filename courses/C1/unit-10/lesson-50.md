# 3. Language for Debate & Nuanced Opinions

Work with these expressions.

- external validation – approval or recognition from others  
- social comparison – evaluating yourself against others  
- personal fulfilment – personal satisfaction and meaning  
- conventional success – socially accepted idea of achievement  
- status-driven mindset – focus on prestige or appearance  
- social expectations – pressure created by society or culture  
- self-worth – value you place on yourself  
- personal priorities – what matters most individually  
- achievement culture – environment focused on performance and success  
- fulfilment – deep satisfaction or purpose

# 4. Debate Language Toolkit

Good debate is flexible, not aggressive.

## Expressing Position

- Broadly speaking,…  
- My instinct is that…  
- I'm inclined to believe…  
- To some extent,…  
- Personally, I tend to think…  

## Challenging Ideas Politely

- I'm not completely convinced that…  
- That assumes that…  
- There may be another way of looking at this  
- I can see the logic, although…  
- That's true up to a point, but…  

## Modifying Your Opinion

- On second thought,…  
- I hadn't considered that  
- That makes me rethink…  
- My view is shifting slightly because…

# 7. Reading

Read.

Ideas about success change across cultures and generations. For some people, success means financial independence and professional achievement. For others, it means freedom, family life or emotional wellbeing.

The difficulty is that success often becomes socially visible. Titles, salaries and achievements are easy to compare, while fulfilment or peace of mind are far harder to measure.

As a result, people sometimes pursue goals they believe they should want rather than goals that genuinely matter to them.

This does not mean ambition is harmful. Rather, the challenge lies in recognising whether ambition reflects personal values or outside expectations.
