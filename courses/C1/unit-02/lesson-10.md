## 4. Grammar – Inversion for Emphasis 

We use inversion to add sophistication and emphasis:

- Rarely do people question what strength really means.  
- Not only do humans rely on endurance, but they also depend on efficiency.  
- Never has physical strength been the only measure of ability.  
- Only later do we realise how limited that definition is.  

---

### Task 1 – Transformation

Rewrite using inversion:

1. People rarely question strength  
2. Humans are not only strong but also efficient  
3. Physical strength is not the only factor  
4. We realise this later  
5. People do not fully understand ability

## 5. Grammar – Evaluative Stance 

We express evaluation and opinion with nuance:

- It is often assumed that strength equals power, yet this view is limited.  
- What is particularly interesting is the way humans compensate for physical weakness.  
- It is worth considering whether endurance is a more meaningful measure.  
- This perspective raises important questions about ability.

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

The concept of strength is often narrowly defined in physical terms. Muscles, speed and power tend to dominate our understanding of what it means to be capable. However, this definition may be more limiting than it appears.

Humans, when compared to many animals, lack obvious physical advantages. Yet they possess a unique combination of endurance, adaptability and cognitive ability.

What is particularly striking is how these qualities interact. Rather than relying on raw strength, humans depend on efficiency, consistency and problem-solving.

In this sense, strength is not a fixed concept but a dynamic one. It evolves depending on context, perspective and need.
