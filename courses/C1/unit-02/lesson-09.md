## 4. Grammar – Advanced Aspect 

We distinguish between:
- habits (repeated actions)
- states (long-term conditions)

Compare:

- People walk every day → simple habit  
- People have been walking regularly → ongoing process  
- People tend to walk → general tendency  
- People are constantly checking their phones → emphasis + criticism  

---

## 5. Grammar – Subtle Emphasis 

We avoid overusing basic cleft sentences.

Instead of:
❌ It is walking that improves thinking  

We use:

- What really matters is consistency  
- The real issue lies in repetition  
- What people underestimate is the cumulative effect

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

The power of habits lies not in their intensity, but in their consistency. Small actions, repeated over time, tend to produce results that are far greater than their immediate impact would suggest.

One of the most overlooked aspects of human behaviour is the cumulative effect of routine. What seems insignificant in the short term can, over weeks and months, lead to substantial change.

This is particularly true of physical movement. Walking for a short period each day may appear trivial, yet it contributes to both physical and cognitive improvement.

What makes habits powerful is their ability to operate almost unconsciously. Once established, they require little effort, yet continue to shape behaviour in meaningful ways.
