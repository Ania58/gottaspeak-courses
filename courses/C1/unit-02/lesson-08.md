## 4. Grammar – Causative Structures 

We move beyond:
❌ “Walking helps you think”

We use:

- Walking enables people to think more clearly  
- Movement allows the brain to function more efficiently  
- Physical activity causes the brain to process information faster  
- Walking leads individuals to generate new ideas

## 5. Grammar – Hedging in Scientific Language

At C1, we avoid absolute statements:

- Walking appears to enhance cognitive performance  
- It is widely believed that movement improves mental clarity  
- There seems to be a connection between activity and focus  
- It could be argued that physical movement supports creativity  

---

### Task

Rewrite using hedging:

1. Walking improves thinking  
2. Movement increases creativity  
3. Exercise makes people happier  
4. The brain works better when moving  
5. Activity improves focus

## 6. Grammar – Reduced Clauses (C1+ Depth)

Instead of:
→ When people are walking, they think better  

We say:
→ When walking, people tend to think more clearly  

Or:
→ Walking regularly, individuals improve their cognitive performance  

---

## 9. Reading

Read the text.

The relationship between movement and cognition has gained increasing attention in recent years. What was once considered a simple physical activity is now understood to play a crucial role in mental performance.

Walking, in particular, appears to stimulate brain activity in ways that enhance creativity and problem-solving. Rather than remaining static, the brain seems to benefit from continuous, low-intensity movement.

One explanation lies in the regulation of blood flow and oxygen supply, which allows the brain to function more efficiently. Another factor may be the psychological effect of movement, which helps individuals clear their minds and focus more effectively.

In this sense, movement is not merely physical — it is cognitive.
