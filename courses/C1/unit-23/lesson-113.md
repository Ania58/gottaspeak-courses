# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- non-verbal communication – communication through gestures, facial expressions, posture and other physical behaviour
- facial microexpression – extremely brief facial expression that may reveal genuine emotion
- behavioural cue – observable action that may provide information about someone's thoughts or feelings
- subconscious signalling – communication occurring without deliberate intention
- interpersonal perception – the way people interpret and understand others
- gesture interpretation – process of assigning meaning to body movements
- emotional leakage – unintentional expression of genuine emotions despite attempts to hide them
- cultural convention – socially accepted behaviour that varies between cultures
- deceptive appearance – behaviour that creates a false impression
- non-verbal inconsistency – mismatch between spoken words and body language

# 7. Reading

Read.

Body language plays a significant role in communication, yet its meaning is often more complex than popular psychology suggests. Although facial expressions, posture and gestures provide valuable information, researchers increasingly warn against interpreting individual behaviours in isolation.

Many behaviours commonly associated with deception or insecurity have perfectly innocent explanations. Avoiding eye contact, crossing one's arms or speaking quietly may simply reflect personality, cultural background or temporary stress rather than dishonesty or lack of confidence.

Experts therefore recommend analysing non-verbal communication within its broader context. Rather than searching for universal signs of lying or confidence, effective communicators consider verbal messages, cultural norms, relationships and the surrounding situation before reaching conclusions.
