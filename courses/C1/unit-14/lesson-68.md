# Lesson 68 – Can People Really Change?

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- discuss personal growth and change naturally
- talk about mistakes, forgiveness and second chances
- express nuanced opinions about reputation and trust
- reflect on how experiences shape people

---

# 1. Story Starter

Read the story.

At university, Marcus was known for all the wrong reasons.

He skipped classes, partied constantly and often borrowed money from friends without paying it back. People described him as irresponsible and unreliable.

Eventually, most of his friends stopped trusting him.

Years later, after losing an important job opportunity because of his behaviour, Marcus decided to change.

He returned to university, found stable work and slowly rebuilt his life. He stopped making excuses and began taking responsibility for his mistakes.

Ten years later, he had become a completely different person.

However, some people who knew him in the past still saw him as the irresponsible young man he used to be.

Marcus understood why they felt that way, but he also wondered:

### "If people never let you move on from your past, can you really change?"

# 7. Reading

Read.

People often speak about change as though it happens suddenly. In reality, personal growth is usually gradual and involves setbacks, failures and repeated effort.

Psychologists suggest that major life events, difficult experiences and increased self-awareness can motivate people to change their behaviour.

However, rebuilding trust is often more difficult than changing oneself. While individuals may genuinely become different people, others may continue to judge them based on past experiences.

For this reason, change requires not only personal effort but also patience and consistency.

Ultimately, actions over time matter more than promises.
