# Lesson 69 – Revenge or Forgiveness?

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- discuss forgiveness and revenge naturally
- talk about betrayal, trust and apologies
- express complex emotions and opinions
- analyse different ways people respond to being hurt

---

# 1. The Investigation

Three people were hurt in different ways.

Your task:

- Decide whether they should forgive or not.
- Consider the emotional consequences of both choices.
- Explain your reasoning.

---

## Case A – Sarah

Sarah's best friend revealed a very personal secret that she had promised to keep confidential.

The friendship ended immediately.

Two years later, her former friend contacted her and apologised sincerely, admitting she had been immature and selfish.

Sarah still feels hurt and isn't sure whether she should forgive her.

---

## Case B – Ethan

Ethan was passed over for a promotion because a colleague spread false rumours about him.

Eventually, the truth came out and the colleague lost their own job.

Ethan now has the opportunity to recommend this person for another position.

Nobody would blame him for refusing.

---

## Case C – Mia

Mia's partner cheated on her.

After months apart, her partner admitted everything, apologised and promised to change.

Many people around Mia tell her that once trust is broken, it can never be repaired.

---

# 7. Reading

Read.

People often assume that forgiveness means excusing bad behaviour, but psychologists argue that this is not necessarily true.

Forgiveness does not require forgetting what happened or pretending that nothing changed. Instead, it often means letting go of resentment and refusing to allow anger to control your life.

Research suggests that people who forgive tend to experience lower levels of stress and greater emotional wellbeing.

However, forgiveness does not always lead to reconciliation. In some situations, rebuilding trust may be impossible, even when forgiveness is genuine.

Ultimately, forgiveness is less about the past and more about deciding how to move forward.
