# Lesson 66 – Is It Ever OK to Lie?

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- discuss honesty and deception naturally
- express and defend opinions about moral dilemmas
- distinguish between different types of lies
- talk about trust, intentions and consequences

---

# 1. Debate Starter

Read the statement.

## "Honesty is always the best policy."

---

## Initial Reaction

Choose your position.

- Strongly agree
- Mostly agree
- Unsure
- Mostly disagree
- Strongly disagree

Explain your answer.

---

# 2. Three Situations

Read the examples.

## Situation A

Your friend proudly shows you a new haircut.

You think it looks terrible.

They ask:

### "Be honest. What do you think?"

Would you tell the truth?

---

## Situation B

Your partner asks whether you remembered an important date.

You completely forgot.

You could blame work or traffic and avoid an argument.

Would you lie?

---

## Situation C

A child asks difficult questions about death.

You know they are too young to fully understand.

Would you simplify the truth or tell them everything?

---

## Discussion

1. Which lie seems most acceptable?
2. Are intentions more important than facts?
3. Can lies protect people?
4. Is brutal honesty always helpful?

# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- white lie – harmless lie intended to avoid hurting someone
- honesty – telling the truth
- deception – intentionally misleading someone
- trust – confidence in another person
- intention – purpose behind an action
- consequence – result of an action
- half-truth – statement that is partly true but misleading
- betrayal – breaking someone's trust
- transparency – openness and honesty
- moral dilemma – difficult choice between right and wrong

# 7. Reading

Read.

Most people claim to value honesty, yet almost everyone tells lies from time to time.

Many lies are relatively harmless and intended to protect feelings or avoid conflict. These so-called white lies are often accepted by society.

However, repeated dishonesty can damage trust and relationships. Once trust is broken, rebuilding it may take years.

Some psychologists argue that complete honesty is unrealistic and that social relationships depend partly on tact and sensitivity. Others believe that honesty should never be sacrificed for convenience.

Perhaps the challenge is not deciding whether to tell the truth, but learning how to tell it with empathy.
