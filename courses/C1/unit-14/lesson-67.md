# Lesson 67 – Would You Break the Law to Help Someone?

## 🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- discuss moral dilemmas and legal issues naturally
- distinguish between legality and morality
- justify decisions and evaluate consequences
- express nuanced opinions about responsibility and justice

---

# 1. Case Studies

Read the situations.

Your task:

- Decide what you would do.
- Explain your reasoning.
- Consider both moral and legal consequences.

---

## Case A – Speeding to the Hospital

Your friend is seriously injured.

The ambulance is delayed, and the nearest hospital is twenty minutes away.

You decide to drive at twice the speed limit to get there.

You are caught by a speed camera.

---

Questions

1. Did you do the right thing?
2. Should you be punished?
3. Does the reason justify breaking the law?

---

## Case B – The Desperate Father

A father cannot afford expensive medicine that his daughter urgently needs.

Without the medicine, she might die.

He steals it from a pharmacy.

---

Questions

1. Is stealing always wrong?
2. Should the father go to prison?
3. Would you have done the same?

---

## Case C – The Refugee

A family fleeing war enters another country illegally.

Without doing so, they would have remained in danger.

---

Questions

1. Is breaking immigration laws acceptable in this situation?
2. Should governments make exceptions?
3. What would you do?

---

# 2. First Conclusions

Discuss.

# 3. Language From the Topic

Work with these expressions.

- moral dilemma – difficult choice involving right and wrong
- legality – whether something follows the law
- morality – principles of right and wrong
- consequence – result of an action
- justice – fair treatment under the law
- responsibility – obligation to act correctly
- compassion – sympathy and concern for others
- punishment – penalty for wrongdoing
- exception – special situation that does not follow the usual rule
- circumstance – condition affecting a decision

# 7. Reading

Read.

People often assume that legality and morality are the same thing. However, history provides many examples of laws that were later considered unjust.

At the same time, allowing individuals to ignore laws whenever they believe they are morally right could lead to chaos and unpredictability.

For this reason, societies constantly struggle to balance justice, compassion and responsibility.

Some argue that exceptional circumstances justify breaking certain rules. Others believe that once exceptions become common, trust in the legal system begins to disappear.

The relationship between morality and legality therefore remains one of the most difficult questions in ethics.
