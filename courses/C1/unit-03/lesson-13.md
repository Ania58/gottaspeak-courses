## 4. Grammar – Layered Speculation 

We combine multiple levels of uncertainty:

- It might have been generated using AI  
- It could easily be mistaken for a real voice  
- It must have seemed completely authentic  
- It may not have been verified properly  
- It can’t have come from a real person  

---

2. It was probably created by AI  
3. I’m sure it wasn’t real

## 5. Grammar – Epistemic Stance (C1+ Depth)

We express how confident we are in knowledge:

- There is strong evidence to suggest that…  
- It is difficult to determine whether…  
- It appears increasingly likely that…  
- There is little reason to believe that…  
- It remains unclear whether…

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

As artificial intelligence becomes more advanced, the boundary between reality and fabrication is becoming increasingly difficult to define. Technologies such as voice cloning and deepfakes have made it possible to recreate a person’s identity with remarkable accuracy.

What makes this development particularly concerning is not just the technology itself, but its accessibility. Tools that were once limited to experts are now widely available, allowing almost anyone to create convincing but entirely false content.

The result is a shift in how we interpret information. Where people once relied on their senses, they must now question them. Seeing and hearing are no longer reliable indicators of truth.

In this environment, trust becomes fragile, and certainty becomes rare.
