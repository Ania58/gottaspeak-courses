## 4. Grammar – Modality (Certainty vs Doubt)

We express different levels of certainty:

- It must be real. (strong belief)  
- It might be real. (possibility)  
- It can’t be real. (certainty something is false)  

C1+ nuance:

- It could easily be mistaken for something genuine  
- This might not be entirely reliable  
- It can’t have been verified properly  
- It must have seemed convincing at the time  

---

## 5. Grammar – Distancing & Reporting Language

We avoid direct claims:

- It is claimed that the voice was generated by AI  
- There is reason to believe the message was manipulated  
- It appears that the caller was impersonating someone  
- This suggests that trust can be easily exploited

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

Trust has always played a central role in communication, yet in the digital age it has become increasingly fragile. Information can be created, modified and distributed at an unprecedented speed, making it difficult to distinguish between what is real and what is fabricated.

What is particularly concerning is how easily perception can be manipulated. A message does not need to be true in order to be convincing. If it appears credible, many people will accept it without verification.

Voices, once considered reliable indicators of identity, are no longer immune to deception. With the rise of advanced technology, even something as personal as a human voice can be replicated.

This raises an important question: if we can no longer trust what we hear, what can we trust?
