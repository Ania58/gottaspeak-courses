## 4. Grammar – Modality for Intent & Inference 

We express what someone **intended** or **may have intended**:

- He must have been trying to pressure her  
- They might have intended to mislead the audience  
- This could have been designed to trigger fear  
- He can’t have realised how obvious it was  
- They may not have expected such a reaction  

---

## 5. Grammar – Rhetorical Framing & Implicit Meaning

Manipulation often works through **how something is presented**, not what is said directly.

Examples:

- “This is your last chance” → creates urgency  
- “Everyone is doing it” → social pressure  
- “It would be a shame if…” → indirect threat

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

Manipulation rarely appears in an obvious form. In most cases, it is subtle, gradual and carefully structured. Rather than forcing decisions, it guides them, often without the person realising it.

One of the most effective strategies is emotional manipulation. By triggering fear, urgency or trust, individuals can be influenced in ways that bypass rational thinking.

Another common technique is the selective presentation of information. By focusing only on certain facts while ignoring others, a message can appear convincing even when it is incomplete or misleading.

What makes manipulation particularly dangerous is its invisibility. People tend to believe they are making independent decisions, even when their thinking has been shaped by external influence.
