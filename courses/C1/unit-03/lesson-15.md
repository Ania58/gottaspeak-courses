## 4. Grammar – Mixed Conditionals 

We connect past and present consequences:

- If I had checked the number, I wouldn’t be worried now  
- If they had verified the identity, they wouldn’t have lost money  
- If people were more cautious, they wouldn’t have been deceived  

---

### Task 1 – Transformation

Rewrite:

1. I didn’t check → now I have a problem  
2. He trusted them → he lost money  
3. They didn’t think → they were tricked  
4. She didn’t verify → she regrets it  
5. People act quickly → they get scammed

## 5. Grammar – Mitigation & Softening (C1+)

We avoid sounding absolute:

- It might be safer to verify the source  
- It would probably be better to wait before acting  
- One way to reduce risk is to double-check information  
- It is generally advisable to remain cautious

## 8. Reading

Read the text.

In a world where deception is becoming increasingly sophisticated, the ability to think critically is more important than ever. While technology continues to evolve, human judgment remains the most effective defence against manipulation.

One of the key strategies is verification. Rather than accepting information at face value, individuals must learn to question sources, check details and confirm identity.

Equally important is emotional awareness. Many scams succeed because they trigger immediate reactions, leaving little time for reflection. Recognising these emotional triggers can prevent impulsive decisions.

Ultimately, protection does not depend on avoiding risk entirely, but on managing it intelligently.
